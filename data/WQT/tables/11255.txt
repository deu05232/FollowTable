# Understanding the pH Scale  What pH Means for Acidity and Alkalinity

| pH Range | Classification | Typical Chemistry Notes | Example Liquids (Non-branded) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0–1 | Extremely acidic | High H+ activity; strong acids | Battery acid |
| 2–3 | Very acidic | Often associated with citric/carbonic acids | Lemon juice |
| 4–5 | Moderately acidic | Weak acids dominate | Black coffee |
| 6–6.9 | Slightly acidic | Close to neutral; buffering matters | Milk |
| 7.0 | Neutral | Equal H+ and OH− in pure water | Distilled water |
| 7.1–8 | Slightly alkaline | Mild base; carbonate/bicarbonate buffering | Sea water |
| 8.1–9 | Moderately alkaline | Higher carbonate content; increased OH− | Baking soda solution |
| 9.1–10 | Alkaline | Often requires additives/ionization | Soap solution |
| 10.1–14 | Strongly alkaline | Strong bases; caustic | Bleach |
| Measurement Tips | — | Calibrate with pH 4/7/10 buffers | Use fresh buffers |
| Common Errors | — | Temperature effects; dirty probes | Rinse with DI water |
