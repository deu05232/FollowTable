# Typical Blood Pressure Ranges by Age (Not a Diagnostic Classification)

| Age range | Typical systolic (mm Hg) | Typical diastolic (mm Hg) | Comments |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Newborn (0–1 mo) | 60–90 | 30–60 | Wide physiologic variability |
| Infant (1–12 mo) | 75–100 | 45–65 | Depends on activity/crying |
| Toddler (1–3 yr) | 85–105 | 50–70 | Growth-related changes |
| Preschool (3–5 yr) | 90–110 | 55–75 | Use pediatric charts for diagnosis |
| School age (6–12 yr) | 95–120 | 60–80 | “Typical” is not a cutoff |
| Teen (13–17 yr) | 105–135 | 65–85 | Athletic teens may run lower |
| Adult (18–39 yr) | 110–130 | 70–85 | Normal shifts by population |
| Adult (40–59 yr) | 115–135 | 75–90 | Averages, not categories |
| Older adult (60+ yr) | 120–145 | 70–90 | Pulse pressure often higher |
| Note | — | — | These are reference ranges, not classification thresholds |
| Suggested use | — | — | For context only; not for labeling a reading |
