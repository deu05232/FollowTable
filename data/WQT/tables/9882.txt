# High-Zinc Food Groups (Category-Based Cheat Sheet)

| Food category | Examples (multiple foods per cell) | Typical zinc range (mg) | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Shellfish | Oysters, crab, lobster, mussels | 3–75 | Often highest zinc sources |
| Red meat | Beef chuck, ground beef, lamb, pork chops | 3–10 | Varies by cut and cooking method |
| Poultry | Chicken breast, turkey, duck | 0.5–4 | Usually lower than red meat |
| Dairy | Cheddar, mozzarella, yogurt, milk | 0.3–4 | Cheese tends to be higher |
| Seeds & nuts | Pumpkin seeds, sesame, cashews, almonds | 1–8 | Higher per ounce than many plants |
| Legumes & grains | Chickpeas, lentils, oats, fortified cereals | 1–6 | Fortification changes values |
| Vegetables | Mushrooms, spinach, peas | 0.1–1 | Lower-density zinc sources |
