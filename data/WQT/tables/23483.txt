# Florida Felony Degree Penalty Schedule (Maximum Terms and Fines)

| Felony degree | Standard maximum prison term | Standard maximum fine | Typical description |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Capital felony | Death or life imprisonment | N/A | Most serious felony category |
| Life felony | Life imprisonment | $15,000 | Next-highest category below capital |
| First-degree felony | 30 years | $10,000 | Highest numbered degree |
| Second-degree felony | 15 years | $10,000 | Mid-level felony degree |
| Third-degree felony | 5 years | $5,000 | Lowest felony degree |
| Unclassified felony | As specified by statute | As specified by statute | Some felonies have statute-specific maxima |
| Attempt (generic) | One degree lower than the completed offense | Corresponding lower-degree fine cap | Degree-reduction rule for attempts |
| Conspiracy/solicitation (generic) | Usually one degree lower than the completed offense | Corresponding lower-degree fine cap | General degree-reduction approach |
