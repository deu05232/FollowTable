# Dangerous Snakes of the World — Medically Important Species (Not State-Organized)

| Common name | Scientific name | Primary region | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Inland taipan | Oxyuranus microlepidotus | Australia | Highly venomous elapid |
| Eastern brown snake | Pseudonaja textilis | Australia & PNG | Major cause of envenomation |
| Black mamba | Dendroaspis polylepis | Sub-Saharan Africa | Fast neurotoxic elapid |
| Russell's viper | Daboia russelii | South Asia | Hemotoxic, common bites |
| Saw-scaled viper | Echis carinatus | Middle East & South Asia | Important in rural bites |
| King cobra | Ophiophagus hannah | South & Southeast Asia | Largest venomous snake |
| Boomslang | Dispholidus typus | Africa | Potent hemotoxin |
| Fer-de-lance | Bothrops asper | Central America | Major bite species |
