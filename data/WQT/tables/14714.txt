# Manhattan Project Milestones Relevant to Hiroshima (Non-day-specific)

| Year/Period | Milestone | Location | Output/Deliverable | Connection to Hiroshima |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1942 | Program formally organized under U.S. Army Corps of Engineers | U.S. | Centralized management | Enables rapid scale-up |
| 1943 | Major production sites ramp up | Oak Ridge / Hanford | Fissile material production | Provides uranium/plutonium pathways |
| 1944 | Weapon design matures | Los Alamos | Engineering prototypes | Leads toward deployable device |
| Early 1945 | Assembly and testing preparations | Los Alamos | Final components | Near-ready weapon system |
| Mid 1945 | Delivery integration planning | Pacific bases | B-29 modification program | Enables mission capability |
| July 1945 | Post-test confidence increases | New Mexico (test site) | Demonstrated feasibility | Clears path to use |
| 1945 | Targeting and operational planning | Pacific command | Mission plan | Directly precedes Hiroshima strike |
