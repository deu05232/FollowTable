# U.S.-Involved Battles and Operations in the Pacific Theater (World War II)

| Battle / Operation | Date(s) | Location | Type | Main U.S. forces involved (high-level) | Outcome / Short description |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Attack on Pearl Harbor | 7 Dec 1941 | Oahu, Hawaii (U.S. territory) | Air/naval strike | U.S. Pacific Fleet & U.S. Army in Hawaii | Japanese surprise attack; brought the U.S. formally into WWII |
| Battle of Midway | 4–7 Jun 1942 | Near Midway Atoll, Central Pacific | Naval/air battle | U.S. Navy carriers & aircraft | Major U.S. victory; turning point in the Pacific war |
| Guadalcanal Campaign (major actions incl. Henderson Field, naval battles) | 7 Aug 1942 – 9 Feb 1943 | Guadalcanal, Solomon Islands | Amphibious/land & naval | U.S. Marines, U.S. Navy | Allied victory; first major offensive against Japan |
| Battle of Tarawa (Betio) | 20–23 Nov 1943 | Gilbert Islands (Betio, Tarawa Atoll) | Amphibious assault | U.S. Marine Corps | U.S. victory with heavy casualties; demonstrated difficulty of amphibious landings |
| Battle of Saipan | 15 Jun – 9 Jul 1944 | Mariana Islands (Saipan) | Land campaign | U.S. Marines & U.S. Army | U.S. victory; enabled strategic bombing range to Japan |
| Battle of Leyte Gulf | 23–26 Oct 1944 | Waters around Leyte, Philippines | Naval battle | U.S. Third & Seventh Fleets | Decisive Allied victory; crippled Japanese naval power |
| Battle of Iwo Jima | 19 Feb – 26 Mar 1945 | Volcano Islands (Iwo Jima) | Amphibious/land battle | U.S. Marine Corps | U.S. victory; captured airfields, extremely high casualties |
| Battle of Okinawa | 1 Apr – 22 Jun 1945 | Ryukyu Islands (Okinawa) | Amphibious/land battle | U.S. Tenth Army & U.S. Navy | Allied victory; last major battle before Japan's surrender |
