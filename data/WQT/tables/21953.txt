# Major Temples and Cult Centers in Ancient Egypt (Deities and Locations)

| Site / Temple Complex | City/Region | Primary Deity Venerated | Period (Common) | Notable Feature | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Karnak Temple | Thebes (Luxor) | Amun | Middle Kingdom–Ptolemaic | Massive hypostyle hall | Major state cult center |
| Luxor Temple | Thebes (Luxor) | Amun | New Kingdom | Opet Festival route | Linked to Karnak by avenue |
| Temple of Horus | Edfu | Horus | Ptolemaic | Exceptionally preserved | Major pilgrimage site |
| Temple of Isis | Philae (Aswan) | Isis | Ptolemaic–Roman | Island sanctuary | Relocated due to Aswan dam |
| Temple of Hathor | Dendera | Hathor | Ptolemaic–Roman | Zodiac ceiling | Famous for reliefs |
| Mortuary Temple of Hatshepsut | Deir el-Bahri | Amun | New Kingdom | Terraced design | Includes chapels to multiple gods |
| Ramesseum | West Thebes | Amun | New Kingdom | Colossal statues | Royal mortuary temple |
| Temple of Ptah | Memphis | Ptah | Old Kingdom–Late Period | Artisan cult associations | Strong urban priesthood |
