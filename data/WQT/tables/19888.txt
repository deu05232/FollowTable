# GCSE Chemistry Revision  Acids, Alkalis and pH (Key Concepts)

| Topic | Definition | Key Formula/Rule | Common Example | Typical Practical |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Acid | Substance that donates H+ ions | pH < 7 | Hydrochloric acid | Titration with alkali |
| Alkali | Soluble base producing OH- ions | pH > 7 | Sodium hydroxide | Neutralisation |
| Neutralisation | Acid + base reaction | Acid + base → salt + water | HCl + NaOH | Temperature change test |
| Indicator | Dye showing pH range | Universal indicator scale | Litmus | Colour chart comparison |
| Strong vs weak acids | Degree of ionisation | Strong = fully ionised | HCl vs CH3COOH | Conductivity test |
| Salts | Ionic products of neutralisation | Metal/carbonate rules | NaCl, CuSO4 | Crystallisation |
| Carbonates | React with acids to make CO2 | Acid + carbonate → salt + water + CO2 | CaCO3 + HCl | Gas test with limewater |
