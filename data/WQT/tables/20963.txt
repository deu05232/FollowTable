# Vasopressors — Mechanism, Receptor Profile, and Clinical Use

| Agent (Common Name) | Primary Receptor Activity | Mechanism Summary | Typical Clinical Use | Monitoring Considerations |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Epinephrine | α1, β1, β2 | Increases SVR and cardiac output | Anaphylaxis, cardiac arrest | Arrhythmias, lactate |
| Norepinephrine | α1 >> β1 | Potent vasoconstriction | Septic shock first-line | Peripheral ischemia |
| Phenylephrine | α1 | Pure vasoconstrictor | Anesthesia-related hypotension | Reflex bradycardia |
| Dopamine | D1, β1, α1 (dose-dependent) | Inotropy/vasoconstriction by dose | Selected shock states | Tachyarrhythmias |
| Dobutamine | β1 >> β2 | Inotrope, mild vasodilation | Cardiogenic shock | Hypotension, tachycardia |
| Vasopressin | V1 (vascular) | Non-adrenergic vasoconstriction | Refractory septic shock adjunct | Hyponatremia, ischemia |
| Angiotensin II | AT1 | Vasoconstriction via RAAS | Refractory vasodilatory shock | Thrombosis risk |
| Metaraminol | α1, some β1 | Vasoconstriction + mild inotropy | Acute hypotension | Extravasation |
