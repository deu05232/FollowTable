# U.S. Military Retirement & Disability Pay Programs (How Payout Is Determined)

| Plan / Program | Eligibility trigger (typical) | Based on (payout determination) | Notes / caps (short) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Legacy Active-Duty Longevity Retirement (Final Pay) | 20+ years active duty; entered service before 9/8/1980 | **Final basic pay × multiplier** (2.5% × years of service) | Monthly retired pay; COLA applies |
| Legacy Active-Duty Longevity Retirement (High-36) | 20+ years active duty; entered service on/after 9/8/1980 | **Average of highest 36 months of basic pay × multiplier** (2.5% × years) | Most common “legacy” calculation |
| Career Status Bonus / REDUX (CSB/REDUX) | Opt-in around 15th year (legacy) | **High-36 average × reduced multiplier**, with different COLA method | Includes mid-career bonus tradeoff |
| Blended Retirement System (BRS) Pension | 20+ years active duty under BRS | **High-36 average × BRS multiplier** (2.0% × years) | Defined benefit portion of BRS |
| BRS Defined Contribution (TSP) | Under BRS; contributions during service | **Government/employee contributions + investment returns** | Separate from the pension; portable |
| Temporary Early Retirement Authority (TERA) | Early retirement window (often 15–19 years) | **High-36 or Final Pay method × years**, then **reduced by early-retirement factor** | Availability depends on authorized drawdowns |
| Disability Retirement (DoD) | Unfit for duty; service-connected disability rating | **Higher of** (a) **disability % × retired base pay** or (b) **longevity formula** (multiplier × retired base pay) | Disability % method typically capped at 75% |
| Disability Severance Pay (DoD) | Unfit; disability too low for retirement (varies by case) | **Years of service × basic pay multiplier** (lump sum) | One-time payment instead of retired pay |
| Concurrent Retirement and Disability Pay (CRDP) | Eligible longevity retiree + VA disability rating (typically 50%+) | **Restores retired pay previously offset by VA compensation** | Not a separate “retirement formula,” but changes net payout |
