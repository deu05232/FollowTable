# Autonomic Neurotransmitters and Major Target-Organ Responses

| Autonomic division | Primary transmitter(s) | Typical release site | Example target organ | Example response |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sympathetic (most organs) | Norepinephrine | Postganglionic varicosities | Heart | Increased rate and contractility |
| Sympathetic (adrenal medulla) | Acetylcholine → Epinephrine | Preganglionic to chromaffin cells | Lungs | Bronchodilation |
| Sympathetic (sweat glands) | Acetylcholine | Postganglionic to eccrine glands | Skin | Increased sweating |
| Parasympathetic | Acetylcholine | Postganglionic terminals | GI tract | Increased motility and secretion |
| Enteric (modulatory) | Nitric oxide, VIP | Myenteric plexus interneurons | Intestinal smooth muscle | Relaxation |
| Parasympathetic (eye) | Acetylcholine | Postganglionic ciliary ganglion | Iris sphincter | Pupil constriction |
| Sympathetic (vascular) | Norepinephrine | Postganglionic terminals | Arterioles | Vasoconstriction |
| Non-adrenergic/non-cholinergic | Nitric oxide | Postganglionic nitrergic fibers | Corpus cavernosum | Erection (smooth muscle relaxation) |
