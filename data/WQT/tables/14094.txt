# Extraocular Muscles  Innervation Plus Clinical Pupil Findings and Reflexes

| Extraocular muscle | Nerve supply | Common bedside finding when impaired | Pupil / reflex notes (non-muscle focus) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Medial rectus | CN III | Impaired adduction; diplopia on lateral gaze | May accompany parasympathetic involvement causing dilated pupil |
| Superior rectus | CN III | Weak elevation, especially in abduction | Pupil may be enlarged if CN III compressive lesion |
| Inferior rectus | CN III | Weak depression, especially in abduction | CN III palsy can reduce light reflex (efferent) |
| Inferior oblique | CN III | Weak elevation in adduction | Pupil asymmetry may be present |
| Superior oblique | CN IV | Vertical diplopia; head tilt compensation | Pupils typically normal (no parasympathetic fibers) |
| Lateral rectus | CN VI | Failure to abduct; esotropia | Pupils typically normal |
| Levator palpebrae superioris | CN III | Ptosis | Ptosis often coexists with mydriasis in CN III palsy |
| Orbicularis oculi | CN VII | Incomplete eyelid closure | Corneal exposure; blink reflex efferent limb |
| Sphincter pupillae (not extraocular) | CN III (parasympathetic) | Mydriasis when denervated | Light reflex efferent pathway emphasized |
