# New York Sentencing Overview  Felony Classes Plus Post-Release Supervision and Minimums

| Felony class | Standard maximum term (determinate/indeterminate) | Common minimum term rule | Post-release supervision (PRS) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Class A-I felony | Life | 15–25 years minimum (typical) | 2.5–5 years | Violent felony framework |
| Class A-II felony | Life | 3–8 years minimum (varies) | 1.5–3 years | Drug-law context often applies |
| Class B felony | 25 years | 1–8 years minimum (varies) | 1.5–3 years | Some are violent felonies |
| Class C felony | 15 years | 1–5 years minimum (varies) | 1.5–3 years | Split sentencing possible |
| Class D felony | 7 years | 1–3 years minimum (varies) | 1.5–2 years | Alternatives to incarceration |
| Class E felony | 4 years | 1–1.5 years minimum (varies) | 1.5 years | Often probation-eligible |
