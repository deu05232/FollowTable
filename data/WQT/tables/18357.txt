# Common Clinical Note Shorthand Organized by Category (not by Abbreviation)

| Category | Terms included (abbrev → expansion) | Typical note section | Example snippet | Caveats |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Vitals & measurements | BP → blood pressure; HR → heart rate; RR → respiratory rate; SpO2 → oxygen saturation | Vitals | "BP 128/76, HR 82" | Units vary |
| Labs | CBC → complete blood count; BMP → basic metabolic panel; LFTs → liver function tests; INR → international normalized ratio | Results | "CBC wnl" | Panel contents differ by site |
| Imaging | CXR → chest X-ray; CT → computed tomography; MRI → magnetic resonance imaging; US → ultrasound | Assessment/Plan | "CXR neg" | US may mean ultrasound or u/s shorthand |
| Medications | PRN → as needed; BID → twice daily; TID → three times daily; qHS → every night at bedtime | Orders | "APAP 650 mg PO q6h PRN" | Some abbreviations discouraged |
| Conditions | HTN → hypertension; DM → diabetes mellitus; CHF → congestive heart failure; COPD → chronic obstructive pulmonary disease | Problem list | "PMH: HTN, DM" | Ambiguity across specialties |
| Procedures | CABG → coronary artery bypass graft; EGD → esophagogastroduodenoscopy; LP → lumbar puncture; I&D → incision and drainage | History | "s/p CABG" | Same abbrev can mean different things |
| Disposition | D/C → discharge or discontinue; FU → follow-up; SNF → skilled nursing facility; ICU → intensive care unit | Plan | "D/C home w/ FU" | D/C is context-dependent |
