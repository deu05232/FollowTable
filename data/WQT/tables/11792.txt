# Comparative Public Holidays and Meanings (Selected Countries, Common Observances)

| Holiday / Observance | Country / Jurisdiction | Official status | Typical timing (non-calendar) | What it commemorates (short explanation) | Notes / common confusions |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| New Year's Day | Botswana | Public holiday (nationwide) | Start of January | Marks the first day of the year in the Gregorian calendar. | Common worldwide; sometimes paired with “New Year Holiday” on Jan 2 elsewhere. |
| New Year's Day | Spain | National public holiday | Start of January | Celebrates the beginning of the new calendar year. | Name is similar across countries but legal status can vary by region. |
| Epiphany (Three Kings' Day) | Spain | Public holiday (widely observed) | Early January | Christian feast remembering the visit of the Magi to the infant Jesus. | Often confused with Christmas/Boxing Day in non-Epiphany countries. |
| Labour Day / Workers' Day | Botswana | Public holiday (nationwide) | Early May | International day recognizing workers and labor movements. | Different from “Labor Day” in the U.S./Canada (often in September). |
| Independence Day | Venezuela | National public holiday | Early July | Marks Venezuela’s declaration of independence from Spanish rule (1811). | Not the same as “Battle of Carabobo Day” (another Venezuelan historical observance). |
| Swiss Federal Fast (Eidgenössischer Bettag) | Switzerland (some cantons) | Cantonal public holiday | Third Sunday in September (observed locally) | Day of prayer, reflection, and thanksgiving established in the Swiss Confederation tradition. | Not a “fasting” day in the modern dietary sense; observance varies by canton. |
| Jeûne genevois (Geneva Fast) | Switzerland (Geneva) | Cantonal public holiday | Early September (Thursday after first Sunday of Sept) | Historic day of fasting and remembrance rooted in Geneva’s Protestant tradition. | Name resembles “Swiss Federal Fast” but is specific to Geneva and has different origin. |
| Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Jayanti | India (state-level, esp. Maharashtra) | Public holiday (regional) | Mid–late February | Honors the birth anniversary of Shivaji Maharaj, founder of the Maratha Empire. | Dates can differ based on calendar tradition used (Gregorian vs Hindu calendar). |
| Indigenous Resistance Day (Día de la Resistencia Indígena) | Venezuela | Public holiday | Mid October | Commemorates Indigenous resistance and reinterprets the legacy of colonization in the Americas. | Related to/renamed from “Columbus Day” in some countries; meaning differs by jurisdiction. |
| Assumption Day | Switzerland / Venezuela | Public holiday (varies by region) | Mid August | Christian feast celebrating Mary’s assumption into heaven. | Same name in multiple countries; legal observance differs by canton/country. |
