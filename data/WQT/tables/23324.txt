# Key Neurotransmitters Used in Autonomic Pathways (by Division and Synapse Type)

| Neurotransmitter | Primary ANS division(s) where used | Typical pathway position | Typical receptor family at target | Common site/notes (ANS-specific) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Acetylcholine (ACh) | Sympathetic, Parasympathetic, Enteric-related autonomic reflexes | Preganglionic (both divisions); Postganglionic in most parasympathetic | Nicotinic (Nn) in ganglia; Muscarinic (M) at effector | ACh is the standard transmitter from preganglionic autonomic neurons to ganglionic neurons |
| Norepinephrine (NE) | Sympathetic | Postganglionic (most sympathetic efferents) | Adrenergic (α/β) | Classic “adrenergic” sympathetic neuroeffector transmitter |
| Epinephrine (Epi) | Sympathetic (adrenal medulla endocrine arm) | Released to blood by chromaffin cells (acts like a modified postganglionic output) | Adrenergic (α/β) | Preganglionic sympathetic ACh stimulates adrenal medulla; circulating hormone-like transmitter |
| Dopamine (DA) | Sympathetic (subset) | Postganglionic in some vascular beds | Dopaminergic (D1-like) | Commonly noted in renal/mesenteric vasculature sympathetic signaling (subset, not universal) |
| Nitric oxide (NO) | Parasympathetic (subset) and autonomic NANC pathways | Co-transmitter/modulator at neuroeffector junction | NO/cGMP signaling (not a classic membrane receptor) | Often associated with parasympathetic-mediated smooth muscle relaxation; not used at ganglionic Nn synapses |
| ATP (purinergic) | Sympathetic (subset) and some parasympathetic/enteric autonomic signaling | Co-transmitter at neuroeffector junction | Purinergic (P2X/P2Y) | Frequently described as co-released with NE in some sympathetic terminals |
| Neuropeptide Y (NPY) | Sympathetic (subset) | Co-transmitter/modulator (postganglionic) | Peptide receptors (Y receptors) | Often co-stored/co-released with NE to modulate sympathetic responses |
| Vasoactive intestinal peptide (VIP) | Parasympathetic (subset) | Co-transmitter/modulator (postganglionic) | VIP receptors (VPAC) | Frequently paired with ACh in parasympathetic pathways to certain smooth muscles and glands |
