# Electrical Conductivity by State of Matter (Qualitative Comparison)

| State of matter | Typical electrical behavior | Charge carriers | Notes | Example substances |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Solid (insulating) | Very low conductivity | Bound electrons | Conducts only when breakdown occurs | Glass, PTFE |
| Solid (metallic) | Very high conductivity | Free electrons | Conductivity decreases with temperature | Copper, aluminum |
| Liquid (ionic) | Moderate to high conductivity | Ions | Depends strongly on concentration | Saltwater, molten salts |
| Liquid (non-ionic) | Low conductivity | Few ions | Usually poor conductors | Pure oils, distilled water |
| Gas (neutral) | Extremely low conductivity | Rare ions/electrons | Becomes conductive under high field | Air at STP |
| Plasma | Extremely high conductivity | Electrons and ions | Often treated as quasi-neutral | Fluorescent lamps, solar wind |
| Superconductor (phase) | Zero DC resistivity | Cooper pairs | Occurs below critical temperature | YBCO, NbTi |
