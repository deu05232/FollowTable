# Antibiotic dosing schedules for chronic bacterial prostatitis (adult) — dose and frequency

| Antibiotic (generic) | Dose per administration | Frequency | Route | Typical duration | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ciprofloxacin | 500 mg | twice daily | PO | 4–6 weeks | Common first-line option |
| Levofloxacin | 500 mg | once daily | PO | 4–6 weeks | Alternative fluoroquinolone |
| Ofloxacin | 300 mg | twice daily | PO | 4–6 weeks | Another fluoroquinolone option |
| Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (DS) | 160/800 mg | twice daily | PO | 4–6 weeks | Used when susceptible/appropriate |
| Doxycycline | 100 mg | twice daily | PO | 2–4 weeks | Consider when atypical pathogens suspected |
| Azithromycin | 500 mg | once daily | PO | 3–7 days | Often used for suspected STI-related pathogens |
| Fosfomycin tromethamine | 3 g | every 48–72 hours | PO | 2–6 weeks | Sometimes used for multidrug-resistant organisms |
