# Cooked Food Storage Times by Method (Narrative Cells, No Side-by-Side Comparison)

| Cooked item | Storage guidance (combined text) | Reheating guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Cooked chicken | Keep 3–4 days in the fridge; if freezing, aim to use within about 4 months for best quality. | Reheat to 165°F |
| Cooked ground meat | Refrigerate 3–4 days; freeze up to 3 months for best texture. | Reheat until steaming hot |
| Cooked rice | Refrigerate 3–4 days; freeze around 1 month to reduce quality loss. | Reheat thoroughly |
| Soup/stew | Refrigerate 3–4 days; freeze 2–3 months (longer possible but quality declines). | Bring to a boil |
| Cooked pasta | Refrigerate 3–5 days; freeze 1–2 months depending on sauce. | Heat until hot throughout |
| Cooked vegetables | Refrigerate 3–5 days; freeze 8–12 months for best quality. | Heat to preference |
| Pizza | Refrigerate 3–4 days; freeze 1–2 months in wrapped portions. | Oven reheat for crispness |
| Cooked fish | Refrigerate 3–4 days; freeze 2–3 months to avoid off flavors. | Reheat gently |
| Casserole | Refrigerate 3–4 days; freeze 2–3 months. | Bake to 165°F |
| Cooked beans | Refrigerate 3–5 days; freeze 2–3 months. | Simmer until hot |
