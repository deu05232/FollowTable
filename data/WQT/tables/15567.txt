# Seated & Hip-Opening Asanas  English Names Paired with Sanskrit (Romanized)

| English Pose Name | Sanskrit (Transliteration) | Notes / Closely Named Versions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Staff Pose | Dandasana | Neutral seated foundation; differs from Seated Forward Bend |
| Seated Forward Bend | Paschimottanasana | Often confused with Head-to-Knee (Janu Sirsasana) |
| Head-to-Knee Pose | Janu Sirsasana | One leg extended, one heel in; asymmetrical fold |
| Bound Angle Pose | Baddha Konasana | Also called Cobbler’s Pose/Butterfly |
| Half Lord of the Fishes Pose | Ardha Matsyendrasana | Seated twist; multiple lineages/variations |
| Pigeon Pose | Eka Pada Rajakapotasana | Many classes teach a prep version (“pigeon prep”) |
| Cow Face Pose | Gomukhasana | Often a shoulder + hip opener combo |
| Fire Log Pose | Agnistambhasana | Also called Double Pigeon; can resemble stacked shins |
| Seated Wide-Angle Forward Bend | Upavistha Konasana | Wide legs; fold from hips |
| Boat Pose | Navasana | Core posture; also seen as Ardha Navasana (Half Boat) |
| Happy Baby Pose | Ananda Balasana | Supine hip opener; sometimes used as a cooldown |
| Reclining Bound Angle Pose | Supta Baddha Konasana | “Supta” indicates reclining |
