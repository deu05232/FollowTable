# Public holidays in Canada (selected) — dates, observances, and what they commemorate

| Date | Observance | What it commemorates / meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| July 1 | Canada Day | Marks the confederation of Canada in 1867 and celebrates national identity. |
| First Monday in September | Labour Day | Recognizes workers and the labour movement; widely observed as an end-of-summer public break. |
| December 25 | Christmas Day | Commemorates the birth of Jesus Christ and is a major cultural and religious holiday. |
| December 26 | Boxing Day | Traditionally a day for giving to those in need and post-Christmas customs; widely observed as a public day off. |
