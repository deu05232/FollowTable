# Chronological Escalation in the U.S.–Japan Pacific War (Selected Milestones, 1941–1945)

| Phase | Date | Milestone | Escalation / Turning-Point Note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Opening shocks | 7 Dec 1941 | Pearl Harbor attacked | Converts Pacific crisis into full-scale U.S. war commitment. |
| Opening shocks | Dec 1941 – May 1942 | Japan’s rapid conquests across Southeast Asia & Western Pacific | Expands war footprint and raises stakes for a long U.S. counteroffensive. |
| First checks | 4–8 May 1942 | Battle of the Coral Sea | First major stop to Japanese expansion; sets conditions for the next decisive carrier battle. |
| First checks | 4–7 Jun 1942 | Battle of Midway | Strategic pivot: Japan loses key carriers; initiative begins shifting to the U.S. |
| Allied advance | 1943 (Jan–Nov) | Solomon Islands / New Guinea offensives intensify | Sustained pressure and attrition erode Japan’s ability to replace losses. |
| Allied advance | 20–23 Jun 1944 | Philippine Sea (“Great Marianas Turkey Shoot”) | Dramatically reduces Japan’s trained naval aviators, weakening defense of the home islands. |
| Air war enabling step | 15 Jun – 9 Jul 1944 | Saipan captured (Marianas) | Enables sustained long-range bombing of Japan from forward bases, accelerating endgame urgency. |
| Strategic isolation | Oct 1944 | Leyte Gulf | Japan’s naval power collapses as a decisive factor; blockade/isolation becomes more effective. |
| Endgame battles | 19 Feb – 26 Mar 1945 | Iwo Jima | Improves feasibility and tempo of B-29 operations (escort/emergency landings), intensifying pressure on Japan. |
| Endgame battles | 1 Apr – 22 Jun 1945 | Okinawa | Massive casualties heighten concern about invasion costs and strengthen search for war-ending options. |
| Diplomatic endgame | 26 Jul 1945 | Potsdam Declaration | Defines surrender terms and increases pressure in the final weeks. |
| Final act | 6 Aug 1945 | Hiroshima bombed | Marks the climactic escalation at the end of the Pacific war trajectory. |
