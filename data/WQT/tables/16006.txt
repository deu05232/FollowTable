# Vaccinations for Brazil (Travel Advice) — Country-Specific Requirements and Recommendations

| Vaccine | Status for Entry/Travel | Applies to All Travelers? | Who Should Get It (Traveler Categories) | Notes / Proof Needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Yellow fever | Recommended; may be required if arriving from certain countries | No | Travelers visiting risk areas; travelers with recent travel/transit in yellow-fever-risk countries | Proof may be checked at entry when arriving from a risk country (International Certificate) |
| Hepatitis A | Recommended | Yes (most travelers) | Food/water exposure risk, first-time travelers, those staying with locals | Consider even for short stays |
| Hepatitis B | Recommended | No | Longer stays, potential medical/dental care, sexual exposure risk, tattoos/piercings | Often advised for frequent travelers |
| Typhoid | Recommended | No | Rural travel, street food, extended trips | Injectable or oral options |
| Rabies (pre-exposure) | Consider for some travelers | No | Outdoor/adventure travel, work with animals, remote areas with limited access to care | Still need post-exposure doses if bitten |
| Routine vaccines (MMR, Tdap, Varicella, Polio, Influenza) | Ensure up to date | Yes | All travelers | Not typically an entry requirement, but strongly advised |
| COVID-19 | Recommended / may be required by carrier or policy changes | Varies | All eligible travelers | Check current entry rules and airline requirements |
| Malaria vaccine | Not applicable (no widely used travel vaccine) | No | N/A | Prevention relies on chemoprophylaxis + bite avoidance depending on region |
