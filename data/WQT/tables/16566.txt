# Antibiotic dosing regimens commonly used for prostatitis (adult dosing examples)

| Antibiotic (generic) | Typical oral dosing regimen | Usual duration (acute vs chronic bacterial prostatitis) | Notes on when chosen |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ciprofloxacin | 500 mg PO twice daily | Acute: 2–4 weeks; Chronic: 4–6 weeks | Often used when gram-negative coverage is needed |
| Levofloxacin | 500–750 mg PO once daily | Acute: 2–4 weeks; Chronic: 4–6 weeks | Once-daily fluoroquinolone option |
| Ofloxacin | 300 mg PO twice daily | Acute: 2–4 weeks; Chronic: 4–6 weeks | Alternative fluoroquinolone regimen |
| Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (TMP-SMX) | 160 mg/800 mg PO twice daily | Acute: 2–4 weeks; Chronic: 4–6 weeks | Common alternative when fluoroquinolones not suitable |
| Doxycycline | 100 mg PO twice daily | Typically 10–14 days (STI-associated) or per culture | Often considered when Chlamydia suspected |
| Azithromycin | 500 mg PO once daily | Typically 3–7 days (STI-associated) or per protocol | Used in select STI-related cases |
| Ceftriaxone (parenteral) | 1 g IM/IV once daily | Often initial dose(s) then step-down PO; duration per source | Sometimes used for severe illness or gonococcal infection |
| Amoxicillin-clavulanate | 875 mg/125 mg PO twice daily | Commonly 2–4 weeks (culture-guided) | Option when Enterococcus or susceptible organisms suspected |
