# Straight-Chain Alkane Fuel Blends and Typical Boiling Ranges

| Fuel blend / cut | Main alkane components (approx.) | Typical carbon range | Boiling range (°C) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| LPG (commercial) | Propane + n-butane (variable) | C3–C4 | -42 to -0.5 | Mixture; composition depends on season |
| Light naphtha | n-pentane + n-hexane + isomers | C5–C6 | 30–90 | Refinery fraction, not a single compound |
| Heavy naphtha | n-heptane + n-octane + isomers | C7–C8 | 90–130 | Fractional cut |
| Kerosene-range paraffins | n-nonane to n-dodecane (plus others) | C9–C12 | 150–240 | Mixture used as fuel/jet range |
| Diesel-range paraffins | n-dodecane to n-hexadecane (plus others) | C12–C16 | 180–360 | Broad boiling interval |
| Mineral spirits | C7–C12 paraffins/cycloparaffins | C7–C12 | 130–230 | Solvent/fuel blend |
| Paraffin wax (technical) | long-chain n-alkanes (mixture) | C20–C40 | >300 | Not a single boiling point |
| Natural gasoline | pentanes + hexanes (variable) | C5–C6 | 25–90 | Field/processing mixture |
