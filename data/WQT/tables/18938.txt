# Aneuploidy Syndromes by Prenatal Ultrasound Findings (Syndrome-Focused, No Chromosomal Region Column)

| Suspected syndrome | Common ultrasound findings | Pregnancy screening clue | Typical outcome/complication | Comments |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Down syndrome | Increased nuchal translucency, cardiac defects | Positive serum screen | Variable intellectual disability | Ultrasound is not diagnostic |
| Edwards syndrome | Growth restriction, clenched hands, omphalocele | Low PAPP-A | High perinatal mortality | Multiple structural anomalies |
| Patau syndrome | Holoprosencephaly, cleft lip/palate | Abnormal serum markers | Severe congenital anomalies | Often detected early |
| Turner syndrome | Cystic hygroma, hydrops | Abnormal cfDNA result | Congenital heart disease | Phenotype variable |
| Klinefelter syndrome | Often normal prenatal US | cfDNA may flag | Hypogonadism/infertility later | Many undiagnosed until adulthood |
| Triploidy | Severe growth restriction, placental abnormalities | Very abnormal markers | Early pregnancy loss common | Maternal complications possible |
| 22q11.2 deletion syndrome | Conotruncal heart defects | cfDNA microdeletion panel | Immune/cardiac issues | Ultrasound signs overlap |
