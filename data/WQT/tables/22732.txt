# English and British Monarchs  Claims, Depositions, and Succession Notes

| Monarch | Claim/Basis | Accession context | Deposition/Abdication | Successor | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Edward II | Hereditary | Succeeded Edward I | Deposed by Parliament | Edward III | Favored Despensers; baronial revolt |
| Richard II | Hereditary | Succeeded Edward III line | Forced abdication | Henry IV | Bolingbroke’s seizure |
| Henry VI | Hereditary | Infant king | Deposed (first time) | Edward IV | Wars of the Roses |
| Mary I | Statutory & hereditary | After Edward VI | Not deposed | Elizabeth I | Catholic restoration |
| James II | Hereditary | After Charles II | Deposed (Glorious Revolution) | William III & Mary II | Flight interpreted as abdication |
| Edward VIII | Hereditary | After George V | Abdicated | George VI | Marriage crisis |
| James Francis Edward Stuart | Jacobite claim | Claimed after 1701 | Never reigned | — | Known as “Old Pretender” |
