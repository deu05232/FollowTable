# Irish Surname Anglicizations Mapped to Traditional Gaelic Forms (Genealogy-Oriented)

| English Surname | Gaelic Equivalent (Irish) | Common English Variants | Notes for Older Records |
|---|---|---|---|
| McCarthy | Mac Cárthaigh | MacCarthy, McCarty | Sometimes written without capital C in older handwriting |
| O'Connor | Ó Conchobair | O Connor, Connor | Often reduced to Connor in English records |
| Doyle | Ó Dúill | Doyel | Can be mistaken for a given name; here treated as surname |
| Gallagher | Ó Gallchobhair | Gallaher, Gallacher | Variant spellings frequent in emigration records |
| Donnelly | Ó Donaill | Donley, Danly | Not the same as O’Donnell (Ó Domhnaill) |
| Nolan | Ó Nualláin | Nolen | Vowel shifts common in anglicization |
| Fitzgerald | Mac Gearailt | FitzGerald, Gerald | “Fitz-” often retained; Gaelic uses Mac + Gerald |
| Lynch | Ó Loingsigh | Linch | Sometimes confused with the unrelated surname “Lynn” |
| O'Donnell | Ó Domhnaill | O Donnell, Donnell | “mh” can look like “w/v” in manuscripts |
| Maguire | Mag Uidhir | McGuire, M’Guire | Mag/Mac prefixes can interchange in English |
| Keane | Ó Catháin | Kane, O'Kane | Regional: Keane often Munster; Kane common Ulster |
| Kavanagh | Mac Cárthaigh (Caomhánach) | Cavanagh, Kavanah | Caomhánach is the specific Gaelic family form often cited |
