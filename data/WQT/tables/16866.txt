# State-Level Treatment of Capital Gains in the U.S. (Short-Term vs Long-Term) — Selected States

| State | Short-Term Capital Gains (Holding ≤ 1 year) | Long-Term Capital Gains (Holding > 1 year) | How Taxed (State Rule of Thumb) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| California | Taxed as ordinary income (up to top state bracket) | Taxed as ordinary income (no special LTCG rate) | No separate capital-gains schedule | High top bracket; no preferential LTCG rate |
| Florida | 0% | 0% | No state individual income tax | State does not tax individual wage/capital income |
| Texas | 0% | 0% | No state individual income tax | Applies to individuals (not corporate franchise taxes) |
| New York | Taxed as ordinary income (up to top state bracket) | Taxed as ordinary income (no special LTCG rate) | No separate capital-gains schedule | NYC residents may owe additional local income tax |
| Washington | Generally 0% on most capital gains | State capital gains excise tax may apply to certain long-term gains | Special excise tax regime (limited base) | Applies to certain asset sales; exemptions/thresholds may apply |
| Massachusetts | Taxed as ordinary income (state rate) | Preferential rate may apply for qualifying long-term gains | Separate treatment for some long-term gains | Long-term gains may receive a lower rate than short-term |
| Pennsylvania | Taxed at flat state income tax rate | Taxed at flat state income tax rate | No holding-period preference | Same rate generally applies to short/long term |
| Illinois | Taxed at flat state income tax rate | Taxed at flat state income tax rate | No holding-period preference | Flat-rate structure (state-level) |
| Colorado | Taxed as ordinary income (flat state rate) | Taxed as ordinary income (flat state rate) | No holding-period preference | Same rate generally applies to short/long term |
| New Hampshire | Typically 0% for most capital gains | Typically 0% for most capital gains | No broad tax on wage income; limited investment-income taxes historically | Treatment can differ for dividends/interest vs gains |
