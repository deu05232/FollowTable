# Yellow Fever Travel Vaccine Guidance by Country (Entry Requirement vs Recommendation)

| Country | Yellow fever entry requirement (mandatory) | Yellow fever vaccination recommended (health advice) | Applies to whom / when | Certificate validity / notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Kenya | Yes | Yes | Required for travelers arriving from countries with yellow fever transmission risk; recommended for most travelers visiting risk areas | Proof may be checked at entry; carry the International Certificate of Vaccination |
| South Africa | Conditional | Sometimes | Required only if arriving from/transiting through a yellow-fever-risk country; generally not recommended for most itineraries outside risk exposure | Requirement is tied to travel history, not destination alone |
| Indonesia | Conditional | Sometimes | Required if arriving from a yellow-fever-risk country; not routinely recommended for most travelers unless risk exposure via itinerary | Travelers may be denied entry without proof if coming from risk areas |
| Brazil | No (typically) | Yes | Not generally required for entry; recommended for travel to endemic regions (e.g., parts of Amazon basin) | Recommendation varies by state/region and season |
| Ghana | Yes | Yes | Mandatory for entry for most travelers; also recommended due to ongoing transmission risk | Commonly checked at the border; certificate is essential |
| Peru | No (typically) | Yes | Not typically required for entry; recommended for Amazon/lowland jungle areas | Not usually recommended for high-altitude cities |
| Thailand | Conditional | No/limited | Required only if arriving from a yellow-fever-risk country; generally not recommended for Thailand-only itineraries | Focus is on entry rule tied to prior travel |
| Tanzania | Conditional | Yes (select areas) | May be required if arriving from a risk country; recommended for travel to certain risk areas depending on itinerary | Requirements can change; verify with current border guidance |
