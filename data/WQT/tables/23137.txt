# High-Heat Suitability vs Fat Composition  Cooking Oils (Smoke Point Bands + Fat Balance)

| Oil | Smoke point band | Approx. smoke point (°C) | Heating behavior summary | SFA (%) | MUFA (%) | PUFA (%) | Fat profile takeaway |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Ghee (clarified butter) | High | 250 | Handles high heat better than butter due to removed milk solids | 62 | 29 | 4 | Very SFA-heavy; comparatively stable for high heat |
| Butter (whole) | Low | 150 | Milk solids brown and burn; not ideal for high heat | 51 | 21 | 3 | SFA-heavy but low smoke point limits high-heat use |
| Lard (rendered pork fat) | Medium-high | 190 | Good for pan-frying; flavor can be noticeable | 39 | 45 | 11 | Balanced SFA/MUFA; moderate heat tolerance |
| Tallow (beef fat) | High | 210 | Traditional frying fat; solid at room temp | 50 | 42 | 4 | High SFA+MUFA; typically stable for frying |
| Palm oil (refined) | Medium-high | 235 | Often used for frying; semi-solid | 49 | 37 | 9 | High SFA; generally more heat-stable than high-PUFA oils |
| Rice bran oil (refined) | High | 255 | Common for deep-frying; relatively neutral | 22 | 43 | 33 | Mixed MUFA/PUFA; chosen for frying performance |
| Safflower oil (high-oleic, refined) | High | 230 | Better for high heat than linoleic safflower | 7 | 75 | 13 | High MUFA variant suited to higher heat |
| Safflower oil (linoleic, refined) | Medium-high | 215 | Prolonged high heat can be less ideal due to high PUFA | 7 | 13 | 75 | Very PUFA-heavy; better for shorter cooks |
| Flaxseed oil (unrefined) | Low | 110 | Not for heating; use cold | 9 | 18 | 73 | Very PUFA-heavy (omega-3 rich); heat sensitive |
| Walnut oil (unrefined) | Low-medium | 160 | Use gently or as finishing oil | 9 | 23 | 63 | PUFA-heavy; best for low heat/finishing |
| Almond oil (refined) | Medium-high | 220 | Mild flavor; works for sautéing and some frying | 8 | 70 | 17 | MUFA-forward; generally moderate stability |
