# Autonomic Neurotransmitters Mapped to Preganglionic vs Postganglionic Outputs (including major exceptions)

| Neurotransmitter | Used at autonomic ganglia (preganglionic → ganglionic neuron) | Used at neuroeffector junction (postganglionic → effector) | Division where this is typical | Major exception/clarifying note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Acetylcholine (ACh) | Yes (dominant transmitter; Nn receptors) | Yes in most parasympathetic outputs (muscarinic receptors) | Both divisions at ganglia; Parasympathetic at effector | Sympathetic postganglionic to most targets is not ACh (see NE), but some sympathetic outputs can be cholinergic |
| Norepinephrine (NE) | No (not the standard ganglionic transmitter) | Yes (dominant transmitter for most sympathetic postganglionic fibers) | Sympathetic (postganglionic) | Often co-released with ATP/NPY in subsets rather than acting alone |
| Epinephrine (Epi) | No | Acts mainly via circulation rather than a classic synaptic postganglionic ending | Sympathetic (adrenal medulla pathway) | Released from adrenal medulla after preganglionic ACh stimulation; functions as a blood-borne autonomic messenger |
| Dopamine (DA) | No (not typical) | Yes in selected sympathetic pathways | Sympathetic (subset) | Most sympathetic terminals are noradrenergic; DA is prominent only in certain regional vascular control descriptions |
| Nitric oxide (NO) | Not typical as primary ganglionic transmitter | Yes as NANC co-transmitter/modulator | Often Parasympathetic-associated (subset) | Gaseous transmitter; synthesized on demand; not stored in vesicles like classic transmitters |
| ATP (purinergic) | Not typical as primary ganglionic transmitter | Yes as co-transmitter in some autonomic terminals | Sympathetic (common subset), also elsewhere | Acts fast via P2X (ionotropic) and slower via P2Y (metabotropic) depending on target |
| Neuropeptide Y (NPY) | No | Yes as co-transmitter/modulator | Sympathetic (subset) | Typically coexists with NE to shape duration/strength of sympathetic signaling |
| Vasoactive intestinal peptide (VIP) | No | Yes as co-transmitter/modulator | Parasympathetic (subset) | Often co-released with ACh in parasympathetic neuroeffector signaling; peptide-mediated longer-lasting effects |
