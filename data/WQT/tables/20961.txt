# Non-cardioselective Beta Blockers — Dosage Forms and Therapeutic Uses

| Drug (Brand/Generic) | Dosage Form | Common Indications | Usual Adult Dose Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Inderal (propranolol) | Tablet / Oral solution | Hypertension, migraine prophylaxis, essential tremor | 40–320 mg/day | Non-selective beta blocker |
| Coreg (carvedilol) | Tablet | Heart failure, hypertension | 6.25–50 mg/day | Also has alpha-blocking activity |
| Normodyne (labetalol) | Tablet / Injection | Hypertensive urgency, pregnancy hypertension | 200–2400 mg/day | Used IV for severe hypertension |
| Sotalol AF (sotalol) | Tablet | Atrial fibrillation, ventricular arrhythmias | 80–320 mg/day | QT prolongation risk |
| Corgard (nadolol) | Tablet | Hypertension, angina | 40–240 mg/day | Long half-life |
| Trandate (labetalol) | Injection | Hypertensive emergency | Titrated IV | Hospital use |
| Betagan (levobunolol) | Ophthalmic drops | Glaucoma | 1–2 drops daily | Local administration |
| Timoptic (timolol) | Ophthalmic drops | Glaucoma | 1 drop 1–2x/day | Systemic effects possible |
