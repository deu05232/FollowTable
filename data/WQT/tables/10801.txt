# Common Antibiotics Considered for Prostatitis (Names and Classes Only)

| Antibiotic | Drug class | Typical indication note | Common brand examples |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ciprofloxacin | Fluoroquinolone | Often used for bacterial prostatitis | Cipro |
| Levofloxacin | Fluoroquinolone | Often used when gram-negative coverage desired | Levaquin |
| Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole | Sulfonamide + DHFR inhibitor | Option for susceptible organisms | Bactrim, Septra |
| Doxycycline | Tetracycline | Considered when STI pathogens suspected | Vibramycin |
| Azithromycin | Macrolide | Used for chlamydial infections | Zithromax |
| Ceftriaxone | Cephalosporin (3rd gen) | Used in some STI-related regimens | Rocephin |
| Fosfomycin | Phosphonic acid derivative | Sometimes discussed for resistant organisms | Monurol |
| Amoxicillin-clavulanate | Aminopenicillin + beta-lactamase inhibitor | Sometimes used if susceptible | Augmentin |
