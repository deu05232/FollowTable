# Autonomic Neurochemistry in Clinical Drugs (examples used in ANS)

| Drug/Agent | Primary target receptor | Mimics/blocks | Typical clinical use | Noted autonomic outcome |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Atropine | Muscarinic (M) antagonist | Blocks parasympathetic signaling | Bradycardia, organophosphate poisoning | ↑ HR, ↓ secretions |
| Propranolol | β1/β2 antagonist | Blocks sympathetic effects | Hypertension, arrhythmias | ↓ HR, ↓ tremor |
| Phenylephrine | α1 agonist | Mimics sympathetic | Nasal decongestant, hypotension | Vasoconstriction |
| Albuterol | β2 agonist | Mimics sympathetic | Asthma | Bronchodilation |
| Pilocarpine | Muscarinic agonist | Mimics parasympathetic | Glaucoma, xerostomia | ↑ lacrimation/salivation |
| Prazosin | α1 antagonist | Blocks sympathetic | Hypertension, PTSD nightmares | Vasodilation |
| Neostigmine | AChE inhibitor | Increases acetylcholine | Myasthenia gravis | ↑ GI motility |
| Clonidine | α2 agonist | Reduces sympathetic outflow | Hypertension | ↓ NE release, sedation |
