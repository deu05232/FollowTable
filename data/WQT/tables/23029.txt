# Companion Plants Used as Natural Pest Repellents (Plant–Pest Pairings)

| Plant (common name) | Scientific name | Pests reportedly deterred | Typical placement/use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Basil | Ocimum basilicum | Mosquitoes, houseflies | Pots near doors/windows; around patios |
| Lavender | Lavandula angustifolia | Moths, fleas, mosquitoes | Near seating areas; along walkways |
| Rosemary | Salvia rosmarinus | Cabbage moth, carrot fly | Mixed into veggie beds; border plant |
| Sage | Salvia officinalis | Cabbage moth, carrot fly | Near brassicas and carrots |
| Mint (peppermint) | Mentha × piperita | Ants, aphids, mosquitoes | In containers to prevent spreading; near entryways |
| Catnip | Nepeta cataria | Mosquitoes, cockroaches | In pots near outdoor areas |
| Nasturtium | Tropaeolum majus | Whiteflies, squash bugs | As a companion/edge plant in beds |
| Garlic | Allium sativum | Aphids, spider mites | Interplant among susceptible crops |
| Onion | Allium cepa | Carrot fly, aphids | Rows adjacent to carrots/leafy greens |
| Marigold (French marigold) | Tagetes patula | Root-knot nematodes, aphids | Interplant in vegetable beds |
| Rue | Ruta graveolens | Fleas, Japanese beetles | As a border plant (handle carefully) |
