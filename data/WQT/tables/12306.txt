# Aktion Reinhard Extermination Camps (Death Camps) in Occupied Poland

| Program | Extermination camp | District / locality (then) | Transported victims (primary groups) | Estimated killed (approx.) | Fate of the site after peak killing |
|---|---|---|---|---:|---|
| Aktion Reinhard | Bełżec | Lublin District (General Government) | Jews from Galicia & Lublin region; some Roma | 430,000–600,000 | Dismantled; grounds replanted/covered to erase evidence.
| Aktion Reinhard | Sobibór | Lublin District (General Government) | Jews from Poland, Netherlands, Slovakia; some USSR Jews | 170,000–250,000 | Closed after Oct 1943 revolt; demolished and disguised as a farm.
| Aktion Reinhard | Treblinka II | Warsaw District (General Government) | Jews from Warsaw and Radom; some from other countries | 700,000–900,000 | Closed in 1943; destroyed and concealed; minimal remnants.
| Aktion Reinhard (related) | Chełmno (Kulmhof) | Wartheland (annexed) | Jews from Łódź region; Roma from nearby areas | 150,000–320,000 | Temporarily halted 1943; resumed 1944; later destroyed as Soviets advanced.
| Aktion Reinhard (support) | Lublin/Majdanek killing facilities | Lublin District (General Government) | Jews, Polish prisoners, Soviet POWs | 78,000–235,000 | Liberated 1944; evidence of mixed functions (labor + mass killing).
| Aktion Reinhard (deportation network) | Key deportation hub: Warsaw (Umschlagplatz) → Treblinka II | Warsaw District | Primarily Jews from Warsaw Ghetto | (part of Treblinka total) | Served as major transit point enabling extermination-camp throughput.
| Aktion Reinhard (deportation network) | Key deportation hub: Lwów/Lemberg → Bełżec | Galicia District | Primarily Jews from Galicia | (part of Bełżec total) | Illustrates program logistics; killing occurred at extermination camp.
