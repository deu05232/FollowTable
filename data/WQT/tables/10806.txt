# Prostatitis Antibiotic Regimen Overview (Duration and Route Without Concrete Dose Frequency)

| Antibiotic option | Route | Typical course length | When it is considered | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ciprofloxacin | Oral | 2–6 weeks | Suspected gram-negative bacterial prostatitis | Adjust to culture results |
| Levofloxacin | Oral | 2–6 weeks | Alternative fluoroquinolone option | Consider local resistance |
| Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole | Oral | 4–6 weeks | If organism is susceptible | Monitor for adverse effects |
| Doxycycline | Oral | 2–4 weeks | When STI pathogens are possible | Partner evaluation may be needed |
| Azithromycin | Oral | Short course | Chlamydia-associated cases | Follow STI guidance |
| Ceftriaxone | IM/IV | Single-encounter therapy | Gonorrhea coverage in STI scenarios | Often paired with another agent |
| Fosfomycin | Oral | Multi-dose course | Resistant organisms discussed in some settings | Evidence varies |
| Amoxicillin-clavulanate | Oral | 2–4 weeks | If culture suggests susceptibility | Not first-line in many guides |
