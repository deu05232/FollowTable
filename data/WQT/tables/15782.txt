# pH Classification Ranges for Drinking Water (No Brands)

| Category | pH Range | Labeling Language Often Seen | Typical Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Strongly acidic | < 4.0 | "very acidic" | Often flavored/acidified drinks |
| Acidic | 4.0–6.4 | "acidic" | Some carbonated waters may fall here |
| Slightly acidic | 6.5–6.9 | "near neutral" | May vary by source/bottling |
| Neutral | 7.0 | "neutral" | Pure water reference point |
| Slightly alkaline | 7.1–8.4 | "alkaline" | Many spring/mineral waters |
| Alkaline | 8.5–9.5 | "high pH" | Often ionized/processed waters |
| Very alkaline | > 9.5 | "ultra alkaline" | Less common for bottled water |
| Measurement caveats | N/A | N/A | Temperature, carbonation, and storage can shift readings |
