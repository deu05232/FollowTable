# Antibiotic Pharmacokinetics Reference (Not a Dosing Schedule)

| Antibiotic | Oral bioavailability | Protein binding | Half-life (hours) | Primary elimination | Key interaction example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ciprofloxacin | Moderate | Low–moderate | ~4 | Renal | Chelation with iron/calcium |
| Levofloxacin | High | Low | ~6–8 | Renal | QT-prolonging agents |
| Doxycycline | High | High | ~18–22 | Biliary/fecal | Reduced absorption with antacids |
| Azithromycin | Moderate | Low | ~40–68 | Biliary | QT-prolonging agents |
| Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole | High | Moderate | ~10 (TMP) | Renal | Potentiates warfarin |
| Fosfomycin | Moderate | Low | ~3–8 | Renal | Metoclopramide may lower levels |
| Ceftriaxone | N/A (parenteral) | High | ~6–9 | Biliary/renal | Calcium precipitation (IV) |
