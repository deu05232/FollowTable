# World War II Timeline  Pacific War Milestones with Manhattan Project Progress

| Date | Pacific Theater Milestone | Parallel Technical/Program Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Dec 7, 1941 | Attack on Pearl Harbor draws the United States into war with Japan | U.S. scientific advisory work on uranium fission expands into organized research |
| Apr 18, 1942 | Doolittle Raid signals U.S. ability to strike the Japanese home islands | Early bomb design approaches (gun-type vs. implosion) begin to be evaluated |
| Jun 4–7, 1942 | Battle of Midway shifts the strategic balance in the Pacific | Manhattan Project is formally organized under U.S. Army Corps of Engineers management |
| Aug 7, 1942 | Guadalcanal campaign begins, marking the start of major U.S. offensives | Uranium enrichment pathways (gaseous diffusion, electromagnetic separation) scaled up |
| Oct 20, 1944 | Battle of Leyte Gulf cripples Japanese naval power | Los Alamos refines implosion lens concepts and initiator requirements |
| Mar 9–10, 1945 | Tokyo firebombing demonstrates strategic airpower against Japan | Bomb assembly procedures and component tolerances are standardized |
| Jul 16, 1945 | Potsdam-era pressure on Japan intensifies (Pacific context) | Trinity test detonates the first nuclear device in New Mexico |
| Aug 6, 1945 | Atomic bombing of Hiroshima | “Little Boy” gun-type uranium device used; blast yield estimates circulate post-strike |
