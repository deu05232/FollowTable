# Common Antibiotic Regimens for Acute Bacterial Prostatitis (Adults)

| Antibiotic (oral unless noted) | Typical dose | Frequency | Typical duration |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ciprofloxacin | 500 mg | twice daily | 10–14 days |
| Levofloxacin | 500–750 mg | once daily | 10–14 days |
| Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (TMP/SMX DS) | 160/800 mg | twice daily | 10–14 days |
| Doxycycline | 100 mg | twice daily | 10–14 days |
| Ceftriaxone (IV/IM) | 1–2 g | once daily | 1–3 days, then step-down oral therapy |
| Gentamicin (IV) | 5–7 mg/kg | once daily | 1–3 days, then step-down oral therapy |
| Piperacillin-tazobactam (IV) | 3.375–4.5 g | every 6–8 hours | severe cases; tailor to culture |
| Ertapenem (IV) | 1 g | once daily | severe/resistant cases; tailor to culture |
