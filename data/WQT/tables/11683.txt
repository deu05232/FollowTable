# Oxygen-Containing Functional Groups  Structures and IUPAC Naming Cues

| Functional group (main item) | Typical structural notation (generic) | Functional group formula fragment | IUPAC prefix (as substituent) | IUPAC suffix (as principal group) | Notes / quick recognition |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Alcohol (hydroxyl) | R–OH | –OH | hydroxy- | -ol | O bonded to sp3 C; H-bond donor/acceptor |
| Phenol (aryl hydroxyl) | Ar–OH | –OH on aromatic ring | hydroxy- | -ol (often “phenol” as retained name) | OH directly on aromatic ring (Ar) |
| Ether | R–O–R' | –O– | alkoxy- (e.g., methoxy-) | (no common suffix; named as alkoxyalkane) | O between two carbons; not carbonyl |
| Aldehyde | R–C(=O)H | –CHO | formyl- (or oxo- when substituent) | -al | Terminal carbonyl with H on carbonyl carbon |
| Ketone | R–C(=O)–R' | >C=O | oxo- | -one | Internal carbonyl; carbonyl carbon bonded to two carbons |
| Carboxylic acid | R–C(=O)OH | –COOH | carboxy- | -oic acid | Carbonyl + hydroxyl on same carbon |
| Ester | R–C(=O)OR' | –COOR' | alkoxycarbonyl- | -oate | Carbonyl attached to –OR; named “alkyl alkanoate” |
| Acid anhydride | R–C(=O)–O–C(=O)–R' | –(CO)O(CO)– | (alkyl/aryl)carbonyl- (context-dependent) | -oic anhydride | Two acyl groups linked by O |
| Acyl (carboxylic) halide | R–C(=O)X | –COX | halocarbonyl- (e.g., chlorocarbonyl-) | -oyl chloride / -oyl bromide | Carbonyl bonded to halogen (X = Cl, Br, etc.) |
| Carboxamide (amide) | R–C(=O)NH2 (or NR2) | –CONH2 / –CONR2 | carbamoyl- | -amide | Carbonyl bonded to nitrogen |
| Lactone (cyclic ester) | cyclic –C(=O)O– in ring | –COO– (in ring) | (often named as oxacycloalkanone derivatives) | -one / -olactone (common) | Ester functionality inside a ring |
| Epoxide (oxirane) | 3-membered cyclic ether | –O– (in 3-ring) | epoxy- | (named as oxirane or epoxyalkane) | Strained 3-membered ring with O |
| Peroxide | R–O–O–R' | –O–O– | peroxy- | (no common suffix) | O–O bond present; distinct from ether |
