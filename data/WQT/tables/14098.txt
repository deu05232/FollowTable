# Orbital Foraminal Anatomy for Nerves Involved in Eye Movements (with Muscle Mentions)

| Orbital opening / canal | Nerve(s) passing | Typical lesion sign | Example muscle affected (if any) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Superior orbital fissure | CN III | Ptosis, diplopia, "down and out" eye | Levator palpebrae superioris / multiple EOMs |
| Superior orbital fissure | CN IV | Vertical diplopia, worse on stairs | Superior oblique |
| Superior orbital fissure | CN VI | Inability to abduct | Lateral rectus |
| Superior orbital fissure | V1 (ophthalmic) | Loss of corneal reflex (afferent) | Not a muscle (sensory) |
| Optic canal | CN II | Vision loss | Not applicable |
| Inferior orbital fissure | Infraorbital nerve (V2 branch) | Cheek numbness | Not applicable |
| Cavernous sinus (region) | CN III, IV, V1, VI | Painful ophthalmoplegia | Multiple EOMs (nonspecific) |
| Stylomastoid foramen | CN VII | Inability to close eye | Orbicularis oculi (not extraocular) |
