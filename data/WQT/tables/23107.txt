# Winemaking Styles Cheat Sheet  How Production Choices Change Flavor and Body

| Production Style/Technique | Typical Wines | Key Aroma/Flavor Impact | Perceived Body Impact (Light–Full) | Common Indicators | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Stainless steel fermentation | Aromatic whites | Citrus, green fruit, fresh herbs | Light | "Unoaked" | Minimizes oak flavors |
| New oak aging | Many reds & some whites | Vanilla, toast, smoke, spice | Medium–Full | "Barrel-aged" | Adds tannin & texture |
| Malolactic fermentation | Chardonnay, some reds | Butter, cream, soft acidity | Medium | "MLF" / creamy | Rounds mouthfeel |
| Extended maceration | Structured reds | Dark fruit, savory, tannic | Full | Deep color, high tannin | More extraction |
| Carbonic maceration | Beaujolais-style reds | Banana, bubblegum, bright fruit | Light–Medium | "Nouveau" | Low tannin |
| Sur lie aging | Whites & sparkling | Bread dough, nuttiness | Medium | "Sur lie" | Adds texture |
| Fortification | Port/Sherry styles | Dried fruit, nuts, caramel | Medium–Full | Higher ABV | Alters sweetness often |
| Residual sugar retention | Off-dry styles | Honeyed fruit, candied notes | Light–Medium | "Off-dry" | Sweetness affects perception |
