# Plants Known to Repel Specific Pests (Comparison Table by Plant)

| Plant (common name) | Scientific name | Pests repelled (outcome) | Typical placement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Marigold (French marigold) | *Tagetes patula* | root-knot nematodes, aphids | Vegetable beds; borders |
| Lemongrass | *Cymbopogon citratus* | mosquitoes, gnats | Pots near doorways/patios |
| Rue | *Ruta graveolens* | fleas, Japanese beetles | Perimeter planting (handle with care; can irritate skin) |
| Tansy | *Tanacetum vulgare* | ants, flies | Near compost areas or edges (can be invasive) |
| Thyme | *Thymus vulgaris* | cabbage worms, whiteflies | Around brassicas and mixed beds |
| Dill | *Anethum graveolens* | aphids, spider mites | Near susceptible ornamentals/veg (can self-seed) |
| Bay laurel | *Laurus nobilis* | pantry moths, weevils | Leaves used dried for storage areas |
| Borage | *Borago officinalis* | tomato hornworms, cabbage worms | Interplanted in veg beds; attracts beneficials too |
