# Key WWII Pacific Milestones Leading to the Hiroshima Bombing (Chronological Timeline)

| Date | Event (short description) |
|---|---|
| 7 Dec 1941 | Attack on Pearl Harbor brings the United States into WWII against Japan. |
| 8–10 Dec 1941 | Japan invades the Philippines; U.S.–Japanese fighting expands across the Western Pacific. |
| 4–7 Jun 1942 | Battle of Midway shifts the naval balance toward the United States in the Pacific. |
| 7 Aug 1942 | Guadalcanal campaign begins, marking sustained Allied offensive operations. |
| 19–20 Jun 1944 | Battle of the Philippine Sea weakens Japanese carrier air power (“Great Marianas Turkey Shoot”). |
| 24 Jul–2 Aug 1944 | Capture of Tinian provides a major base for B-29 operations in range of Japan. |
| 9–10 Mar 1945 | Tokyo firebombing demonstrates the scale of strategic air attack against Japanese cities. |
| 1 Apr 1945 | Battle of Okinawa begins, highlighting expected costs of a Japan invasion. |
| 16 Jul 1945 | Trinity test successfully detonates the first atomic device. |
| 26 Jul 1945 | Potsdam Declaration calls on Japan to surrender; Japanese leaders do not accept the terms. |
| 6 Aug 1945 | Atomic bomb dropped on Hiroshima. |
