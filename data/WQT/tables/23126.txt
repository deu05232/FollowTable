# Dietary Fiber  Definitions and Classifications (No Food List)

| Section | Term | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Definition | Dietary fiber | Non-digestible carbohydrates intrinsic to plants that resist digestion in the small intestine. |
| Definition | Functional fiber | Isolated or synthesized non-digestible carbohydrates with physiological benefits. |
| Classification | Soluble fiber | Forms gels; may help lower LDL cholesterol and improve glycemic control. |
| Classification | Insoluble fiber | Increases stool bulk; may improve laxation and transit time. |
| Measurement | AOAC methods | Enzymatic-gravimetric methods commonly used to quantify total dietary fiber. |
| Daily intake | General guideline | Many adults fall short of recommended intake; targets vary by age/sex. |
| Labeling | Nutrition Facts | Fiber listed in grams per serving on packaged foods, but values differ by product. |
