# Common Garden Pests and Suggested Companion Plants (Summary)

| Pest | Signs of Damage | Plants Said to Help | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mosquitoes | Biting nuisance near patios | Lemongrass, Lemon thyme, Basil | Often mentioned for scent-based deterrence |
| Aphids | Curled leaves, sticky honeydew | Chives, Nasturtium, Marigold | Companion planting frequently recommended |
| Carrot fly | Tunnels in carrot roots | Lettuce, Sage, Rosemary | Used as intercrop/companion barrier |
| Whiteflies | Tiny white insects on leaf undersides | Basil, Mint, Marigold | Strong-scent herbs commonly suggested |
| Slugs & snails | Ragged holes, slime trails | Lavender, Rosemary, Sage | Texture/scent sometimes cited |
| Japanese beetle | Skeletonized leaves | Chives, Garlic, Tansy | Mixed evidence; sometimes used near roses |
| Cabbage moth | Holes in brassica leaves | Thyme, Sage, Mint | Used around brassicas |
| Ants | Ant trails near beds | Mint, Tansy | Sometimes claimed to deter trails |
| Rabbits | Chewed seedlings | Marigold, Lavender, Sage | Often listed in “deterrent plants” articles |
