# Military Medical Retirement vs. Disability Severance — What the Pay is Based On (Not VA Rate Tables)

| Benefit / plan name | Trigger / eligibility concept | Pay basis used (the "base") | Main computation driver | How it differs from VA disability compensation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Permanent Disability Retirement (PDRL) | Found unfit with stable condition; qualifies for permanent medical retirement | Final basic pay or High-36 average (depends on member’s retired pay plan) | Higher of: (disability % × retired pay base) OR (service multiplier × retired pay base), subject to statutory caps | VA compensation is a separate benefit; this is a DoD retired pay calculation |
| Temporary Disability Retirement (TDRL) | Found unfit; condition not stable and placed on temporary list | Final basic pay or High-36 average (depends on member’s plan) | Disability % (subject to minimum/maximum rules while on TDRL) × retired pay base; later reevaluated | Not a VA rate table; it uses retired pay base plus disability percentage |
| Disability Severance Pay (Medical Separation) | Unfit but does not qualify for retirement; separated with severance | Monthly basic pay at separation (not an average-of-36 formula in most cases) | Years of service (capped) × 2 × monthly basic pay (conceptual structure) | One-time payment; VA compensation is monthly and based on VA ratings/dependents |
| Length-of-Service Retirement (Regular/Active) | Reaches retirement eligibility via years of service | Final basic pay or High-36 average (depending on retirement plan) | Service multiplier (e.g., 2.5% or 2.0% per year) × retired pay base | Pure longevity retirement; VA compensation may be concurrent/offset depending on rules |
| Combat-Related Special Compensation (CRSC) | Retiree with combat-related disabilities approved for CRSC | Not based on retired pay base alone; calculated as a restoration up to limits | Determined from retired pay and approved combat-related VA-rated conditions (restores waived retired pay up to cap) | Not a VA rate table; it is a DoD special compensation tied to waived retired pay |
| Concurrent Retirement and Disability Pay (CRDP) | Eligible retirees with VA-rated disability (typically 50%+) | Uses longevity retired pay amount as the foundation for restoration | Restores retired pay previously offset by VA compensation (phased rules vary by law) | Separate from VA tables; focuses on restoring retirement pay rather than rating-by-dependent amounts |
