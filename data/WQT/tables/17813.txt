# Narrative Overview of Military Retirement and Disability Programs (Long-Form Descriptions)

| Program | Description | Eligibility Snapshot |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Final Pay Retirement | This approach calculates retirement pay using the member’s final basic pay at separation/retirement, which can be advantageous for those with pay increases late in their career and is generally associated with older entry cohorts. The exact multiplier depends on years of service and statutory rules. | Typically legacy cohorts with specified entry dates |
| High-36 Retirement | This method uses an average of the highest 36 months of basic pay and applies a service-based multiplier. It is often described as smoothing year-to-year pay changes and is common for many retirees under modern legacy systems. | Standard legacy system for many members |
| Disability Retirement | Disability retirement can involve complex determinations including medical evaluation, disability ratings, and service connection; payments may be computed under different formulas and caps depending on circumstances, offsets, and whether the member qualifies under length-of-service rules. | Medically retired members |
| VA Disability Compensation | VA compensation is a tax-free benefit tied to disability rating levels and can be adjusted for dependents; it is not calculated as a percentage of current active-duty base pay and may interact with other benefits in special cases. | Veterans with service-connected disabilities |
| Concurrent Receipt Programs | Several policies govern how retirement pay and VA compensation can be received concurrently; details vary depending on disability rating thresholds, combat-related criteria, and length-of-service retirement status, and they may restore some or all retired pay that would otherwise be offset. | Retirees meeting statutory criteria |
| Survivor Benefits | Survivor benefits may provide income streams to eligible beneficiaries following a member’s death, with elections and coverage levels affecting premiums and payout amounts; interactions with other programs can be significant and depend on the member’s choices. | Eligible survivors of members/retirees |
