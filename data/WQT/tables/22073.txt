| Snake (common name) | Scientific name | Typical adult length (cm) | LD50 (mg/kg, mouse, SC) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Eastern diamondback rattlesnake | Crotalus adamanteus | 120–180 | 1.2 | Large-bodied pit viper |
| Mojave rattlesnake | Crotalus scutulatus | 80–120 | 0.2 | Neurotoxic components reported |
| Copperhead | Agkistrodon contortrix | 60–90 | 10.0 | Often considered less potent |
| Cottonmouth | Agkistrodon piscivorus | 80–120 | 2.0 | Semi-aquatic pit viper |
| Coral snake | Micrurus fulvius | 50–80 | 1.3 | Elapid; fixed fangs |
| Timber rattlesnake | Crotalus horridus | 90–150 | 1.5 | Variable coloration |
| Prairie rattlesnake | Crotalus viridis | 80–120 | 1.9 | Widespread in plains |
| Massasauga | Sistrurus catenatus | 50–80 | 2.5 | Small pit viper |
