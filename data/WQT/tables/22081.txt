# Venomous snakes in Florida by region within the state

| Species | Common name | Where in Florida it occurs |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Crotalus adamanteus | Eastern diamondback rattlesnake | Many parts of the state, especially coastal pine flatwoods and scrub |
| Crotalus horridus | Timber rattlesnake | More common in northern Florida and panhandle forested areas |
| Agkistrodon piscivorus | Cottonmouth | Widespread near wetlands and freshwater across much of the state |
| Agkistrodon contortrix | Copperhead | Mostly the panhandle and far north; limited farther south |
| Micrurus fulvius | Eastern coral snake | Patchy statewide; often in sandy soils and woodland edges |
