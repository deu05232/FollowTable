# CN III Branches and Target Regions (Grouped, Not Muscle-by-Muscle)

| Nerve division | Broad target region | Included muscles/structures (listed as a group) | Parasympathetic association | Practical note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CN III superior division | Superior orbit | Levator palpebrae superioris; Superior rectus | null | Often affected in partial III palsy |
| CN III inferior division | Inferior/medial orbit | Medial rectus; Inferior rectus; Inferior oblique | To ciliary ganglion via short ciliary nerves | May present with pupil involvement |
| CN IV (single trunk) | Superomedial orbit | Superior oblique | null | Long intracranial course |
| CN VI (single trunk) | Lateral orbit | Lateral rectus | null | Vulnerable to raised ICP |
| Ciliary ganglion (postganglionic) | Posterior orbit | Sphincter pupillae; Ciliary muscle | From CN III (EW) | Explains pupil/accommodation signs |
| V1 (sensory, orbital) | Orbit/forehead | Cornea; upper eyelid; dorsum of nose | null | Afferent limb of corneal reflex |
| Sympathetic fibers (carotid plexus) | Orbit | Dilator pupillae (via long ciliary) | Sympathetic | Horner syndrome context |
| Extraocular motor set (summary) | Orbit | “Most muscles by III, SO by IV, LR by VI” | — | Mnemonic-level, not per-muscle detail |
