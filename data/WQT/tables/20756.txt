# Autonomic Neurotransmitters and Typical Effector Responses (Sympathetic vs Parasympathetic)

| Division | Neurotransmitter | Typical target organ | Common effect (summary) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sympathetic | Norepinephrine | Heart | Increased rate and contractility | “Adrenergic” response |
| Sympathetic | Norepinephrine | Blood vessels (skin/viscera) | Vasoconstriction | 
| Sympathetic | Epinephrine (circulating) | Bronchi | Bronchodilation | From adrenal medulla |
| Parasympathetic | Acetylcholine | GI tract | Increased motility and secretion | “Cholinergic” response |
| Parasympathetic | Acetylcholine | Eye | Pupillary constriction | 
| Parasympathetic | Acetylcholine | Bladder | Detrusor contraction | 
| Sympathetic | Dopamine | Kidney | Increased renal blood flow | Selected vascular beds |
| Enteric (often grouped) | Nitric oxide | GI sphincters | Smooth muscle relaxation | NANC transmitter |
