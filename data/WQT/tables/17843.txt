# U.S. gubernatorial term-limit policies by state (selected states) — consecutive vs total-term caps

| State | Rule type | Terms allowed | Consecutive requirement | Can serve again after a break? | Brief policy statement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Alabama | Consecutive cap | 2 | Yes (max consecutive) | Yes | Limited to two consecutive terms |
| Florida | Consecutive cap | 2 | Yes (max consecutive) | Yes | Two consecutive terms, then must sit out |
| Georgia | Consecutive cap | 2 | Yes (max consecutive) | Yes | Two consecutive terms permitted |
| Colorado | Consecutive cap | 2 | Yes (max consecutive) | Yes | Two consecutive terms permitted |
| Delaware | Total-term cap | 2 | No (cap is total terms) | No (once cap reached) | Limited to two terms overall |
| Michigan | Total-term cap | 2 | No (cap is total terms) | No (once cap reached) | Limited to two terms overall |
