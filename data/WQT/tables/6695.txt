| Blue Streak | 1938 | An Edward Vettel wooden coaster that still utilizes its original rolling stock and braking system. It is one of two Vettel shallow track coasters still standing, and is the sixth oldest wooden coaster in the country. [23] The park replaced the coaster's original trains with new Century Flyer trains built by National Amusements Devices in 1966. The original lighter trains were rebuilt and placed back on the ride in 2002 to reduce maintenance costs. When the ride reopened in 2010, the NAD trains were returned to service due to insurance demands and cost to retrofit the original trains with modern restraints. | Blue Streak |
| Little Dipper | 1950s | An Allan Herschell kiddie coaster that still has its original cars. | Little Dipper |
| Devil's Den | 1968 | A Pretzel Manufacturing gravity powered dark ride which is one of two left in operation, the other one being Hawnted House [24] at Camden Park in West Virginia. It is considered a roller coaster since it has a lift hill, a drop, and the whole ride is based on gravity. | Devil's Den |
