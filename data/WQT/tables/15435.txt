# Yoga Pose Names with Mythology Philosophy Background Emphasis

| English Pose Name | Sanskrit Name | Associated Figure/Story | Extended Background (long) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sage Marichi Pose | Marichyasana | Marichi (Vedic sage) | Named for the sage Marichi; often introduced alongside stories of lineage and symbolism, emphasizing the contemplative aspect of naming beyond the physical form. |
| Dancer Pose | Natarajasana | Shiva as Nataraja | Connected to the cosmic dance of Shiva; frequently used to discuss themes of balance between creation and dissolution rather than just physical alignment. |
| Monkey Pose | Hanumanasana | Hanuman | Refers to Hanuman’s leap; commonly taught with devotional framing and moral lessons about perseverance and humility. |
| Fish Pose | Matsyasana | Vishnu’s fish avatar | Linked to the Matsya avatar narrative; background discussions may include protection, renewal, and mythic cosmology. |
| Eagle Pose | Garudasana | Garuda | Garuda’s symbolism is sometimes used to elaborate on overcoming obstacles and the role of faith in practice. |
| Lotus Pose | Padmasana | Lotus symbolism | Often presented with philosophical commentary on purity and detachment, extending beyond a simple bilingual name mapping. |
| Cow Face Pose | Gomukhasana | Sacred cow imagery | Frequently accompanied by cultural symbolism discussions, shifting the focus from mere nomenclature to interpretive meaning. |
