# Non-Antibiotic Medications Often Discussed Alongside Prostatitis Symptoms

| Drug | Class | Symptom target | Typical schedule (example) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tamsulosin | Alpha-1 blocker | Voiding symptoms | 0.4 mg once daily | BPH-like LUTS relief |
| Alfuzosin | Alpha-1 blocker | Voiding symptoms | 10 mg once daily | Take with food |
| Ibuprofen | NSAID | Pain/inflammation | 400 mg every 6-8 hours PRN | GI risk |
| Naproxen | NSAID | Pain/inflammation | 250-500 mg twice daily | Renal caution |
| Pregabalin | Neuropathic pain agent | Pelvic pain | 75 mg twice daily | Sedation |
| Finasteride | 5-alpha reductase inhibitor | Prostate size-related LUTS | 5 mg once daily | Not an antibiotic |
| Phenazopyridine | Urinary analgesic | Dysuria | 200 mg three times daily for up to 2 days | Urine discoloration |
| Oxybutynin | Antimuscarinic | Urgency/frequency | 5 mg 2-3 times daily | Anticholinergic effects |
