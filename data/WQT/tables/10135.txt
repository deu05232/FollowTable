# CN III, IV, VI  Extraocular Muscle Innervation + Lesion Findings

| Cranial nerve | Extraocular muscles innervated | Typical resting eye position with lesion | Key bedside finding | Common cause examples |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CN III (Oculomotor) | Superior rectus; Inferior rectus; Medial rectus; Inferior oblique | "Down and out" | Ptosis; diplopia | Aneurysm; diabetes |
| CN III (parasympathetic fibers) | (No extraocular muscle) | — | Dilated pupil; loss of light reflex | Posterior communicating artery aneurysm |
| CN IV (Trochlear) | Superior oblique | Slight hypertropia | Worse going downstairs | Trauma |
| CN VI (Abducens) | Lateral rectus | Medial deviation | Cannot abduct | Elevated intracranial pressure |
| Combined III/IV/VI | Multiple EOMs | Variable ophthalmoplegia | Multiple gaze deficits | Cavernous sinus syndrome |
| Internuclear ophthalmoplegia (MLF) | (Not a cranial nerve) | Adduction deficit | Dissociated nystagmus | Multiple sclerosis |
