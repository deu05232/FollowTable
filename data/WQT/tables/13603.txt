# Dietary Fiber  Definitions, Types, and Food Sources (No Per‑Serving Gram Values)

| Section | Item | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Definition | Dietary fiber | Non-digestible carbohydrates and lignin intrinsic to plants |
| Types | Soluble fiber | Forms gels; found in oats, barley, legumes |
| Types | Insoluble fiber | Adds bulk; found in wheat bran, many vegetables |
| Health | Satiety | May help people feel full longer |
| Health | Lipids | Can help lower LDL cholesterol in some diets |
| Sources | Fruits | Often provide fiber along with water and micronutrients |
| Sources | Whole grains | Commonly higher fiber than refined grains |
| Sources | Legumes | Typically among the richest whole-food sources |
| Meal idea | Swap | Choose whole grains over refined grains most of the time |
