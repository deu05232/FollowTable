# Diplomacy and Surrender Signals Before Hiroshima (High-level Summary)

| Timeframe | Actor | Message/Action | Channel | Relevance to Hiroshima |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1943 | Allied leadership | Agreement on unconditional surrender principle | Summit diplomacy | Frames later surrender terms |
| Late 1944 | Japan | Internal debate on war termination | Cabinet deliberations | Shows division, no exact date |
| Early 1945 | United States | Planning for invasion vs. alternatives | Military-policy discussions | Context for escalation |
| May 1945 | Allied powers | European war ends; resources shift | Public announcements | Enables focus on Pacific |
| Summer 1945 | United States | Finalization of ultimatum language | Drafting process | Precedes Potsdam |
| July 1945 | Japan | Mixed responses to surrender signals | Diplomatic backchannels | Ambiguity persists |
| August 1945 | United States | Decision executed to use atomic weapon | Command authorization | Immediate pre-strike phase |
