# Autonomic Pharmacology  Receptor Subtypes and Typical Physiologic Effects

| Receptor | Common location | Typical agonist example | Typical effect | Clinical note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Alpha-1 | Vascular smooth muscle | Phenylephrine | Vasoconstriction, ↑BP | Decongestants |
| Alpha-2 | Presynaptic terminals | Clonidine | Decreases transmitter release | Antihypertensive |
| Beta-1 | Heart | Dobutamine | ↑HR, ↑contractility | Inotrope |
| Beta-2 | Bronchi, uterus | Albuterol | Bronchodilation, uterine relaxation | Asthma therapy |
| Beta-3 | Adipose, bladder | Mirabegron | Lipolysis, detrusor relaxation | Overactive bladder |
| Muscarinic M2 | SA/AV node | Muscarine (conceptual) | ↓HR, ↓AV conduction | Vagal-like effects |
| Muscarinic M3 | Glands, smooth muscle | Bethanechol | ↑Secretions, smooth muscle contraction | Urinary retention |
| Nicotinic (Nn) | Autonomic ganglia | Nicotine | Ganglionic depolarization | Autonomic stimulation |
