# Cat Breeds  Expected Lifespan and Common Hereditary Disorders

| Breed/category | Expected lifespan (years) | High-risk hereditary condition | Typical clinical signs | Screening/notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Persian | 10–15 | PKD | Increased thirst, weight loss | Ultrasound screening recommended |
| Maine Coon | 10–13 | HCM | Exercise intolerance, murmur | Echocardiogram in breeding lines |
| Siamese | 12–16 | Amyloidosis | Chronic diarrhea, weight loss | Monitor GI and liver/kidney markers |
| Bengal | 12–16 | PRA | Night blindness | Genetic testing available |
| Scottish Fold | 11–14 | Osteochondrodysplasia | Stiff gait, pain | Avoid affected breeding; pain control |
| Sphynx | 8–14 | HCM | Rapid breathing, weakness | Regular cardiac screening |
| Mixed-breed (domestic shorthair) | 12–18 | Lower inherited risk (varies) | Varies by individual | Generally robust; preventive care matters |
