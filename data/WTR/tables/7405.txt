# Zoonotic Diseases  Animal Reservoirs and Routes of Transmission

| Disease | Main animal reservoir(s) | Human transmission route | Typical setting | Key control measure | Vaccine available |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rabies | Dogs, bats | Bites/saliva | Stray dog exposure, caves | Post-exposure prophylaxis | Yes |
| Anthrax | Cattle, sheep, goats | Spores via skin/inhalation/ingestion | Handling hides/wool | PPE, livestock vaccination | Limited (high-risk) |
| Leptospirosis | Rodents, dogs | Contact with contaminated urine/water | Flooding, farms | Rodent control, protective gear | Some regions |
| Brucellosis | Goats, cattle | Unpasteurized dairy, animal contact | Farms, slaughterhouses | Pasteurization | Animal vaccines |
| Avian influenza (H5/H7) | Poultry, wild birds | Close contact, aerosols | Live bird markets | Biosecurity, culling | Strain-specific |
| Plague | Rodents | Flea bites | Rural endemic foci | Flea control | No (widely) |
| Toxoplasmosis | Cats | Oocysts in soil/food | Gardening, undercooked meat | Food hygiene | No |
| Q fever | Sheep, goats, cattle | Aerosols from birth products | Farms | Hygiene at birthing | Yes (limited) |
