| Interesting facts about the asteroid or its discoverer | Telescope mounted through the roof of his house Gave Carl Friedrich Gauss (see "The Lost is Found") the honor of naming the sixth asteroid. | By 1854, Hind had discovered eight more asteroids. He worked at G. Bishop's private observatory "South Villa" at Regents Park. | The Markree Castle Observatory was owned by Edward Jonathan Cooper, who was too busy to observe on the night that Graham discovered Metis, the only asteroid found from an Irish observatory. | Hygiea is the fourth largest asteroid after Ceres, Pallas, and Vesta. Even though Hygiea is larger than the "Principal Four," its albedo is only 0.07. De Gasparis discovered a total of seven asteroids. | Luther was one of the few astronomers who devoted himself almost exclusively to searching for new asteroids. He found his 24th asteroid by February 20, 1890. | Working in his living room above the Café Procope in Paris, he was the first astronomer to discover two asteroids on the same night. By May 5, 1861, he had identified 14 new asteroids. | Peters' first discovery was made six weeks after the start of the Civil War. He was the second astronomer to find two asteroids in a single night. In two decades of searching, he found a total of 48 asteroids. | Watson found the 100th asteroid in 1868, one of six that he discovered that year. In 1874, while in Peking, he made the first asteroid discovery from China. Watson made a total of 22 asteroid discoveries. | Between 1874 and 1923, Palisa discovered a total of 122 asteroids-28 of them at the Pola Marine Observatory, and the rest at the Vienna Observatory. |
