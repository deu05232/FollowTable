# Prescription Pain Medications  Generic Name vs. Brand Name (Cross-Check Table)

| Pain medicine (generic) | Brand name(s) | Drug class | Notes for recognition |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tramadol | Ultram, ConZip | Opioid-like analgesic | Often labeled as a controlled pain medicine |
| Hydrocodone + Acetaminophen | Norco, Vicodin | Opioid combination | Combination product; label often shows both ingredients |
| Oxycodone | Roxicodone, OxyContin | Opioid | Immediate-release vs extended-release brand difference |
| Oxycodone + Acetaminophen | Percocet | Opioid combination | Common post-surgical pain prescription |
| Morphine sulfate | MS Contin, Kadian | Opioid | ER products have distinct brand names |
| Hydromorphone | Dilaudid | Opioid | Higher potency opioid; name often confused with morphine |
| Fentanyl (transdermal) | Duragesic | Opioid | Frequently supplied as patches |
| Buprenorphine (transdermal) | Butrans | Opioid (partial agonist) | Patch form used for chronic pain |
| Celecoxib | Celebrex | NSAID (COX-2 selective) | Non-opioid prescription anti-inflammatory |
| Ketorolac | Toradol | NSAID | Commonly used short-term for acute pain |
| Sumatriptan | Imitrex | Triptan (migraine) | Migraine-specific acute pain medicine |
