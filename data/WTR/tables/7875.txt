# Migraine Acute Therapy Options (Including Opioids and Anti-Nausea Agents)

| Therapy Group | Example Drugs (Generic) | Example Brands | Primary Role in Migraine Care | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Triptans | Sumatriptan, Rizatriptan | Imitrex, Maxalt | Abort migraine via serotonin agonism | Not general-purpose analgesics |
| Ergot derivatives | Dihydroergotamine | D.H.E. 45 | Abort migraine | Vascular contraindications |
| Antiemetics/Neuroleptics | Metoclopramide, Prochlorperazine | Reglan, Compazine | Treat nausea; sometimes headache relief | Sedation/EPS risk |
| NSAIDs (non-opioid) | Ketorolac, Diclofenac | Toradol, Voltaren | Pain relief | GI/renal risk |
| Opioids | Meperidine, Tramadol, Hydrocodone | Demerol, Ultram, Norco | Rescue therapy (some settings) | Dependence/overuse risk |
| Adjuncts | Diphenhydramine, Dexamethasone | Benadryl, Decadron | Reduce side effects/recurrence | Not analgesics |
| CGRP antagonists | Ubrogepant, Rimegepant | Ubrelvy, Nurtec ODT | Migraine-specific acute relief | Not NSAIDs |
| 5-HT1F agonist | Lasmiditan | Reyvow | Migraine-specific acute relief | Driving impairment warning |
