# Antimicrobial Coverage by Organism (Organism-Centered Map of Active Agents)

| Organism | Usual Setting | Typically Active Agents | Common Resistance Notes | Preferred When | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| MRSA | Skin/soft tissue, hospital | Vancomycin, Linezolid, Daptomycin, TMP-SMX | Inducible clinda resistance possible | Severe infections: IV agents | Not all agents for pneumonia (e.g., daptomycin inactive) |
| MSSA | Skin/soft tissue | Nafcillin/Oxacillin, Cefazolin | Beta-lactamase common | Bacteremia: beta-lactams | Avoid vancomycin if MSSA confirmed |
| Pseudomonas aeruginosa | Hospital/ICU | Cefepime, Piperacillin-tazobactam, Meropenem, Ciprofloxacin | Rapid resistance emergence | High-risk HAP/VAP | Often needs combination initially |
| ESBL-producing Enterobacterales | Complicated UTI, sepsis | Meropenem, Ertapenem | Resistant to many cephalosporins | Severe invasive disease | De-escalate if susceptible options exist |
| Streptococcus pneumoniae | CAP, otitis | Amoxicillin, Ceftriaxone, Levofloxacin | Macrolide resistance varies | CAP with typical features | Dose matters for penicillin nonsusceptibility |
| Candida albicans | Candidemia, mucosal | Fluconazole, Echinocandins | Non-albicans may resist azoles | Stable pts: fluconazole | Source control and duration depend on site |
| Neisseria gonorrhoeae | STI | Ceftriaxone | Rising resistance to older agents | Uncomplicated gonorrhea | Treat partners; test of cure in some cases |
| Mycobacterium tuberculosis | Pulmonary TB | Isoniazid, Rifampin, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol | MDR strains require second-line | Drug-susceptible TB | Requires multi-drug regimen |
