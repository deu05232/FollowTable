# Plant Hormones  Key Effects on Growth, Ripening, and Stress Responses

| Hormone | Typical site of production | Main process affected | Function / effect (plain language) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Auxins (IAA) | Shoot tips, young leaves, developing seeds | Cell elongation, tropisms | Drives stem elongation; helps plants bend toward light and regulate root/shoot patterning. |
| Gibberellins (GA) | Young tissues, seeds | Germination, stem growth | Breaks seed dormancy and promotes germination; lengthens stems and increases growth. |
| Cytokinins | Roots (transported upward), developing tissues | Cell division, aging | Stimulates cell division and shoot formation; delays leaf aging (senescence). |
| Abscisic acid (ABA) | Leaves and roots (especially under stress) | Water stress, dormancy | Closes stomata to reduce water loss; promotes seed dormancy during unfavorable conditions. |
| Ethylene | Ripening fruits, stressed tissues | Ripening, abscission | Promotes fruit ripening; triggers leaf/fruit drop and coordinates stress responses. |
| Brassinosteroids | Many tissues (shoots, pollen, seeds) | Growth, vascular development | Enhances cell expansion and division; supports vascular tissue development and stress tolerance. |
| Jasmonates (JA) | Wounded tissues, leaves | Defense responses | Activates defense against insects and mechanical damage; can slow growth during attack. |
| Salicylic acid (SA) | Leaves (especially during infection) | Disease resistance | Coordinates defense against pathogens and helps establish systemic resistance. |
