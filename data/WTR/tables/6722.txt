# Comparative Overview of Major World Religions (Origins, Beliefs, Followers, Branches, Symbols)

| Religion | Origin period (approx.) | Origin region | Core beliefs (high-level) | Approx. followers (global) | Major branches / denominations | Common symbols |
|---|---|---|---|---:|---|---|
| Christianity | 1st century CE | Levant / Roman Judea | Jesus as Messiah; salvation; Bible; Trinity (most traditions) | 2.3 billion | Catholic; Eastern Orthodox; Protestant (e.g., Anglican, Lutheran, Baptist); Pentecostal | Cross; Ichthys (fish) |
| Islam | 7th century CE | Arabian Peninsula (Mecca/Medina) | One God (Allah); Qur'an; Muhammad as prophet; Five Pillars | 2.0 billion | Sunni; Shia; Ibadi; Sufism (mystical traditions across branches) | Crescent and star (cultural); calligraphy |
| Hinduism | 2nd millennium BCE–present (layered) | Indian subcontinent | Dharma/karma; samsara; moksha; diverse deities and philosophies | 1.2 billion | Vaishnavism; Shaivism; Shaktism; Smarta | Om (Aum); lotus |
| Buddhism | 5th century BCE | Northern India / Nepal region | Four Noble Truths; Eightfold Path; enlightenment (nirvana) | 500 million | Theravada; Mahayana; Vajrayana | Dharma wheel; lotus |
| Sikhism | 15th–16th centuries CE | Punjab (South Asia) | One God; teachings of the Gurus; equality; seva (service) | 25 million | Khalsa tradition; major gurdwara-based schools (no centralized denominations) | Khanda; Ik Onkar |
| Judaism | 2nd–1st millennium BCE (formation) | Levant | Covenant with God; Torah; ethical monotheism | 15 million | Orthodox; Conservative; Reform; Reconstructionist | Star of David; Menorah |
| Chinese folk religion / Traditional Chinese religion | Ancient–present (syncretic) | China | Ancestor veneration; local deities; ritual practice; harmony | 400 million | Regional traditions; often blends with Taoism/Buddhism | Incense; ancestral tablets |
| Taoism (Daoism) | 4th–3rd century BCE (classical) | China | Dao as ultimate principle; wu-wei; harmony with nature | 12 million | Zhengyi; Quanzhen; temple-based lineages | Taijitu (yin-yang); bagua |
| Shinto | Ancient–present | Japan | Kami (spirits); ritual purity; shrine worship | 100 million | Shrine Shinto; sect Shinto movements | Torii gate; shimenawa |
| Baháʼí Faith | 19th century CE | Persia (Iran) | Unity of God/religions/humanity; progressive revelation | 7 million | (No major denominations; administrative order-based community) | Nine-pointed star; ringstone symbol |
