# Emergency Department Acute Migraine Medication Pathways (Mixed Classes)

| Step | Medication class | Generic examples | Common brand names | Primary role in regimen |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | NSAIDs | ketorolac; diclofenac; ibuprofen | Toradol; Voltaren; Advil | Pain and inflammation relief |
| 1 | Triptans | sumatriptan; rizatriptan | Imitrex; Maxalt | Abort migraine via serotonergic vasoconstriction |
| 1 | Antiemetics (dopamine antagonists) | metoclopramide; prochlorperazine | Reglan; Compazine | Treat nausea; can help headache |
| 2 | Opioids | tramadol; hydrocodone/acetaminophen; oxycodone | Ultram; Vicodin; OxyContin | Rescue analgesia when others fail |
| 2 | Corticosteroids | dexamethasone; prednisone | Decadron; Deltasone | Reduce recurrence/inflammation |
| 3 | Ergot derivatives | dihydroergotamine | D.H.E. 45 | Refractory migraine abortive |
| 3 | Adjuncts | magnesium sulfate; IV fluids | (various) | Supportive care in ED |
| Avoid/Use with caution | Anticoagulants/antiplatelets | warfarin; clopidogrel | Coumadin; Plavix | Not analgesics; bleeding-risk context |
