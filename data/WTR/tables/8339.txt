# Emergency Department Migraine Treatment Options (Narrative Comparison)

| Treatment Group | Representative Medicines (mixed naming) | How It Works (summary) | Common Side Effects | Practical Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Antiemetic neuroleptics | metoclopramide, prochlorperazine, chlorpromazine | reduces nausea and may help headache pathways | restlessness, drowsiness | often given IV |
| Triptans | Imitrex, sumatriptan | constricts cranial vessels | chest tightness, tingling | avoid in some heart conditions |
| NSAIDs | ketorolac, Toradol | blocks prostaglandins to reduce pain/inflammation | GI upset, kidney risk | Toradol is commonly used IV |
| Opioids | morphine, hydromorphone | opioid receptor agonists for analgesia | nausea, sedation, dependence | generally discouraged for migraine |
| Ergots | D.H.E. 45 | vasoconstriction and serotonin effects | nausea, hypertension | contraindicated in pregnancy |
| Adjuncts | magnesium, fluids | supportive therapy | flushing | depends on protocol |
| Discharge advice | rest, hydration | symptom management | — | follow-up if persistent |
