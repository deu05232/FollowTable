# Common Waterborne and Foodborne Illnesses and Their Exposure Routes

| Disease | Causative Agent | Main Exposure Route | Typical Source | Person-to-Person Spread? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cholera | Vibrio cholerae | Drinking contaminated water | Untreated water, sewage contamination | Possible |
| Typhoid fever | Salmonella Typhi | Food/water ingestion | Contaminated food, unsafe water | Yes |
| Hepatitis A | Hepatitis A virus | Fecal-oral | Contaminated food/water | Yes |
| Norovirus gastroenteritis | Norovirus | Fecal-oral | Food handling, contaminated surfaces | Very common |
| Giardiasis | Giardia lamblia | Drinking/swallowing contaminated water | Lakes, pools, childcare outbreaks | Yes |
| Cryptosporidiosis | Cryptosporidium spp. | Recreational water ingestion | Pools, water parks | Yes |
| Shigellosis | Shigella spp. | Fecal-oral | Daycare, poor sanitation | Yes |
| Legionellosis | Legionella pneumophila | Inhalation of aerosols | Cooling towers, plumbing systems | Rare |
