# Healthy Eating Outcomes by Diet Pattern (Effects Without Cost)

| Diet Pattern | Typical Foods | Reported Benefits | Potential Downsides | Who It May Suit | Evidence Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mediterranean-style | Olive oil, fish, legumes, vegetables | Improved heart markers | Requires cooking/variety | Families, long-term habits | Strong observational evidence |
| DASH-style | Fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy | Lower blood pressure | Can feel restrictive | People managing BP | Supported by trials |
| Plant-forward | Beans, whole grains, vegetables | Higher fiber intake | Protein planning needed | Budget-conscious cooks | Depends on food choices |
| Low-carb | Meat, eggs, non-starchy veg | Appetite control for some | Hard to sustain socially | People preferring fewer carbs | Mixed results |
| Whole-foods focused | Minimally processed foods | Less added sugar | Time-consuming prep | Meal-preppers | Lifestyle dependent |
| Gluten-free (non-celiac) | GF grains, alternatives | Subjective GI comfort | Risk of low fiber | Those with sensitivities | Evidence limited |
| Intermittent fasting | Time-restricted eating | Calorie reduction for some | Hunger/energy swings | People liking structure | Adherence varies |
| Keto | Very low carb, high fat | Rapid water-weight loss | Nutrient gaps possible | Short-term goals | Requires monitoring |
