# Antibiotic Susceptibility (MIC) Reference Ranges for Common Bacterial Pathogens

| Organism | Antibiotic | MIC (mcg/mL) susceptible | MIC intermediate | MIC resistant | Test method | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| Escherichia coli | Levofloxacin | ≤0.25 | 0.5 | ≥1 | Broth microdilution | UTI isolate guidance |
| Klebsiella pneumoniae | Ceftriaxone | ≤1 | 2 | ≥4 | Broth microdilution | ESBL screening recommended |
| Pseudomonas aeruginosa | Piperacillin-tazobactam | ≤16/4 | 32/4–64/4 | ≥128/4 | Broth microdilution | Consider local antibiogram |
| Staphylococcus aureus | Oxacillin | ≤2 | 4 | ≥8 | Broth microdilution | Detects MRSA when resistant |
| Streptococcus pneumoniae | Penicillin (non-meningitis) | ≤2 | 4 | ≥8 | Broth microdilution | Different breakpoints for meningitis |
| Enterococcus faecalis | Vancomycin | ≤4 | 8–16 | ≥32 | Broth microdilution | VRE if resistant |
| Haemophilus influenzae | Azithromycin | ≤4 | 8 | ≥16 | Broth microdilution | Beta-lactamase status matters |
| Neisseria gonorrhoeae | Cefixime | ≤0.25 | 0.5 | ≥1 | Agar dilution | Resistance surveillance needed |
