# Major Hominin Fossil Discoveries and Where They Were Found

| Fossil / Specimen | Species (common attribution) | Approx. age | Discovery year | Discovery location | Why it mattered (short note) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| “Lucy” (AL 288-1) | Australopithecus afarensis | ~3.2 million years | 1974 | Hadar, Ethiopia | Landmark partial skeleton used to infer bipedalism |
| “Ardi” (ARA-VP-6/500) | Ardipithecus ramidus | ~4.4 million years | 1994 | Aramis, Ethiopia | Early hominin anatomy with clues about locomotion and habitat |
| Turkana Boy (KNM-WT 15000) | Homo erectus / H. ergaster | ~1.6 million years | 1984 | Nariokotome, Kenya | Exceptional juvenile skeleton; growth and body plan |
| Laetoli footprints | Australopithecus (often linked to A. afarensis) | ~3.6 million years | 1978 | Laetoli, Tanzania | Direct evidence of habitual bipedal walking |
| “Mrs. Ples” (STS 5) | Australopithecus africanus | ~2.1–2.6 million years | 1947 | Sterkfontein, South Africa | Important skull for understanding australopith diversity |
| Homo naledi (Dinaledi Chamber remains) | Homo naledi | ~236–335 thousand years | 2013 | Rising Star Cave, South Africa | Unexpected late-surviving hominin with mosaic traits |
