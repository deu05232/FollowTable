# Laboratory Identification Clues for Selected Fungal Diseases (No Treatment Information)

| Fungal condition | Common organism(s) | Key specimen | Microscopy / culture clue | Typical clinical setting |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dermatophytosis (tinea) | Trichophyton spp., Microsporum spp. | Skin scraping | Septate hyphae on KOH | Athlete’s foot, ringworm |
| Vulvovaginal candidiasis | Candida albicans | Vaginal swab | Budding yeast, pseudohyphae | Pruritus, discharge |
| Oral thrush | Candida spp. | Oral scrape | Yeast forms in smear | Steroid inhaler use, immunosuppression |
| Aspergillosis | Aspergillus fumigatus | BAL fluid | Acute-angle branching septate hyphae | Neutropenia, lung disease |
| Cryptococcosis | Cryptococcus neoformans | CSF | India ink capsule; antigen positive | HIV/AIDS, transplant |
| Histoplasmosis | Histoplasma capsulatum | Urine antigen, tissue | Small intracellular yeasts | Ohio/Mississippi River valleys |
| Coccidioidomycosis | Coccidioides spp. | Sputum/tissue | Spherules with endospores | Desert Southwest |
| Sporotrichosis | Sporothrix schenckii | Tissue | Cigar-shaped yeasts | Rose gardener exposure |
