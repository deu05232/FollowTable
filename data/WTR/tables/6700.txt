# Perceived Cost of Healthy Eating by City (No Price Figures)

| City | Typical healthy options mentioned | Perceived affordability | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| New York City | Salad bars, poke bowls, organic groceries | Expensive | High rent and delivery fees increase the feeling of cost |
| Los Angeles | Juice shops, vegan cafes, farmer’s markets | Mixed | Plenty of options, but “premium” branding can raise costs |
| Chicago | Meal-prep, grocery chains, salad shops | Moderate | More mid-priced choices than coastal cities |
| Austin | Healthy fast-casual, meal kits | Moderate | Popularity makes some places feel “trendy” |
| Miami | Fresh seafood, smoothie bars | Expensive | Tourist areas skew costs upward |
| Seattle | Organic groceries, coffee shops with protein boxes | Expensive | Many people report healthy food feels pricey |
| Atlanta | Grill-and-bowl spots, supermarkets | Affordable | More budget-friendly sentiment outside downtown |
| Denver | Whole-food cafes, meal-prep services | Mixed | Depends heavily on neighborhood |
