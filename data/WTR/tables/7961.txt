# Ibanez Electric Guitars  How Key Design Choices Translate to Feel and Tone (Player-Focused Comparison)

| Ibanez family / typical examples | Core construction & hardware (feature) | Pickup/voicing direction (feature) | Neck & fretboard design (feature) | Real-world feel (impact) | Real-world sound (impact) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| RG (Superstrat) | Basswood or Nyatoh body; bolt-on; fixed bridge or double-locking trem | High-output H-H or H-S-H | Thin Wizard-style neck; flat-ish board | Fast, low-effort fretting; easy wide bends; trem versions feel “elastic” under the picking hand | Tight low end, quick attack; excels at articulate high-gain rhythms and cutting leads |
| S (Sabre) | Very thin carved body; bolt-on; usually fixed or trem | Medium-to-high output humbuckers | Slim neck; often 24 frets | Light on the shoulder; body feels “sleek”; less physical resistance when you dig in hard | Slightly more airy/lean than thicker bodies; clear top end; responsive to touch dynamics |
| AZ (Modern) | Alder/Basswood body; bolt-on; premium trem or fixed | Versatile H-H or H-S-H with coil-split options | Oval C / modern D feel; often roasted maple; more rounded edges | More substantial hand feel than super-thin necks; stable tuning/feel; comfortable for long sessions | Balanced EQ; strong note separation; convincing cleans and modern gain without getting harsh |
| RGA (Arched RG) | Arched-top RG-style; bolt-on; commonly fixed bridge | Often hotter ceramic-leaning sets | Wizard-ish neck; 24 frets | Similar speed to RG but the top contour sits nicer under the forearm | A touch more focused midrange “push”; punchy palm-mutes; lead notes feel more “forward” |
| Iron Label (metal-focused variants) | Typically basswood/nyatoh; minimal cosmetics; fixed bridge common | Very high output humbuckers | Thin, flat neck; jumbo frets | Built for low action and aggressive picking; can feel less forgiving if your touch is heavy | Immediate attack, compressed feel; tight under gain; less “spongy” bloom on clean tones |
| Prestige / Japanese-built lines (across RG/AZ/S) | Tighter tolerances; higher-grade hardware; refined fretwork | Higher clarity sets; often more dynamic | Smoother fret ends; more consistent neck carve | “Broken-in” feel out of the case; bends feel smoother; fewer dead spots perceptually | More dynamic range; clearer complex chords; better sustain consistency across the neck |
| Artcore / AS (semi-hollow) | Laminated/semi-hollow; set neck common; tune-o-matic style | Lower-to-medium output humbuckers | Rounder neck; shorter feel due to body geometry | Softer attack under the fingers; encourages lighter touch; very comfy seated | Warm mids, woody air; blooms on chords; can feed back earlier at high gain |
| RGD (down-tuned-oriented) | Longer scale; fixed bridge common | Modern high-output pickups | Wider string spacing feel; 24 frets | More string tension at pitch; clearer intonation; feels “tighter” for drop tunings | Better low-string definition; less flub; percussive, piano-like low notes |
