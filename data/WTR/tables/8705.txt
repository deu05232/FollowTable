# Selected Hominin Fossil Discoveries (Findings-Focused Timeline)

| Approx. fossil age (years ago) | Hominin / early human ancestor | Key specimen(s) / find name | Discovery year (reported) | Discovery location | What was found (type) | Brief scientific notes |
|---:|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| 3.66 million | *Australopithecus afarensis* | Laetoli footprints | 1978 | Laetoli, Tanzania | Trackway (fossil footprints) | Early evidence of habitual bipedal walking in a group context |
| 2.1 million | *Australopithecus sediba* | “Karabo” (MH1) & MH2 | 2008 | Malapa, South Africa | Partial skeletons | Mosaic of australopith and Homo-like traits; debated placement near early Homo |
| 1.98 million | *Paranthropus boisei* | OH 5 (“Zinj”) | 1959 | Olduvai Gorge, Tanzania | Cranium | Robust chewing anatomy; helped define robust australopith lineage |
| 1.6 million | *Homo erectus* | Nariokotome Boy (KNM-WT 15000) | 1984 | West Turkana, Kenya | Juvenile skeleton | Tall body proportions; important for growth and locomotion studies |
| 1.2–0.8 million | *Homo antecessor* | Gran Dolina TD6 remains | 1994 | Atapuerca, Spain | Multiple bones/teeth | Among earliest widely cited Homo in Western Europe; interpretation debated |
| 430,000 | *Homo heidelbergensis* (often applied) | Sima de los Huesos hominins | 1992 | Atapuerca, Spain | Large assemblage of bones | Key Middle Pleistocene population; linked to Neanderthal ancestry discussions |
| 60,000–50,000 | Denisovans (archaic humans) | Denisova 3 (finger bone) | 2008 | Denisova Cave, Siberia, Russia | Phalanx + later teeth | Identified largely via ancient DNA; revealed previously unknown hominin group |
| 40,000 | *Homo neanderthalensis* | Feldhofer Neanderthal 1 | 1856 | Neander Valley, Germany | Cranial and postcranial bones | First recognized Neanderthal fossils; foundational for paleoanthropology |
