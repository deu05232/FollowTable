# Empiric Antibiotic Regimens by Syndrome (Regimen-Focused, Not Single-Drug Rows)

| Clinical Syndrome | Severity/Setting | Empiric Regimen (Combination) | Add-on Coverage Triggers | De-escalation Notes | Typical Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sepsis of unknown source | ICU | Piperacillin-tazobactam + Vancomycin | Add antifungal if neutropenic | Narrow to culture results | 7–10 days |
| Hospital-acquired pneumonia | Inpatient | Cefepime + Vancomycin | Add MRSA/dual Pseudomonas if high risk | Stop MRSA agent if ruled out | 7 days |
| Intra-abdominal infection | Inpatient | Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole | Use piperacillin-tazobactam if severe | Source control critical | 4–7 days |
| Febrile neutropenia | Oncology | Cefepime (monotherapy) | Add vancomycin for catheter/skin signs | Adjust after ANC recovery | Varies |
| Diabetic foot infection | Outpatient vs inpatient | Amoxicillin-clavulanate OR (Vancomycin + Piperacillin-tazobactam) | Add anaerobe coverage if ischemic/necrotic | Tailor to deep culture | 1–2+ weeks |
| Bacterial meningitis (adult) | ED/ICU | Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin + Ampicillin | Add acyclovir if encephalitis suspected | Narrow once organism known | 7–21 days |
| Pelvic inflammatory disease | Outpatient | Ceftriaxone + Doxycycline + Metronidazole | Consider admission if severe | Partner treatment important | 14 days |
