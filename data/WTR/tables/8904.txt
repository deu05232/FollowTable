# Reproductive and Metabolic Hormones  Producers and Key Body Effects

| System | Hormone | Produced by | Key effect(s) in practical terms | Example outcome / measurable change |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Gonadal (female) | Estradiol (E2) | Ovaries (granulosa cells); also adipose (minor) | Supports endometrial growth; maintains bone density; influences secondary sex characteristics | Proliferative endometrium; improved bone mineral maintenance |
| Gonadal (female) | Progesterone | Ovaries (corpus luteum); placenta in pregnancy | Prepares and stabilizes endometrium; reduces uterine contractility; supports pregnancy | Secretory endometrium; decreased uterine tone |
| Gonadal (male) | Testosterone | Testes (Leydig cells) | Promotes spermatogenesis (with FSH); increases muscle mass and libido; supports secondary sex traits | Increased lean mass; androgen-dependent hair/voice changes |
| Pituitary-gonadal axis | LH (luteinizing hormone) | Anterior pituitary | Triggers ovulation and corpus luteum formation; stimulates testosterone production in Leydig cells | LH surge precedes ovulation; increased testicular testosterone |
| Pituitary-gonadal axis | FSH (follicle-stimulating hormone) | Anterior pituitary | Stimulates ovarian follicle development; stimulates Sertoli cells and spermatogenesis | Follicle maturation; increased sperm production markers |
| Pineal/circadian | Melatonin | Pineal gland | Synchronizes circadian rhythms; influences sleep timing | Earlier sleep onset; phase shifting of sleep-wake cycle |
| Calcium/bone | Calcitriol (1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D) | Kidney (activated form) | Increases intestinal calcium/phosphate absorption; supports bone mineralization | Increased Ca²⁺ absorption; higher serum calcium (with adequate intake) |
| Adrenal cortex | Cortisol | Adrenal cortex (zona fasciculata) | Stress response: increases glucose availability; anti-inflammatory; maintains vascular responsiveness | Increased gluconeogenesis; reduced inflammatory cytokine activity |
| Adipose | Leptin | Adipocytes | Signals energy sufficiency; suppresses appetite and supports normal reproductive axis | Reduced hunger signals; permissive effect on GnRH activity |
| Gut | Ghrelin | Stomach | Stimulates hunger; promotes GH release | Increased appetite; pre-meal rise in ghrelin |
| Kidney/blood | Erythropoietin (EPO) | Kidney interstitial cells | Stimulates red blood cell production in bone marrow | Increased reticulocyte count; rising hematocrit |
| Heart/volume | ANP (atrial natriuretic peptide) | Atrial cardiomyocytes | Promotes sodium excretion; lowers blood volume and blood pressure | Increased natriuresis; reduced BP |
