# Lunar Phase Names and Visual Appearance Guide (No timing included)

| Phase name | Illumination description | Typical sky appearance | Common viewing tip | Photography note | Common misconception |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| New Moon | 0% illuminated (not visible) | Near the Sun, hard to see | Best for stargazing (dark skies) | Use landscape/long exposure | “Moon disappears for days” |
| Waxing Crescent | Thin crescent after sunset | Low in western sky | Look shortly after sunset | Expose for earthshine | “Crescent equals morning only” |
| First Quarter | Half-lit, right side bright (N. Hem.) | High in afternoon/evening | Great for craters on terminator | Short exposures work | “Quarter means 1/4 visible” |
| Waxing Gibbous | More than half lit | Bright evening object | Good for maria contrast | Watch overexposure | “Gibbous is rare” |
| Full Moon | 100% illuminated | Rises near sunset | Best for casual viewing | Use low ISO to avoid blowout | “Full moon is best for detail” |
| Waning Gibbous | Bright late night/morning | Seen after midnight | Observe before dawn | Stable seeing often | “Waning means shrinking size” |
| Third Quarter | Half-lit, left side bright (N. Hem.) | Morning sky | Good for rugged terminator | Similar to first quarter | Confused with first quarter |
| Waning Crescent | Thin crescent before sunrise | Low in eastern sky | Look before sunrise | Capture earthshine | “Crescent always points up” |
