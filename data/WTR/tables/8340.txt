# OTC Pain Relief Categories and When to Use Them

| Category | Common Active Ingredients | Typical Uses | Notable Risks/Warnings | Example Products (not a pairing) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| NSAIDs | ibuprofen, naproxen | inflammation, muscle aches, menstrual cramps | stomach bleeding, kidney risk | "anti-inflammatory tablets" |
| Acetaminophen products | acetaminophen | headache, fever, general aches | liver toxicity with high dose/alcohol | "fever reducers" |
| Topical analgesics | menthol, capsaicin, lidocaine | localized joint/muscle pain | skin irritation, numbness | creams, patches |
| Opioid analgesics | oxycodone, hydrocodone | severe acute pain | dependence, respiratory depression | prescription pain pills |
| Adjuvant pain meds | gabapentin, amitriptyline | nerve pain | sedation, dizziness | neuropathic pain agents |
| Migraine-specific | sumatriptan, ergot derivatives | migraine attacks | cardiovascular cautions | triptans |
| Anti-inflammatory steroids | prednisone, methylprednisolone | inflammatory flares | immunosuppression, glucose rise | oral steroid packs |
