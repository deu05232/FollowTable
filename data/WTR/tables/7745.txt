# Symptom-Based Differential for Vaginal Discharge (Limited or No Antifungal Treatment Details)

| Presentation | Likely cause | Typical discharge | pH | Clue on exam | Common tests | Usual management focus |
|---|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Itching + thick white discharge | Vulvovaginal candidiasis | “Cottage cheese” | <4.5 | Erythema, excoriations | Wet mount, KOH | Confirm diagnosis; rule out mixed infection |
| Fishy odor + thin gray discharge | Bacterial vaginosis | Thin/gray | >4.5 | Clue cells | Wet mount, whiff test | Restore vaginal flora; recurrence prevention |
| Frothy yellow-green discharge | Trichomoniasis | Frothy | >4.5 | Strawberry cervix | NAAT | Treat patient and partners |
| Pelvic pain + fever | PID | Variable | Variable | Cervical motion tenderness | NAAT, pregnancy test | Urgent evaluation; avoid complications |
| Dysuria + minimal discharge | UTI/urethritis | Minimal | Variable | Suprapubic tenderness | Urinalysis/culture | Targeted antibacterial therapy |
| Vulvar ulcers | HSV | Minimal | Variable | Vesicles/ulcers | PCR | Antiviral therapy |
| Post-menopausal burning | Atrophic vaginitis | Watery | >5.0 | Pale mucosa | Clinical | Local estrogen consideration |
| Recent antibiotic use + burning | Irritant/chemical vaginitis | Variable | Variable | No pathogen seen | Exclusion | Remove irritant; supportive care |
