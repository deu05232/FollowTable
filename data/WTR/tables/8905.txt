# Reproductive hormones and their effects on the reproductive system

| Hormone | Key physiological effect | Typical source |
|---|---|---|
| Follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) | Stimulates follicle development and estrogen production | Pituitary |
| Luteinizing hormone (LH) | Triggers ovulation; supports corpus luteum formation | Pituitary |
| Estrogen | Promotes development of female secondary sex characteristics; supports endometrial growth | Ovaries |
| Progesterone | Prepares and maintains endometrium for pregnancy | Ovaries (corpus luteum) |
| Testosterone | Supports male secondary sex characteristics and spermatogenesis | Testes |
| Inhibin | Decreases FSH secretion | Gonads |
| Gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) | Stimulates LH/FSH release | Hypothalamus |
| Human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG) | Maintains corpus luteum early in pregnancy | Placenta |
