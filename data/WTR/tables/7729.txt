# Waterborne illness comparison including symptoms and incubation (clinical-heavy)

| Illness | How exposure typically occurs | Key symptoms profile | Typical incubation window |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Norovirus gastroenteritis | contaminated food, surfaces, close contact | vomiting, watery diarrhea, cramps | 12–48 hours |
| Hepatitis A | contaminated food or water | fatigue, nausea, jaundice | 15–50 days |
| Giardiasis | swallowing contaminated recreational water | greasy diarrhea, bloating | 1–2 weeks |
| Cholera | drinking contaminated water | profuse watery diarrhea, dehydration | hours–5 days |
