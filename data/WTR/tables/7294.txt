# Functional Outcomes of Hormone-Acting Drugs (Agonists Antagonists) on Physiology

| Compound (drug class) | Hormone pathway modulated | Functional outcomes in the organism (physiological effects) | Typical clinical/biological use-case (effect-focused) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tamoxifen (SERM) | Estrogen receptor modulator (antagonist in breast) | ↓ estrogen-driven cell proliferation; partial estrogen-like effects in some tissues | Reduce recurrence risk in ER+ breast cancer; modify estrogen-responsive growth |
| Fulvestrant (SERD) | Estrogen receptor antagonist/degrader | Blocks and degrades ER → ↓ estrogen signaling and ↓ hormone-dependent growth | Hormone-sensitive breast cancer after progression on other therapy |
| Leuprolide (GnRH agonist, continuous) | Suppresses LH/FSH after initial flare | ↓ gonadal steroid production (↓ testosterone/estradiol) → medical castration physiology | Prostate cancer; endometriosis; puberty suppression indications |
| Mifepristone (progesterone receptor antagonist) | Blocks progesterone action | Destabilizes endometrium; increases uterine contractility responsiveness | Medication abortion; progesterone-dependent conditions |
| Spironolactone (mineralocorticoid antagonist) | Blocks aldosterone receptor | ↑ sodium excretion, ↓ potassium excretion; ↓ volume/↓ blood pressure (diuretic effect) | Edema/heart failure; resistant hypertension; hyperaldosteronism |
| Desmopressin (ADH analog) | Activates vasopressin V2 receptor | ↑ renal water reabsorption → ↓ urine volume, ↑ urine osmolality | Central diabetes insipidus; nocturnal enuresis |
| Levothyroxine (T4 replacement) | Thyroid hormone replacement | ↑ basal metabolic rate, ↑ heart rate/contractility, improved energy/thermogenesis | Hypothyroidism treatment (restore metabolic effects) |
| Hydrocortisone/prednisone (glucocorticoids) | Mimic cortisol signaling | Anti-inflammatory/immunosuppressive effects; ↑ gluconeogenesis; potential HPA axis suppression | Autoimmune/inflammatory disorders; adrenal insufficiency replacement |
| Octreotide (somatostatin analog) | Inhibits GH and several peptide hormones | ↓ GH secretion; ↓ GI secretions and splanchnic blood flow | Acromegaly symptom control; hormone-secreting tumors |
| Sildenafil (PDE5 inhibitor; NO-cGMP enhancer) | Enhances NO-mediated signaling (hormone-like vascular pathway) | ↑ smooth muscle relaxation → ↑ penile blood flow; ↓ pulmonary vascular resistance | Erectile dysfunction; pulmonary hypertension |
