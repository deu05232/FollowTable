# Laboratory Identification and Susceptibility Testing Summary for Candida Species (Not a Therapy Table)

| Candida Species | Common Specimen | Key ID Test | Typical Microscopy Finding | Example MIC Endpoint Reported | Interpretation Note |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Candida albicans | Vaginal swab | Germ tube test | Budding yeast with pseudohyphae | Fluconazole MIC 0.25–1 mcg/mL | Often susceptible |
| Candida glabrata | Blood | MALDI-TOF | Small yeast, few pseudohyphae | Fluconazole MIC 16–64 mcg/mL | Reduced azole susceptibility common |
| Candida tropicalis | Blood | CHROMagar color | Yeast with pseudohyphae | Voriconazole MIC 0.03–0.5 mcg/mL | Variable by region |
| Candida parapsilosis | Catheter tip | Biochemical panel | Yeast clusters | Echinocandin MIC 0.5–4 mcg/mL | Higher echinocandin MICs possible |
| Candida krusei | Blood | MALDI-TOF | Elongated yeast | Fluconazole MIC intrinsic resistance | Consider non-fluconazole options |
| Candida auris | Swab/blood | PCR | Variable | Multiple drug class MICs elevated | Requires special infection control |
| Candida dubliniensis | Oral swab | Germ tube + sequencing | Similar to C. albicans | Fluconazole MIC 0.25–2 mcg/mL | MisID risk without confirmatory testing |
