# Migraine and Headache Pain Medications  Acute Relief vs Prevention

| Category | Medicine (generic) | Common brand examples | Headache/migraine pain use (type of pain addressed) | When used (pain-relief role) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| NSAID | Ketorolac | Toradol | Acute severe migraine pain, severe headache pain | Acute relief (often urgent/ER settings) |
| NSAID | Ibuprofen | Advil, Motrin | Mild–moderate migraine pain, tension-type headache pain | Acute relief (early in attack) |
| NSAID | Naproxen | Aleve, Naprosyn | Migraine pain with longer duration, menstrual migraine pain | Acute relief; can be combined with other agents |
| Triptan | Sumatriptan | Imitrex | Throbbing migraine headache pain with photophobia/nausea | Acute migraine abortive |
| Triptan | Rizatriptan | Maxalt | Acute migraine headache pain | Acute migraine abortive |
| CGRP receptor antagonist (gepant) | Ubrogepant | Ubrelvy | Acute migraine headache pain | Acute migraine abortive; non-triptan option |
| Ergot derivative | Dihydroergotamine | D.H.E. 45, Migranal | Refractory migraine headache pain | Acute relief (often for difficult attacks) |
| Antiemetic with analgesic benefit | Metoclopramide | Reglan | Migraine headache pain with prominent nausea/vomiting | Adjunct acute treatment; may improve pain control |
| Combination analgesic | Acetaminophen/aspirin/caffeine | Excedrin Migraine | Migraine headache pain, tension headache pain | Acute relief for mild–moderate attacks |
| Preventive (for reducing frequency) | Topiramate | Topamax | Reduces recurrence of migraine headache pain over time | Prevention (not for immediate pain relief) |
| Preventive (CGRP mAb) | Erenumab | Aimovig | Lowers frequency of migraine headache pain episodes | Prevention (scheduled dosing) |
| Preventive (beta-blocker) | Propranolol | Inderal | Reduces frequency/severity of migraine headache pain | Prevention (especially with comorbid anxiety/tremor) |
