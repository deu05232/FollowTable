# Zoonotic diseases and exposure risks (by disease)

| Disease | Causative agent | Animal reservoir/source | Typical human exposure | Main transmission route |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q fever | Coxiella burnetii (bacterium) | Cattle, sheep, goats | Assisting animal births; handling placenta/manure | Inhalation of contaminated aerosols/dust |
| Hantavirus pulmonary syndrome | Hantavirus | Deer mice and other rodents | Cleaning rodent-infested sheds; contact with droppings | Inhalation of aerosolized rodent excreta |
| Psittacosis | Chlamydia psittaci (bacterium) | Parrots, parakeets, poultry | Cleaning bird cages; handling sick birds | Inhalation of dried respiratory secretions/feces |
| Plague | Yersinia pestis (bacterium) | Rodents; fleas on rodents | Handling infected animals; flea exposure | Flea bites; contact with infected tissues |
| Toxoplasmosis | Toxoplasma gondii (protozoan) | Cats (definitive host); undercooked meat animals | Litter box cleaning; gardening; eating undercooked meat | Ingestion of oocysts or tissue cysts |
| Rift Valley fever | Rift Valley fever virus | Sheep, cattle, goats | Slaughtering/assisting births; mosquito exposure | Contact with blood/tissues; mosquito-borne transmission |
