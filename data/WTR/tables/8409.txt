# Notable Biology & Medicine Discoveries — Discovered Entity and What the Finding Explained

| Discovered thing (entity/finding) | Domain | Year | How it was discovered / demonstrated | What the discovery was about | Why it mattered (context/implication) |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Penicillin (antibiotic activity of Penicillium mold) | Microbiology / Medicine | 1928 | Accidental culture contamination and growth inhibition observation | A natural compound that kills or inhibits bacteria | Launched the antibiotic era; dramatically reduced bacterial mortality |
| DNA double helix structure | Genetics | 1953 | X-ray diffraction + model building | The molecular structure that explains replication and heredity | Foundation for molecular biology and modern genomics |
| Helicobacter pylori as a cause of peptic ulcers | Medicine | 1982 | Clinical isolation + self-experimentation and treatment response | Many ulcers are infectious rather than purely stress/acid-driven | Changed ulcer therapy; enabled curative antibiotic treatment |
| Prions (infectious proteins) | Neuroscience / Infectious disease | 1982 | Biochemical purification and transmission studies | Protein-only agents causing transmissible spongiform encephalopathies | Expanded concept of infection beyond nucleic acids; improved diagnostics |
| CRISPR–Cas as an adaptive immune system in bacteria | Molecular biology | 2007 | Genetic analyses and phage challenge experiments | Bacteria store viral sequences to recognize and cut invaders | Enabled CRISPR gene editing; major biotech and research impact |
| Plate tectonics (unifying framework) | Earth science | 1960s | Seafloor spreading, paleomagnetism, global seismicity patterns | Lithospheric plates move and recycle crust | Unified geology; explained earthquakes, volcanism, mountain building |
| Endosymbiotic origin of mitochondria and chloroplasts (modern synthesis) | Evolutionary biology | 1967 | Comparative cell biology and genetic evidence | Organelles originated from symbiotic bacteria | Clarified eukaryotic evolution; guided organelle genomics |
| SARS-CoV-2 (novel coronavirus causing COVID-19) | Virology | 2019 | Genome sequencing from patient samples | Identification of the pathogen and its genetic makeup | Enabled diagnostics, vaccines, and epidemiological tracking |
