# Typical Cat Lifespan by Living Situation and Care Level

| Category | Typical lifespan (years) | Common range (years) | Key notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Indoor pet (routine vet care) | 13–17 | 12–18 | Generally lowest risk of trauma, parasites, and infectious exposure |
| Indoor pet (minimal preventive care) | 10–14 | 8–15 | Missed vaccines/dental care can shorten lifespan |
| Indoor/outdoor (supervised, limited roaming) | 8–12 | 7–13 | Higher injury and disease exposure than indoor-only |
| Outdoor (free-roaming) | 2–7 | 1–10 | High mortality from cars, predators, toxins, and untreated illness |
| Community/feral cat (managed colony) | 3–8 | 2–10 | Trap–neuter–return and feeding can improve survival vs unmanaged ferals |
| Community/feral cat (unmanaged) | 2–5 | 1–7 | No consistent food/shelter; higher disease burden |
| Shelter cat (adopted into home) | 12–16 | 10–18 | Once in stable home, lifespan resembles indoor pets |
| Purebred indoor pet (breed-dependent) | 12–16 | 10–18 | Some breeds trend shorter/longer due to inherited risks |
| Mixed-breed domestic indoor pet | 13–18 | 12–20 | Often robust; wide variation by care and environment |
