# Common systemic antifungal agents and typical clinical uses

| Antifungal drug (generic) | Drug class | Common infection treated | Typical route | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Terbinafine | Allylamine | Onychomycosis (dermatophytes) | Oral | Often used for toenail fungus |
| Fluconazole | Azole | Vulvovaginal candidiasis | Oral | Active against many Candida spp. |
| Itraconazole | Azole | Sporotrichosis (cutaneous/lymphocutaneous) | Oral | Option for certain endemic mycoses |
| Voriconazole | Azole | Invasive aspergillosis | Oral/IV | Common first-line for Aspergillus |
| Posaconazole | Azole | Prophylaxis in high-risk neutropenia | Oral/IV | Used for prevention and treatment in select cases |
| Amphotericin B | Polyene | Severe systemic mycoses | IV | Broad activity; toxicity considerations |
| Flucytosine | Antimetabolite | Cryptococcal meningitis (with other agents) | Oral | Usually combined therapy |
| Echinocandins (e.g., caspofungin) | Echinocandin | Invasive candidiasis | IV | Candida-focused systemic therapy |
