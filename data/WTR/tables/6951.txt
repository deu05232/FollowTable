# Topical and Adjuvant Pain Medicines — Individual Drugs with Alternative Names

| Drug (Generic) | Common Brand Name(s) / Other Names | Form / Route | Pain Types Commonly Treated | Key Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Lidocaine | Lidoderm; Xylocaine | Topical patch/gel; local anesthetic | Localized neuropathic pain; post-herpetic neuralgia | Numbs the area; avoid applying to broken skin unless directed |
| Capsaicin | Zostrix; Qutenza | Topical cream/patch | Neuropathic pain; localized chronic pain | Can cause burning sensation initially; wash hands after use |
| Diclofenac (topical) | Voltaren Gel | Topical gel | Local joint or muscle pain | Lower systemic exposure than oral NSAIDs; still use as directed |
| Methyl salicylate + Menthol | Bengay; Icy Hot | Topical rub | Minor muscle aches and strains | Counterirritant; avoid excessive/occlusive use |
| Gabapentin | Neurontin | Oral | Neuropathic pain (e.g., nerve pain) | Drowsiness possible; dose titration common |
| Pregabalin | Lyrica | Oral | Neuropathic pain; fibromyalgia-related pain | Can cause dizziness/weight gain; controlled substance in some regions |
| Amitriptyline | Elavil | Oral | Neuropathic pain; chronic tension-type pain | Anticholinergic effects (dry mouth, constipation); sedating |
| Duloxetine | Cymbalta | Oral | Neuropathic pain; chronic musculoskeletal pain | SNRI; may help pain with comorbid anxiety/depression |
| Cyclobenzaprine | Flexeril | Oral | Painful muscle spasm (adjunct) | Not a primary analgesic; can be very sedating |
| Buprenorphine | Butrans | Transdermal patch | Chronic moderate–severe pain | Opioid; long-acting patch; misuse/respiratory risk still exists |
