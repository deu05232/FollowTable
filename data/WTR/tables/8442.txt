# Vaginal Yeast Infection Treatments  Over-the-Counter and Prescription Options by Active Ingredient and Product Form

| Category | Active ingredient (generic) | Example brand name(s) | Product form (patient-facing) | Typical duration options | Key practical notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Azole antifungal (OTC) | Miconazole | Monistat | Vaginal cream or suppository | 1-day, 3-day, 7-day | OTC availability; shorter courses may cause more local burning |
| Azole antifungal (OTC) | Clotrimazole | Gyne-Lotrimin, Mycelex | Vaginal cream or tablet | 3-day, 7-day | Often used for uncomplicated yeast infection |
| Azole antifungal (Rx) | Terconazole | Terazol | Vaginal cream or suppository | 3-day, 7-day | Prescription option; may be used when OTC fails |
| Azole antifungal (OTC) | Tioconazole | Vagistat-1 | Single-dose vaginal ointment | 1-day | Convenience of single dose; local irritation possible |
| Oral azole (Rx) | Fluconazole | Diflucan | Oral tablet | Single dose or repeat dose (e.g., 72 hours apart) | Simple dosing; consider drug interactions |
| Combination steroid + antifungal (Rx) | Clotrimazole/Betamethasone | Lotrisone | Topical cream (external use) | Varies by prescription | Not for intravaginal use; may relieve external inflammation |
| Non-azole alternative (Rx) | Nystatin | Mycostatin (varies) | Vaginal tablet/cream | Often 14 days (varies) | Alternative when azoles not tolerated |
