# Selected Anti-Infective Drugs with Spectrum Focus and Practical Counseling Points (One Drug per Row)

| Medication (generic) | Anti-infective type | Main spectrum/target | Common clinical use (examples) | Notable adverse effects / cautions |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Vancomycin | Antibiotic (glycopeptide) | Gram-positive incl. MRSA | MRSA bacteremia, endocarditis (with guidance), severe skin/soft tissue infections | Nephrotoxicity risk; infusion reaction ("red man")—slow rate; monitor levels in serious infections |
| Piperacillin-tazobactam | Antibiotic (antipseudomonal penicillin + BLI) | Broad incl. Pseudomonas, anaerobes | Intra-abdominal infections, severe hospital-acquired pneumonia, polymicrobial diabetic foot infection | Renal dose adjustment; higher AKI risk when combined with vancomycin in some settings |
| Meropenem | Antibiotic (carbapenem) | Very broad incl. ESBL producers, anaerobes | Severe sepsis with resistant Gram-negatives, complicated intra-abdominal infections | Seizure risk (rare; more with CNS disease/renal failure); reserve to limit resistance |
| Clindamycin | Antibiotic (lincosamide) | Gram-positive + anaerobes (no Gram-negative aerobes) | Dental/aspiration-related anaerobic infections; some skin infections | C. difficile colitis risk; check local resistance patterns |
| Ciprofloxacin | Antibiotic (fluoroquinolone) | Gram-negative incl. Pseudomonas (limited Gram+) | Complicated UTI/pyelonephritis, some GI infections | Tendinopathy, QT prolongation, CNS effects; avoid in pregnancy when alternatives exist |
| Isoniazid | Antimycobacterial | Mycobacterium tuberculosis | TB treatment (in regimens) and latent TB therapy | Hepatotoxicity; peripheral neuropathy—give pyridoxine in risk groups |
| Rifampin | Antimycobacterial | Mycobacteria; some Gram+ synergy | TB regimens; prosthetic device infections (adjunct) | Strong drug interactions (CYP induction); orange body fluids; hepatotoxicity |
| Artemether-lumefantrine | Antimalarial (ACT) | Plasmodium falciparum (uncomplicated) | Uncomplicated malaria in appropriate regions/resistance settings | QT prolongation caution; take with food/fat to improve absorption |
| Atovaquone-proguanil | Antimalarial | Blood-stage malaria (treatment/prophylaxis) | Malaria prophylaxis and uncomplicated malaria | Avoid in severe renal impairment; take with food/milk |
| Nystatin | Antifungal (polyene) | Candida (topical/oral suspension) | Oral thrush, cutaneous candidiasis | Not absorbed systemically (oral suspension); mainly local GI upset |
