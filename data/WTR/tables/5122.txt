It is now difficult to obtain from hardware stores. | Charcoal used in cooking, activated charcoal used in fish tank filters and graphite used in pencil leads are good sources of carbon. | Dry ice is available from party stores, refrigeration supply companies and ice cream companies. | Soda water is simply carbonated water, a dilute solution of carbonic acid. | Electrical wire, copper pipe, and copper sheeting are available at hardware stores; American pennies (minted 1944-1946, 1962-1982) are 95% copper and 5% zinc. | Basicop or Bluestone algaecide used to kill algae and other aquatic pests contain copper sulfate. | Some engine antifreezes are primarily ethylene glycol. | Dextrose (glucose) is available in many drug stores; some specialty throat lozenges are dextrose. | Glycerin is an emollient used to soften skin by delaying the evaporation of water. It is available at most drug stores. | Gold jewelry is generally not pure gold. Gold is generally alloyed with other metals to increase strength. | Helium can be obtained from party stores or wherever helium balloons are available. | Muriatic acid (the common name for HCl) is used in swimming pool maintenance. It is also sold as masonry cleaner and is available at the hardware store. Percentage concentrations vary. | Hydrogen peroxide antiseptic (3%) is available from the drug store. Clairoxide hair bleach by Clairol is much more concentrated and is available from beauty supply stores. | Tincture of iodine, a topical antiseptic used for treating wounds, is a solution of iodine dissolved in ethyl alcohol. It is available at most drug stores. | Steel wool, iron nails, iron bolts, nuts, screws are good sources of iron. You can get iron filings by dragging a strong magnet through sand. | Ceramic rust is used to add a red color to pottery and can be purchased at ceramic stores. | Lamp oil or kerosene is sold in the paint departments of most hardware stores. | Lead shot and lead sinkers are used by fishermen and are available at sporting goods stores. | Milk of Magnesia is an antacid used to settle sour (acidic) stomachs. Some antacid tablets also contain magnesium hydroxide. | Talcum powder comes from talc, the softest of all minerals, and is used as a dusting powder for babies. It is available in the bodycare section of the drug store. | Epsom salt is sold at most drug stores and is used as a laxative or as an anti-inflammatory soak. | Methanol is sold as a solvent in paint supply stores under the names "wood alcohol" or "methyl alcohol." | Methylene blue (Methidote antiseptic) is used to treat small injured fish and is available at pet stores. | Mineral oil is sold in drug stores as an emollient. Some baby oils are essentially mineral oil and fragrance. | Pre-1985 Canadian nickels are made of nickel. Note: American "nickels" are composed of 25% nickel and 75% copper. | Portable welding oxygen tanks are available at welding shops and some hardware stores. | Candle wax is made of paraffin. Some grocery stores sell paraffin as a sealant for home canning. | Ex-Lax laxative contained phenolphthalein prior to 1997. In that year it was determined phenolphthalein might cause cancer, and the thus the ingredients were changed. | Some pH reducers (available at pet stores) used in fish tanks are simply dilute solutions of phosphoric acid. | Cream of tartar is available at the market and is used to stabilize delicate foods like meringue toppings and other baked egg-white products. | Some agricultural supply companies sell potash to farmers who need to increase the potassium content in their soils. | Potassium bromide may be purchased from photography stores where it is used in photographic development. | Lite Salt is used as a salt substitute by people who must limit their sodium intake, and is available at most markets. | Mrs. Stewart's liquid laundry bluing is used to whiten clothes and may be found in the detergents section of the market. | Saltpeter or quick salt is used to cure home made sausages and corned beef and may be available at some butcher shops. | Clearwater is a solution of approximately 50% potassium permanganate and is used to remove odors and cloudiness from water to be used in aquariums. | Gas barbecue fuel is generally made of propane and is available at many gasoline stations or picnic supply stores. | Rubbing alcohol (isopropyl alcohol) is a concentrated solution (generally 70%) of 2-propanol and may be found in most drug stores. | Quartz sand is relatively pure silicon dioxide and is available at most building supply stores. | Older non-clad American dimes, quarters, half dollars and silver dollars are 90% silver and 10% copper. | Re-Heater and other hand-warmers are available at sporting goods stores. | Baking soda is pure sodium bicarbonate and may be found in the baking section of the market. | Washing soda is used to treat wool fibers and is available at spinning, weaving, and art supply stores. | The table salt used in cooking is sodium chloride. Iodized salt contains a trace of sodium iodide. | Known also as caustic soda and lye, sodium hydroxide is used in many commercial drain cleaners. | Household bleach is generally a 5% solution of sodium hypochlorite. | Tri-sodium phosphate, commonly known as TSP, is available at hardware stores and is used to clean walls prior to painting. | Borax , such as Twenty Mule Team Borax Laundry Booster is sodium tetraborate decahydrate. | Photographer's hypo is used in photograph development and is available at photography supply stores. | Table sugar is available at grocery stores in large or small bags. | Flowers of sulfur is sold at some garden stores to treat certain plant diseases. | Battery acid, also known as oil of vitriol, is sulfuric acid and may be obtained at some auto supply stores. | The filament in incandescent light bulbs is made of tungsten. | Recent American pennies (1982-present) are 97.5% zinc with a 2.5% copper coating. |
