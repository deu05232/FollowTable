# Major Human Hormones  Source Gland Tissue and Primary Physiological Effects

| Category | Hormone | Main producing gland/tissue | Primary target(s) | Practical physiological effect / function |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hypothalamus | TRH (thyrotropin-releasing hormone) | Hypothalamus | Anterior pituitary (thyrotrophs) | Stimulates TSH release → increases thyroid hormone production |
| Hypothalamus | CRH (corticotropin-releasing hormone) | Hypothalamus | Anterior pituitary (corticotrophs) | Stimulates ACTH release → promotes cortisol secretion during stress |
| Hypothalamus | GnRH (gonadotropin-releasing hormone) | Hypothalamus | Anterior pituitary (gonadotrophs) | Drives LH/FSH secretion → controls ovulation, testosterone production, and gametogenesis |
| Posterior pituitary (released) | ADH (vasopressin) | Hypothalamus (made); posterior pituitary (released) | Kidney collecting ducts; arterioles | Increases water reabsorption (concentrates urine); raises blood pressure via vasoconstriction |
| Posterior pituitary (released) | Oxytocin | Hypothalamus (made); posterior pituitary (released) | Uterus; mammary myoepithelial cells | Triggers uterine contractions; milk ejection (“let-down”) |
| Anterior pituitary | Growth hormone (GH) | Anterior pituitary | Liver; bone; muscle; adipose | Promotes linear growth (via IGF-1), increases protein synthesis, mobilizes fat; raises blood glucose |
| Anterior pituitary | Prolactin (PRL) | Anterior pituitary | Mammary glands; hypothalamus | Stimulates milk production; suppresses GnRH (can reduce fertility when elevated) |
| Thyroid | T3/T4 (thyroid hormones) | Thyroid follicular cells | Most tissues | Increases basal metabolic rate, heat production; supports growth and CNS development |
| Parathyroid | PTH (parathyroid hormone) | Parathyroid glands | Bone; kidney; indirectly gut (via calcitriol) | Raises blood calcium (bone resorption, renal Ca²⁺ reabsorption); lowers phosphate; activates vitamin D |
| Pancreas | Insulin | Pancreatic β-cells | Liver; muscle; adipose | Lowers blood glucose by increasing uptake/storage (glycogen, fat); promotes protein synthesis |
| Pancreas | Glucagon | Pancreatic α-cells | Liver | Raises blood glucose via glycogenolysis and gluconeogenesis; promotes ketone production |
| Adrenal cortex | Aldosterone | Adrenal cortex (zona glomerulosa) | Kidney distal nephron | Increases Na⁺ reabsorption and K⁺/H⁺ excretion → expands blood volume and increases BP |
| Adrenal medulla | Epinephrine (adrenaline) | Adrenal medulla | Heart; vessels; liver; bronchi | “Fight-or-flight”: increases heart rate/contractility, bronchodilation; raises blood glucose |
