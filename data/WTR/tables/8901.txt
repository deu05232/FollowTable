# Major human hormones and their primary physiological effects

| Hormone | Primary effect(s) in the body | Main target/process |
|---|---|---|
| Insulin | Lowers blood glucose by promoting uptake and storage | Liver, muscle, fat |
| Glucagon | Raises blood glucose by promoting glycogen breakdown and glucose release | Liver |
| Cortisol | Supports stress response; increases glucose availability; modulates inflammation | Many tissues |
| Aldosterone | Increases sodium retention and potassium excretion; raises blood volume | Kidneys |
| Antidiuretic hormone (ADH/vasopressin) | Increases water reabsorption; reduces urine output | Kidneys |
| Thyroid hormones (T3/T4) | Increase basal metabolic rate; support growth and nervous system maturation | Many tissues |
| Parathyroid hormone (PTH) | Raises blood calcium level | Bone, kidneys |
| Oxytocin | Triggers uterine contractions and milk ejection | Uterus, breast |
| Prolactin | Stimulates milk production | Breast |
| Epinephrine (adrenaline) | Increases heart rate and mobilizes energy in “fight-or-flight” | Heart, liver, muscle |
