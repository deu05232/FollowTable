# Household Disinfectants — Use Guidance and Pros Cons Comparison

| Substance / Product Type | Typical Use Case | Pros | Cons | Handling Notes | Storage Notes | Odor Level | Surface Compatibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sodium hypochlorite bleach | General disinfection | Fast acting; widely available | Corrosive; color fading | Wear gloves; ventilate | Keep away from heat/light | High | Poor on metals/fabrics |
| Hydrogen peroxide | Bathroom/kitchen cleaning | Less odor; no chlorine | Slower on some pathogens | Avoid eye contact | Store in opaque bottle | Low | Generally good |
| Quaternary ammonium wipes | Routine wipe-down | Convenient; non-bleaching | Residue possible | Do not mix with soaps | Seal package to prevent drying | Low | Good on many surfaces |
| Isopropyl alcohol | Electronics wiping | Quick evaporation | Flammable | Keep away from flames | Close cap tightly | Medium | Good on glass/plastics |
| Vinegar (acetic acid) | Descaling | Food-safe perception | Weak disinfectant | Avoid marble/stone | Standard pantry storage | Medium | Poor on stone |
| Ammonia cleaner | Glass cleaning | Streak-free | Irritating fumes | Never mix with bleach | Ventilated storage | High | Good on glass |
| Oxygen bleach (percarbonate) | Laundry whitening | Color-safe option | Needs warm water | Avoid inhaling dust | Keep dry | Low | Good on fabrics |
| Citric acid | Kettle descaling | Effective on limescale | Not a sanitizer | Rinse thoroughly | Keep dry | Low | Good on metal/plastic |
