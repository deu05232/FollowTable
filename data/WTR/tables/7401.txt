# Country Travel Health Overview  Major Infectious Diseases and Notes (No Risk Grade)

| Country | Food/Waterborne diseases (examples) | Vector-borne diseases (examples) | Other notable diseases | Seasonality note | Traveler advice |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Afghanistan | Typhoid, hepatitis A | Malaria (some areas) | Rabies | Higher malaria in warmer months | Consider vaccines; avoid animal bites |
| Angola | Diarrheal illness, typhoid | Malaria, dengue, yellow fever | Rabies | Vector season peaks during rains | Yellow fever vaccine for entry |
| Bangladesh | Diarrhea, hepatitis A/E | Dengue, malaria (some districts) | Rabies | Dengue peaks monsoon/post-monsoon | Use mosquito protection |
| Belize | Diarrhea, hepatitis A | Dengue, malaria (limited) | Leptospirosis | Rainy season increases mosquito exposure | Use repellents; safe water |
| Benin | Diarrhea, typhoid | Malaria, yellow fever | Meningococcal disease | Meningitis belt season varies | Vaccination and nets |
| Cambodia | Diarrhea, hepatitis A | Dengue, malaria (border areas) | Rabies | Dengue peaks rainy season | Avoid dog bites; repellents |
| Ethiopia | Diarrhea, typhoid | Malaria (lower elevations) | Meningococcal disease | Seasonal meningitis outbreaks | Vaccines; avoid unsafe water |
| Peru | Diarrhea, hepatitis A | Dengue (Amazon), malaria (Amazon) | Leishmaniasis | Amazon has year-round exposure | Mosquito protection, safe food |
