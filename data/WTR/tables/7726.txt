# Exposure Pathways and Risk Context for Selected Infectious Diseases (Reservoir Source + Transmission Route)

| Disease | Primary reservoir/source | How it spreads (main transmission route) | Common exposure settings | Who is at higher risk (risk context) | Prevention interruption points |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rabies | Infected mammals (dogs, bats, raccoons) | Bite/scratch; saliva into broken skin or mucosa | Animal handling, stray dog bites, bat contact in buildings/caves | Veterinarians, animal control, travelers in endemic areas, people with unvaccinated pets | Vaccinate pets; avoid wildlife contact; prompt wound washing; post-exposure prophylaxis |
| Leptospirosis | Rodents, dogs, livestock (urine) | Contact with urine-contaminated water/soil via skin cuts or mucosa | Floodwater cleanup, freshwater recreation, farming | Farmers, sewer workers, disaster responders, people in flooded areas | Protective footwear/gloves; rodent control; avoid wading in suspect water |
| Salmonellosis (non-typhoidal) | Poultry, reptiles, contaminated foods | Fecal–oral via contaminated food; cross-contamination | Backyard poultry, raw eggs, undercooked meat, pet reptiles | Young children, food handlers, immunocompromised, reptile owners | Cook thoroughly; hand hygiene after animal contact; separate raw/ready-to-eat foods |
| Legionnaires’ disease | Man-made water systems (biofilms) | Inhalation of contaminated aerosols (not person-to-person) | Cooling towers, hot tubs, large plumbing systems | Older adults, smokers, chronic lung disease, building occupants near outbreaks | Water system maintenance; temperature control; disinfect/flush systems |
| Dengue | Humans (viremic) as source; Aedes mosquitoes as vector | Mosquito-borne (Aedes bites) | Urban areas with standing water; daytime outdoor exposure | Residents/travelers in endemic regions, people lacking window screens/repellent | Remove standing water; repellents; screens; community vector control |
| Anthrax | Herbivores/animal products; spores in soil | Cutaneous contact, inhalation, ingestion of spores | Handling hides/wool, slaughtering livestock, contaminated soil exposure | Tannery/wool workers, veterinarians, livestock handlers, certain occupations | PPE for animal products; safe carcass disposal; vaccination in high-risk jobs |
| Hantavirus pulmonary syndrome | Rodents (deer mouse, etc.) | Inhalation of aerosolized rodent urine/feces | Cleaning cabins/sheds, sweeping rodent-infested areas | Rural residents, campers, pest-control workers | Wet cleaning (avoid dry sweeping); seal entry points; rodent control |
| Cholera | Contaminated water/food (human fecal contamination) | Fecal–oral via unsafe water/food | Humanitarian crises, poor sanitation, coastal raw seafood | People without safe water/sanitation, displaced populations | Safe water (chlorination/boiling); sanitation; hand hygiene; food safety |
