# Common foodborne infection risks and typical exposure sources

| Pathogen / Disease | Typical exposure route | Common risky foods / settings | Main at-risk groups | Prevention focus |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Salmonellosis | Foodborne (ingestion) | Undercooked poultry, eggs, cross-contaminated surfaces | Young children, older adults | Cook thoroughly; kitchen hygiene |
| Campylobacteriosis | Foodborne (ingestion) | Undercooked chicken, unpasteurized milk | Immunocompromised people | Avoid raw milk; cook poultry |
| Listeriosis | Foodborne (ingestion) | Soft cheeses, deli meats, refrigerated ready-to-eat foods | Pregnant people, newborns | Avoid high-risk foods; refrigeration control |
| Norovirus gastroenteritis | Foodborne / person-to-person via contaminated hands | Restaurants, cruise ships, catered events | Everyone; outbreaks in closed settings | Handwashing; isolate cases |
| Hepatitis A | Foodborne / fecal-oral | Contaminated produce, shellfish, poor sanitation | Unvaccinated travelers | Vaccination; safe water/food |
| Cholera | Water/foodborne | Unsafe drinking water, raw seafood | People in disaster or low-sanitation areas | Safe water; oral rehydration; sanitation |
