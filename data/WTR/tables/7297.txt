# Physiological Effects of Major Human Hormones (Targets and Functional Outcomes)

| Hormone | Primary physiological actions (what it does) | Key target responses / outcomes (easy-to-compare effects) | Notable excess/deficiency patterns (functional) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Insulin | Promotes nutrient storage and lowers plasma glucose | ↑ glucose uptake (muscle/adipose), ↑ glycogen synthesis, ↑ lipogenesis, ↓ hepatic gluconeogenesis | Deficiency: hyperglycemia, ketogenesis; Excess: hypoglycemia, weight gain |
| Glucagon | Mobilizes energy stores and raises plasma glucose | ↑ glycogenolysis, ↑ gluconeogenesis, ↑ lipolysis (supportive) | Excess: hyperglycemia; Deficiency: impaired fasting glucose maintenance |
| Cortisol | Coordinates stress response; maintains vascular tone; shifts metabolism | ↑ gluconeogenesis, ↑ protein catabolism, ↑ lipolysis; anti-inflammatory/immunosuppressive; ↑ blood pressure responsiveness | Excess: central adiposity, hyperglycemia, muscle wasting; Deficiency: fatigue, hypotension |
| Aldosterone | Conserves sodium and water; promotes potassium excretion | ↑ Na⁺ reabsorption and water retention; ↑ K⁺ and H⁺ secretion | Excess: hypertension, hypokalemia; Deficiency: hypotension, hyperkalemia |
| Thyroid hormone (T3/T4) | Sets basal metabolic rate; supports growth and CNS development | ↑ oxygen consumption/heat production, ↑ heart rate/contractility, ↑ lipid/carbohydrate turnover | Excess: weight loss, tachycardia; Deficiency: cold intolerance, bradycardia |
| Growth hormone (GH) | Promotes growth and nutrient partitioning | ↑ IGF-1 production, ↑ linear growth; ↑ lipolysis; ↓ insulin sensitivity | Excess: acromegaly-like changes; Deficiency: reduced growth, increased fat mass |
| Antidiuretic hormone (ADH/vasopressin) | Conserves body water; regulates osmolality | ↑ water reabsorption in kidney; at high levels: vasoconstriction | Deficiency: polyuria/polydipsia; Excess: hyponatremia |
| Oxytocin | Drives parturition and milk ejection | ↑ uterine contractions; ↑ milk let-down via myoepithelial contraction | Deficiency: impaired milk ejection (functional) |
| Parathyroid hormone (PTH) | Raises serum calcium and lowers phosphate | ↑ bone resorption (net), ↑ renal Ca²⁺ reabsorption, ↑ phosphate excretion, ↑ calcitriol activation | Excess: hypercalcemia; Deficiency: hypocalcemia, tetany |
| Erythropoietin (EPO) | Increases oxygen-carrying capacity | ↑ red blood cell production in bone marrow | Deficiency: anemia; Excess: increased hematocrit/thrombosis risk |
