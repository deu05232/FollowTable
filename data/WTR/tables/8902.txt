# Physiological Effects of Major Human Hormones by Target Tissue

| Hormone | Primary physiological effect/function (in the body) | Main target tissue(s) | Key regulated process/outcome |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Insulin | Promotes cellular glucose uptake and storage; inhibits hepatic glucose output | Liver, skeletal muscle, adipose tissue | Lowers blood glucose; increases glycogen and fat storage |
| Glucagon | Stimulates glycogen breakdown and gluconeogenesis | Liver | Raises blood glucose during fasting |
| Epinephrine (adrenaline) | Increases cardiac output; mobilizes fuels for acute stress | Heart, liver, skeletal muscle, adipose tissue | “Fight-or-flight”: higher heart rate, increased glucose and fatty acids |
| Cortisol | Supports stress adaptation; maintains vascular tone; increases glucose availability | Many tissues (liver, muscle, immune cells) | Longer-term stress response; increased gluconeogenesis; anti-inflammatory effects |
| Aldosterone | Increases sodium reabsorption and potassium excretion | Kidney distal nephron | Expands extracellular fluid volume; raises blood pressure |
| Antidiuretic hormone (ADH/vasopressin) | Increases water reabsorption; concentrates urine | Kidney collecting duct; blood vessels | Maintains plasma osmolality; supports blood pressure |
| Parathyroid hormone (PTH) | Raises serum calcium; lowers serum phosphate | Bone, kidney (and indirectly intestine) | Calcium homeostasis via bone resorption and renal reabsorption |
| Thyroid hormones (T3/T4) | Increase basal metabolic rate; support growth and CNS development | Most tissues | Higher energy expenditure; normal growth/maturation |
| Growth hormone (GH) | Stimulates linear growth and tissue anabolism | Liver, bone, muscle, adipose tissue | Increases IGF-1 production; supports growth; alters fat/carbohydrate use |
| Prolactin | Initiates and maintains milk production | Mammary gland | Lactation (milk synthesis) |
| Oxytocin | Triggers uterine contractions and milk ejection | Uterus; mammary myoepithelial cells | Labor progression; milk let-down |
| Estrogen (estradiol) | Drives female reproductive tract maturation; supports bone maintenance | Reproductive organs, bone, breast | Menstrual cycle regulation; secondary sex characteristics; bone preservation |
| Progesterone | Prepares/maintains endometrium; reduces uterine contractility | Uterus; breast | Supports implantation/pregnancy; secretory endometrium |
| Testosterone | Promotes male reproductive development; anabolic effects | Reproductive organs, muscle, bone | Spermatogenesis support; increased muscle mass; male secondary sex traits |
