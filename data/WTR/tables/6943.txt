# Common Pain Medication Classes (with examples)

| Drug Class (main entry) | Typical Uses | Common Examples (not exhaustive) | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| NSAIDs | Mild–moderate pain, inflammation, fever | Ibuprofen; Naproxen; Diclofenac | GI irritation risk; avoid in some kidney disease |
| COX-2 Selective NSAIDs | Inflammatory pain with less GI ulcer risk | Celecoxib; Etoricoxib | Cardiovascular risk considerations |
| Acetaminophen (Analgesic/Antipyretic) | Mild pain, fever | Acetaminophen (Paracetamol) | Liver toxicity with overdose or heavy alcohol use |
| Opioids | Moderate–severe pain | Morphine; Oxycodone; Hydrocodone | Dependence and respiratory depression risks |
| Adjuvant Analgesics: Antidepressants | Neuropathic pain | Amitriptyline; Duloxetine | Often not “painkillers” per se; used for nerve pain |
| Adjuvant Analgesics: Anticonvulsants | Neuropathic pain | Gabapentin; Pregabalin | Sedation/dizziness common |
| Topical Analgesics | Localized pain | Lidocaine patch; Diclofenac gel | Useful for focal pain; fewer systemic effects |
