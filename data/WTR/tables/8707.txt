# Timeline of Key Discoveries in Early Human Evolution (Selected Hominins)

| Approx. time period | Hominin / Evidence | Discovered (year) | Site / Region | Research takeaway (brief) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ~6–7 million years | Early hominin candidates (e.g., Sahelanthropus tchadensis) | 2001 | Chad (Toros-Menalla) | Signals very early hominin diversity in Africa |
| ~4.4 million years | Ardipithecus ramidus | 1994 | Ethiopia (Afar) | Informs debates about early bipedalism |
| ~3.6 million years | Laetoli trackway | 1978 | Tanzania | Footprints supporting upright gait |
| ~3.2 million years | Australopithecus afarensis (“Lucy”) | 1974 | Ethiopia | Skeleton used to reconstruct locomotion |
| ~2.0 million years | Homo habilis (classic finds) | 1960 | Tanzania (Olduvai Gorge) | Early Homo associated with tool-use discussions |
| ~1.8 million years | Homo erectus (early expansions) | 1891 | Indonesia (Java/Trinil) | Highlights geographic spread of early Homo |
