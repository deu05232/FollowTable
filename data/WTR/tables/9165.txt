# Postpartum Hemorrhage — Etiologic Classification (The 4 Ts) and Immediate Interventions

| Category (4 Ts) | Example causes | Typical clinical signs | Key diagnostics | Immediate management |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tone | Uterine atony | Boggy uterus, heavy bleeding | Bimanual exam | Uterotonics, uterine massage |
| Trauma | Lacerations, uterine rupture | Firm uterus, continued bleeding | Speculum exam, imaging if needed | Repair, surgical consult |
| Tissue | Retained placenta | Subinvolution, persistent bleeding | Ultrasound | Manual removal, curettage |
| Thrombin | Coagulopathy, DIC | Oozing, bruising | PT/INR, fibrinogen | Blood products |
| Mixed | Atony + laceration | Variable | As above | Combined measures |
| Iatrogenic | Operative delivery | Trauma pattern | History review | Repair + monitoring |
| Infection-associated | Endometritis | Fever, uterine tenderness | CBC, cultures | Antibiotics |
| Placental disorders | Accreta spectrum | Severe bleeding at delivery | Prenatal imaging/history | Surgical planning |
