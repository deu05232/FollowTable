# Zoonotic Diseases and Exposure Risks (Animal Sources and Typical Transmission Routes)

| Zoonotic disease | Causative agent (type) | Primary animal reservoirs / sources | Typical human infection route (animal exposure) | Notes (exposure setting examples) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Plague | *Yersinia pestis* (bacterium) | Wild rodents (rats, ground squirrels, prairie dogs); cats can be secondary hosts | Flea bites after fleas feed on infected rodents; handling infected animals | Risk with rodent die-offs, hunting, and cat-associated cases |
| Q fever | *Coxiella burnetii* (bacterium) | Sheep, goats, cattle | Inhalation of contaminated aerosols/dust from birth products, manure, wool | High risk during lambing/kidding; abattoirs and farms |
| Hantavirus pulmonary syndrome | Hantaviruses (virus) | Deer mice and other rodents (species vary by region) | Inhalation of aerosolized rodent urine/feces/saliva; cleaning infested spaces | Not primarily person-to-person (rare exceptions in some strains) |
| Psittacosis (parrot fever) | *Chlamydia psittaci* (bacterium) | Parrots, parakeets, cockatiels, pigeons, poultry | Inhalation of dried bird droppings/respiratory secretions; handling birds | Risk for bird owners, pet shops, aviaries |
| Tularemia | *Francisella tularensis* (bacterium) | Rabbits/hares; rodents; can involve ticks and deer flies | Handling infected animals; bites from infected ticks/deer flies; inhalation during landscaping | “Rabbit fever”; risk for hunters and lawn work in endemic areas |
| Brucellosis | *Brucella* spp. (bacterium) | Goats, sheep, cattle, pigs, dogs (species depend on *Brucella*) | Direct contact with animal fluids/tissues; ingestion of unpasteurized dairy | Occupational risk: veterinarians, farmers, abattoir workers |
| Rabies | Rabies lyssavirus (virus) | Dogs (some regions), bats, raccoons, foxes, skunks | Bite or saliva exposure to mucosa/broken skin | Requires prompt post-exposure prophylaxis |
| Toxoplasmosis | *Toxoplasma gondii* (protozoan) | Cats (definitive host); many warm-blooded animals as intermediate hosts | Ingestion of oocysts from cat feces/contaminated soil; handling litter; undercooked meat | Pregnancy risk; gardening and litter box exposure |
| Echinococcosis (hydatid disease) | *Echinococcus granulosus* / *E. multilocularis* (tapeworm) | Dogs and wild canids (foxes); sheep/rodents as intermediate hosts | Ingestion of eggs from canid fecal contamination (hands/food/water/soil) | Risk with sheep-dog cycles; handling dogs in endemic areas |
| Rift Valley fever | Rift Valley fever virus (virus) | Sheep, cattle, goats (amplifying hosts) | Contact with blood/organs during slaughter/animal care; mosquito bites can also occur | Outbreaks linked to livestock epizootics and heavy rains |
