# Oral and Topical Agents for Skin and Soft Tissue Infections (Includes Antifungals and Antibiotics)

| Infection/Condition | Typical Pathogen Type | Example Medication/Product | Category | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cellulitis | Bacterial | Cephalexin | Antibiotic | Nonpurulent cellulitis |
| MRSA abscess | Bacterial | Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole | Antibiotic | Community-associated MRSA |
| Folliculitis | Bacterial | Clindamycin (topical) | Antibiotic | Localized cases |
| Dermatophyte tinea corporis | Fungal | Terbinafine | Antifungal | Topical or oral depending on severity |
| Cutaneous candidiasis | Fungal | Clotrimazole | Antifungal | Topical azole |
| Onychomycosis | Fungal | Itraconazole | Antifungal | Oral pulse or continuous regimens |
| Ectoparasitic scabies | Parasitic | Permethrin 5% cream | Antiparasitic | Treat household contacts |
| Herpes zoster | Viral | Valacyclovir | Antiviral | Start early for benefit |
