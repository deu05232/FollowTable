# # Human hormones  where they are produced and main effects

| Hormone | Main production site | Primary effect(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Insulin | Pancreas (beta cells) | Lowers blood glucose by promoting uptake and storage |
| Glucagon | Pancreas (alpha cells) | Raises blood glucose by stimulating glycogen breakdown and gluconeogenesis |
| Aldosterone | Adrenal cortex | Increases sodium retention and water balance; helps regulate blood pressure |
| Cortisol | Adrenal cortex | Supports stress response; affects metabolism and inflammation |
| Antidiuretic hormone (ADH/vasopressin) | Posterior pituitary (released) | Increases water reabsorption in kidneys; helps control blood pressure |
| Oxytocin | Posterior pituitary (released) | Stimulates uterine contractions and milk let-down |
| Thyroid hormone (T3/T4) | Thyroid gland | Regulates metabolism, growth, and development |
| Parathyroid hormone (PTH) | Parathyroid glands | Raises blood calcium levels |
