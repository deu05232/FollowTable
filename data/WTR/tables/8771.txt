# Feline Lifespan by Living Situation (with Leading Causes of Death)

| Cat group | Typical lifespan (years) | Main mortality driver | Common illnesses/symptoms noted | Typical veterinary interventions |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Indoor-only pet cats | 12–18 | Chronic disease over time | Dental disease, CKD signs (PU/PD), obesity | Dental cleaning, renal diet, weight management |
| Outdoor-only cats | 2–5 | Trauma/infectious exposure | Bite wounds, abscesses, URI signs | Wound care, vaccines, parasite control |
| Indoor/outdoor cats | 7–12 | Mixed: trauma + disease | Fleas/ticks, GI upset, lameness | Preventives, vaccination, microchip |
| Community/feral cats | 3–7 | Infection + limited care | FIV/FeLV risk, parasites, malnutrition | TNR, deworming, vaccination when possible |
| Shelter cats (recent intake) | 8–14 | Stress-related disease | URI complex, diarrhea | Isolation, supportive care, vaccination |
| Senior cats (any setting) | 11–20 | Organ failure/cancer | Weight loss, vomiting, lethargy | Bloodwork monitoring, imaging, palliative care |
