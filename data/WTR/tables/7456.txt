# Major scientific discoveries in human evolution (selected fossil finds)

| Discovery (fossil/find) | Species / subject | Discovered (year) | Location found | Discoverer / team | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Lucy | Australopithecus afarensis | 1974 | Hadar, Ethiopia | Donald Johanson team | Iconic partial skeleton supporting bipedalism in early hominins |
| Ardi | Ardipithecus ramidus | 1994 | Aramis, Ethiopia | Tim White team | Early hominin anatomy showing a different path to bipedalism |
| Taung Child | Australopithecus africanus | 1924 | Taung, South Africa | Raymond Dart | Key evidence for African origins of early hominins |
| Java Man | Homo erectus | 1891 | Trinil, Java, Indonesia | Eugène Dubois | Early evidence of ancient humans outside Africa |
| Omo I | Homo sapiens (early modern human) | 1967 | Omo Kibish, Ethiopia | Richard Leakey expedition | Among the oldest widely cited fossils of anatomically modern humans |
