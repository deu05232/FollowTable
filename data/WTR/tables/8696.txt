# Major Early Human Ancestor Discoveries (Species-Centered Comparison)

| Hominin species | Approx. age (years ago) | Discovery year | Site / region | Country | Key remains / evidence | Scientific notes (very brief) |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|---|---|
| *Sahelanthropus tchadensis* | ~7,000,000 | 2001 | Toros-Menalla (Djurab Desert) | Chad | Cranium (“Toumaï”) | Very early candidate near human–chimp split; bipedality debated |
| *Orrorin tugenensis* | ~6,000,000 | 2000 | Tugen Hills | Kenya | Femur fragments + teeth | Femur anatomy used to argue early bipedal traits |
| *Ardipithecus ramidus* | ~4,400,000 | 1994 | Aramis (Afar) | Ethiopia | Partial skeleton (“Ardi”) | Woodland-adapted hominin; locomotion not like modern chimps |
| *Australopithecus afarensis* | ~3,200,000 | 1974 | Hadar | Ethiopia | Partial skeleton (“Lucy”) | Classic bipedal australopith; key for early hominin anatomy |
| *Homo habilis* | ~2,400,000–1,400,000 | 1960 | Olduvai Gorge | Tanzania | Cranial remains + hand bones | Often linked with Oldowan tool use; early Homo brain expansion |
| *Homo floresiensis* | ~100,000–60,000 | 2003 | Liang Bua Cave (Flores) | Indonesia | Partial skeleton (LB1) | Small-bodied late-surviving Homo; island evolution hypotheses |
| *Homo naledi* | ~335,000–236,000 | 2013 | Rising Star Cave | South Africa | Large assemblage of bones | Mosaic traits; raised questions about diversity and behavior |
| Early *Homo sapiens* | ~315,000 | 2017 | Jebel Irhoud | Morocco | Crania + associated remains | Pushed back age of *H. sapiens*; early modern facial traits |
