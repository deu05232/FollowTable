# Common Infections and First-Line Treatment Options (Condition-Centered Quick Reference)

| Infection/Condition | Typical Pathogens | First-line Treatment | Alternatives | Duration (Typical) | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Uncomplicated cystitis | E. coli | Nitrofurantoin | TMP-SMX, Fosfomycin | 3–7 days | Avoid nitrofurantoin if CrCl very low |
| Community-acquired pneumonia (outpatient) | S. pneumoniae, atypicals | Amoxicillin or Doxycycline | Azithromycin (selected areas) | 5 days | Consider comorbidities and local resistance |
| Acute bacterial sinusitis | S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae | Amoxicillin-clavulanate | Doxycycline | 5–7 days | Reserve antibiotics for persistent/severe cases |
| Cellulitis (nonpurulent) | Streptococci | Cephalexin | Clindamycin | 5–10 days | Add MRSA coverage if risk factors |
| Gonorrhea (uncomplicated) | N. gonorrhoeae | Ceftriaxone | — | Single dose | Add doxycycline if chlamydia not excluded |
| H. pylori infection | H. pylori | Bismuth quadruple therapy | Concomitant therapy | 10–14 days | Requires combination regimen; check local clarithromycin resistance |
| Vulvovaginal candidiasis | Candida spp. | Fluconazole | Topical azoles | 1–7 days | Pregnancy: prefer topical azoles |
| Influenza (early) | Influenza A/B | Oseltamivir | Zanamivir, Baloxavir | 5 days (most) | Best within 48 hours of symptom onset |
