# Agricultural Disease and Pest Mortality Impact (Orchard and Field, Annual Losses)

| Organism affected | Disease/pest | Host crop | Typical mortality rate (plants) | Yield loss (low) | Yield loss (high) | Common control measure | Notes |
|---|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| Apple trees | Fire blight | Apple | 5–25% | 10% | 50% | Pruning + copper sprays | Rapid dieback in warm/wet springs |
| Apple fruit | Apple scab | Apple | 0–5% | 5% | 30% | Fungicide program | Primarily quality/yield impact |
| Apple fruit | Bitter rot | Apple | 1–10% | 5% | 40% | Sanitation + fungicide | Worse in humid storage |
| Potato plants | Late blight | Potato | 10–60% | 20% | 90% | Resistant varieties | Can destroy fields quickly |
| Wheat | Stem rust | Wheat | 0–15% | 5% | 70% | Resistant cultivars | Outbreak-dependent |
| Citrus trees | Citrus greening (HLB) | Citrus | 5–35% | 15% | 80% | Vector control | Long-term decline |
| Chickens | Avian influenza | Poultry | 20–90% | 20% | 90% | Culling + biosecurity | Non-human outbreak metric |
| Honey bees | Varroa mite infestation | Honey bees | 10–50% | 5% | 40% | Miticides | Colony losses vary by season |
