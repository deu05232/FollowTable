# Risk factors and typical triggers for common respiratory diseases

| Condition | Typical triggers/exposures | Common settings | High-risk groups | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Asthma | Allergens, smoke, exercise, cold air | Home, school, workplace | Children, atopic individuals | Trigger patterns vary by person |
| COPD | Tobacco smoke, biomass fuel exposure | Long-term household/work exposure | Older adults, smokers | Often coexists with cardiovascular disease |
| Influenza | Close contact, crowded indoor spaces | Schools, offices, public transport | Older adults, pregnant people | Seasonal strain changes |
| Pneumonia | Aspiration, viral infection, bacterial exposure | Hospitals, long-term care | Infants, older adults, immunocompromised | Etiology differs by setting |
| Tuberculosis | Prolonged exposure to infectious cases | Household, shelters, prisons | People with HIV, close contacts | Latent vs active disease distinction |
