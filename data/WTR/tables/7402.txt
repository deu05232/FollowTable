# Non-communicable Diseases and Risk Factors (Quantitative Indicators Only)

| Condition | Primary risk factor | Approx. prevalence (adult) | Relative risk / odds ratio (example) | Common complication | Screening metric |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Type 2 diabetes | Obesity | 8–10% | RR 3.0 (obesity vs normal BMI) | Kidney disease | Fasting glucose, HbA1c |
| Coronary artery disease | Smoking | 5–7% | RR 2.0 (smokers vs non-smokers) | Myocardial infarction | Lipids, BP, CAC score |
| Stroke | Hypertension | 2–4% | RR 4.0 (uncontrolled HTN) | Disability | BP, carotid ultrasound |
| COPD | Smoking/biomass exposure | 6–10% | OR 5.0 (heavy smokers) | Respiratory failure | Spirometry (FEV1/FVC) |
| Lung cancer | Smoking | <1% | RR 15–30 (smokers) | Metastasis | Low-dose CT (high-risk) |
| Cervical cancer | HPV infection | <1% | RR 10+ (persistent high-risk HPV) | Infertility, mortality | HPV test, Pap smear |
| Liver cirrhosis | Alcohol/HBV/HCV | 0.3–1% | RR 4–8 (heavy alcohol) | Variceal bleeding | LFTs, ultrasound |
| Chronic kidney disease | Diabetes/HTN | 10–15% | RR 2–3 (diabetes) | ESRD | eGFR, albuminuria |
