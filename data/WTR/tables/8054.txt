# Veterinary and Zoonotic Diseases  Case Fatality and Outbreak Notes (Non-human Focus)

| Disease | Primary host species | Transmission | Typical case fatality (host) | Outbreak setting | Human relevance note | Control measure | Data caveat |
|---|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| Rabies | Dogs, bats | Bite/saliva | 99% | Stray dog populations | Human fatal without PEP | Vaccination campaigns | Underreporting common |
| African swine fever | Pigs | Contact/fomites | 30–100% | Commercial farms | No confirmed human cases | Biosecurity, culling | Varies by strain |
| Highly pathogenic avian influenza (H5) | Poultry | Aerosol/contact | 10–90% | Live bird markets | Rare spillover events | Culling, movement bans | Surveillance bias |
| Foot-and-mouth disease | Cattle, sheep | Aerosol/contact | <5% | Mixed herds | Human disease extremely rare | Vaccination/containment | Economic losses dominate |
| Canine distemper | Dogs, wild carnivores | Respiratory | 30–80% | Shelters | Not a human pathogen | Vaccination | Depends on care level |
| Bovine tuberculosis | Cattle | Respiratory | 5–20% | Dairy farms | Zoonotic potential | Test-and-slaughter | Chronic course |
| White-nose syndrome | Bats | Fungal spores | 70–100% | Caves | Ecological impact | Cave closures | Population estimates uncertain |
| Colony collapse (multifactorial) | Honey bees | Multifactorial | 20–50% | Apiaries | Indirect human impact | Varroa control | Not a single disease entity |
