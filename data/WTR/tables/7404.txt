# Waterborne Diseases  Symptoms, Incubation, and Exposure Sources

| Disease | Pathogen type | Primary symptoms | Typical incubation | Common exposure source | Prevention |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cholera | Bacteria | Profuse watery diarrhea, dehydration | Hours–5 days | Contaminated drinking water | Safe water, sanitation, vaccine in some settings |
| Typhoid fever | Bacteria | Prolonged fever, abdominal pain | 6–30 days | Contaminated food/water | Vaccination, hygiene |
| Hepatitis A | Virus | Jaundice, fatigue, nausea | 15–50 days | Food handler contamination | Vaccination |
| Cryptosporidiosis | Parasite | Watery diarrhea, cramps | 2–10 days | Pools, childcare settings | Avoid swallowing pool water |
| Giardiasis | Parasite | Greasy stools, bloating | 1–2 weeks | Wilderness/untreated water | Boil/filter water |
| Norovirus gastroenteritis | Virus | Vomiting, diarrhea | 12–48 hours | Cruise ships, restaurants | Handwashing, surface disinfection |
| Shigellosis | Bacteria | Bloody diarrhea, fever | 1–3 days | Person-to-person, food | Hand hygiene |
| E. coli O157:H7 | Bacteria | Bloody diarrhea, HUS risk | 1–8 days | Undercooked beef, raw produce | Cook meat, avoid cross-contamination |
