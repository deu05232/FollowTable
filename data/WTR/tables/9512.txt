# Animal-Exposure Zoonoses  Pathogens, Key Animal Sources, and How Humans Typically Become Infected

| Zoonotic disease | Pathogen | Main animal sources involved | How infection typically happens (non-waterborne, not mainly person-to-person) | Common exposure scenarios |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Salmonellosis (non-typhoidal) | *Salmonella enterica* (bacterium) | Poultry, reptiles (turtles/lizards/snakes), backyard flocks | Fecal-oral from animal contact; contaminated hands/surfaces after handling animals | Reptile pets; chicks/ducklings; cleaning cages |
| Campylobacteriosis | *Campylobacter jejuni/coli* (bacteria) | Poultry; cattle; puppies/kittens with diarrhea | Handling infected animals or raw poultry; cross-contamination in kitchens | Farms; raw poultry prep; petting zoos |
| Avian influenza (e.g., H5/H7 infections) | Influenza A viruses (virus) | Domestic poultry, live bird markets; some wild birds | Close contact with infected birds or contaminated environments; inhalation of droplets/dust | Culling operations; live bird markets; home slaughter |
| Mpox (monkeypox) | Monkeypox virus (orthopoxvirus) | Rodents and other small mammals (suspected reservoirs); captive wildlife | Animal bites/scratches; handling infected animals; exposure to body fluids | Wildlife handling; exotic pet trade; bushmeat preparation |
| Trichinellosis | *Trichinella* spp. (nematode) | Pigs; wild boar; bear; other carnivores/omnivores | Eating undercooked meat containing larvae (animal-origin food exposure) | Hunting; home sausage; traditional raw/undercooked meats |
| Toxocariasis | *Toxocara canis/cati* (roundworms) | Dogs and cats (especially puppies/kittens) | Ingestion of eggs from contaminated soil/sand or dirty hands after pet contact | Sandboxes; yards; poor hand hygiene after petting |
| Cat-scratch disease | *Bartonella henselae* (bacterium) | Cats (often via cat fleas maintaining infection) | Scratch/bite; contamination of broken skin with cat saliva | Kittens; rough play; flea-infested cats |
| Lymphocytic choriomeningitis (LCMV) | LCMV (arenavirus) | House mice; pet hamsters (occasionally) | Inhalation of aerosolized urine/feces; direct contact with secretions | Rodent infestations; cleaning cages |
| Orf (contagious ecthyma) | Parapoxvirus (virus) | Sheep and goats | Direct skin contact with lesions/scabs on animals | Shearing; bottle feeding; farm/4-H activities |
| Streptococcus suis infection | *Streptococcus suis* (bacterium) | Pigs | Cuts/skin exposure while handling pigs or pork; slaughtering/processing | Pig farming; abattoirs; butchering |
