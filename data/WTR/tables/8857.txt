# Non-Anti-Infective 'Treatments' Commonly Used for Symptom Relief During Illness

| Product/Drug | Category | What It Treats | Example Uses | Key Safety Notes | Infection-Curing? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Acetaminophen | Analgesic/antipyretic | Fever, pain | Flu-like symptoms | Liver toxicity at high doses | No |
| Ibuprofen | NSAID | Pain, inflammation | Sore throat pain | GI bleeding, renal risk | No |
| Dextromethorphan | Antitussive | Cough | Viral URI cough | Serotonin syndrome risk with some meds | No |
| Loperamide | Antidiarrheal | Diarrhea | Traveler’s diarrhea symptoms | Avoid in dysentery/high fever | No |
| Ondansetron | Antiemetic | Nausea/vomiting | Gastroenteritis nausea | QT prolongation risk | No |
| Pseudoephedrine | Decongestant | Nasal congestion | Common cold | Hypertension, insomnia | No |
| Benzocaine lozenges | Local anesthetic | Throat pain | Pharyngitis discomfort | Methemoglobinemia risk (rare) | No |
| Oral rehydration solution | Supportive care | Dehydration | Diarrheal illness | Monitor electrolytes in severe cases | No |
