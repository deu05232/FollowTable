# Meal Prep vs Eating Out  Pros Cons (Narrative-Focused)

| Option | Typical Experience | Perceived Healthiness | Convenience Notes | Taste/Variety | Common Complaints |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Home-cooked meal prep | You shop, cook in batches, portion meals for the week. | Often considered healthier because you control ingredients. | Requires planning, containers, and fridge space. | Can get repetitive unless you rotate recipes. | "I get bored of the same chicken and rice." |
| Salad bar takeout | Grab-and-go bowls with greens, grains, and proteins. | Feels healthy but sauces can add hidden calories. | Fast pickup; easy for lunch. | Lots of mix-and-match options. | "Dressings make it heavy." |
| Healthy fast-casual bowl | Order at a counter; customizable bowls and wraps. | Often marketed as healthy with whole foods. | Very convenient on busy days. | Consistent, predictable flavors. | "Portions vary by location." |
| Smoothie shop | Fruit/greens blended drinks with add-ins. | Can be healthy, but add-ins increase sugar/calories. | Very quick; portable. | Sweet and easy to consume. | "Not filling for long." |
| Subscription meal kit | Recipes delivered weekly; you cook at home. | Generally balanced recipes with portion guidance. | Cooking still required; less planning. | Good variety and new recipes. | "Too much packaging." |
| Grocery deli prepared meals | Pre-made proteins/sides sold cold or hot. | Quality varies by store; can be high sodium. | Minimal effort; microwave-friendly. | Decent variety. | "Sometimes tastes bland." |
| Restaurant "healthy" entree | Sit-down or delivery with a 'lighter' menu section. | Depends on preparation; claims may be vague. | Most convenient if you order delivery. | High variety. | "Hard to know what's actually in it." |
