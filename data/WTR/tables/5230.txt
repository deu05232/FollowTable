# Lower Left Abdominal Pain | MedGuidance

| Cause | Diverticulitis | Constipation | Intestinal Obstruction (volvulus/hernia/ tumor/adhesions) | Mittelschmerz /Ovulation Pain | Ovarian Cysts | Kidney Stones | Kidney Infection | Ectopic Pregnancy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Character of Pain | Sudden and severe pain or mild but worsening (less common) | Dull abdominal pain due to failure to pass stools | Cramp type of pain that comes and goes | Dull and cramp-like type of pain or sharp, sudden pain | Dull, aching, pelvic pain that may also radiate to the lower back and thigh. | Sharp flank pain, comes in waves, fluctuating, may spread to back and groin | Lower abdominal pain, back pain, flank pain or groin pain | Severe lower abdominal or pelvic pain |
| Accompanying Symptoms | Changes in bowel habits, diarrhea or constipation, abdominal tenderness, nausea and vomiting, fever, bloating and bleeding from the rectum. | Infrequent bowel movement, hard stools Straining, bloating and pressure in the rectum | Nausea, vomiting and diarrhea or constipation, inability to pass gas or stools, swollen abdomen | Occurs 2 weeks before period, with or without vaginal spotting | Irregular periods, heaviness, spotting, nausea, vomiting, pressure; sometimes none | Frequent, painful urination, pink, red or brown urine, cloudy foul-smelling urine, nausea, vomiting, fever and chills | Painful urination, persistent urge to urinate, pus or blood in the urine, fever | Missed period, signs of pregnancy, vaginal bleeding |
| Treatment/Remedy | Rest, changes in the diet and prescribed antibiotics Surgical treatment, if severe | Relief of symptoms with passage of stool; use laxatives, modify diet, hydration | Surgical treatment | Pain relievers, rest | Mild pain - none, or analgesics Severe - possible surgery | Mild - none or analgesics Severe - surgery | Antibiotic treatment | Surgery |
