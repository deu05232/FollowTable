# Autoimmune Conditions — Risk Factors, Triggers, and Typical Laboratory Findings

| Condition | Suspected triggers/exposures | Key autoantibodies | Typical lab abnormalities | First-line medications |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rheumatoid arthritis | Smoking, silica | RF, anti-CCP | ↑ESR/CRP, anemia | Methotrexate, NSAIDs |
| Systemic lupus erythematosus | UV light, infections | ANA, anti-dsDNA | Low complement, cytopenias | Hydroxychloroquine, steroids |
| Sjögren syndrome | Viral triggers | anti-SSA/SSB | Hypergammaglobulinemia | Pilocarpine, HCQ |
| Ankylosing spondylitis | Gut dysbiosis | HLA-B27 | ↑CRP | NSAIDs, TNF inhibitors |
| Psoriatic arthritis | Obesity, trauma | Usually seronegative | ↑CRP | Methotrexate, biologics |
| Giant cell arteritis | Age-related immune shift | — | Very high ESR | High-dose steroids |
| Polymyalgia rheumatica | Age-related | — | ↑ESR/CRP | Low-dose steroids |
| Autoimmune thyroiditis | Iodine excess | anti-TPO | Abnormal TSH/T4 | Levothyroxine |
