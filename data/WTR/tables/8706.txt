# Selected Major Discoveries About Human Ancestors (Species, Findspot, and Notes)

| Species / Group | Approx. age | Key specimen or find | Discovery year | Country / locality | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Australopithecus afarensis | ~3.0–3.7 million years | Partial skeletons and cranial material | 1970s | Ethiopia / Tanzania | Often used to discuss bipedalism and variation |
| Australopithecus africanus | ~2.1–3.0 million years | Skull material (classic South African finds) | 1920s | South Africa | Important for southern African australopith record |
| Homo habilis | ~1.4–2.4 million years | Olduvai Gorge remains | 1960 | Tanzania | Early Homo; debates on taxonomy and tool associations |
| Homo erectus | ~0.1–1.9 million years | “Java Man” and related material | 1891 | Indonesia | Early Homo outside Africa; long-lived lineage |
| Ardipithecus (ramidus) | ~4.4 million years | Aramis material | 1994 | Ethiopia | Early hominin anatomy and paleoecology |
| Sahelanthropus (tchadensis) | ~6–7 million years | Cranial remains | 2001 | Chad | Candidate near human-chimp divergence timeframe |
