# Disinfectants in Household Use (Strength Described as Dilution Ratios, Not Percent)

| Household product type | Active chemical | Typical label direction | Dilution format | Practical strength indicator |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bleach for surfaces | Sodium hypochlorite | 1:99 for routine cleaning | Ratio (v/v) | “1 part bleach to 99 parts water” |
| Bleach for mold | Sodium hypochlorite | 1:10 for heavy soil | Ratio (v/v) | “1:10 solution” |
| Vinegar cleaner | Acetic acid | Use undiluted or 1:1 | Ratio (v/v) | “straight” vs “half-and-half” |
| Hydrogen peroxide spray | Hydrogen peroxide | Use as supplied | null given | No % listed in this table |
| Quat disinfectant | Benzalkonium chloride | 1 oz per gallon | Mass/volume instruction | “oz/gal” |
| Floor cleaner | Pine oil (terpenes) | 2 caps per bucket | Household measure | “caps/bucket” |
| Dish sanitizer | Chlorine-based sanitizer | 2 tsp per quart | Household measure | “tsp/qt” |
| Bathroom descaler | Citric acid | 2 tbsp per liter | Household measure | “tbsp/L” |
