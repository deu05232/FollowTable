# Hormone Therapy Agents Used in Cancer Care (Not the Hormones Themselves)

| Agent (generic) | Class | Primary target | Typical indication | Main effect on hormone signaling | Common route |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tamoxifen | SERM | Estrogen receptor | ER+ breast cancer | Blocks ER in breast tissue | Oral |
| Toremifene | SERM | Estrogen receptor | Metastatic breast cancer | Anti-estrogenic in breast | Oral |
| Fulvestrant | SERD | Estrogen receptor | Advanced ER+ breast cancer | ER degradation/antagonism | IM injection |
| Anastrozole | Aromatase inhibitor | Aromatase | Postmenopausal ER+ cancer | Lowers estrogen synthesis | Oral |
| Letrozole | Aromatase inhibitor | Aromatase | Postmenopausal ER+ cancer | Lowers estrogen synthesis | Oral |
| Exemestane | Aromatase inhibitor | Aromatase | ER+ breast cancer | Irreversible aromatase inhibition | Oral |
| Leuprolide | GnRH agonist | Pituitary GnRH receptor | Prostate cancer, endometriosis | Suppresses LH/FSH after flare | Injection |
| Degarelix | GnRH antagonist | Pituitary GnRH receptor | Prostate cancer | Rapidly suppresses LH/FSH | Injection |
