# Major Human Hormones and Their Primary Physiological Effects

| Hormone | Main source (gland/tissue) | Primary target(s) | Function / effect (plain language) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Insulin | Pancreatic β cells | Liver, muscle, fat | Lowers blood glucose by helping cells take up and store nutrients (glycogen/fat). |
| Glucagon | Pancreatic α cells | Liver | Raises blood glucose by signaling the liver to release glucose. |
| Cortisol | Adrenal cortex | Many tissues | Helps the body respond to stress; increases available fuel (glucose), supports blood pressure, dampens inflammation. |
| Aldosterone | Adrenal cortex | Kidneys | Conserves sodium and water; increases potassium excretion; helps maintain blood pressure. |
| Antidiuretic hormone (ADH/vasopressin) | Posterior pituitary (made in hypothalamus) | Kidneys, blood vessels | Reduces urine output by retaining water; can constrict blood vessels to support blood pressure. |
| Thyroid hormones (T3/T4) | Thyroid gland | Most cells | Increases metabolic rate and heat production; supports growth and brain development. |
| Parathyroid hormone (PTH) | Parathyroid glands | Bone, kidneys, intestine (via vitamin D) | Raises blood calcium (releases calcium from bone and reduces calcium loss in urine). |
| Calcitonin | Thyroid (C cells) | Bone | Lowers blood calcium by reducing bone breakdown (minor role in adults). |
| Growth hormone (GH) | Anterior pituitary | Liver, bone, muscle | Promotes growth and tissue repair; shifts metabolism toward using fat for fuel. |
| Prolactin | Anterior pituitary | Breast | Stimulates milk production after childbirth. |
| Oxytocin | Posterior pituitary (made in hypothalamus) | Uterus, breast | Triggers uterine contractions and milk ejection (“let-down”); supports bonding behaviors. |
| Epinephrine (adrenaline) | Adrenal medulla | Heart, vessels, liver, lungs | “Fight-or-flight”: increases heart rate, opens airways, raises blood glucose and blood flow to muscles. |
