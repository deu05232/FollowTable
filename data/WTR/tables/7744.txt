# Respiratory Infection Therapeutics  Antivirals, Antibiotics, and Adjunctive Treatments

| Syndrome | Suspected Cause | Medication/Product | Type | Route | Comment |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Community-acquired pneumonia | Bacterial | Amoxicillin | Antibiotic | Oral | First-line in many settings |
| Atypical pneumonia | Bacterial | Azithromycin | Antibiotic | Oral | Covers atypicals |
| Influenza | Viral | Oseltamivir | Antiviral | Oral | Early initiation preferred |
| COVID-19 (high-risk outpatient) | Viral | Nirmatrelvir/ritonavir | Antiviral | Oral | Drug–drug interactions |
| RSV prevention (older adults) | Viral | RSV vaccine | Vaccine | IM | Seasonal |
| Acute bronchospasm with infection | Mixed | Albuterol inhaler | Symptomatic | Inhaled | Not anti-infective |
| Oropharyngeal candidiasis (included) | Fungal | Clotrimazole troches | Antifungal | Oral | Local therapy option |
| Bacterial sinusitis | Bacterial | Amoxicillin-clavulanate | Antibiotic | Oral | Consider if persistent symptoms |
