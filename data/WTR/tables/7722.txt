# Common infectious disease risks by source and transmission route

| Condition | Typical reservoir or source | Main transmission or exposure route | Common setting where exposure happens |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Rabies | Infected mammals (especially dogs and bats) | Bites or saliva entering broken skin | Animal bites during travel or handling wildlife |
| Leptospirosis | Rodents and other mammals shedding bacteria | Contact with contaminated freshwater or mud via cuts/mucosa | Flooding, farming, freshwater recreation |
| Salmonellosis (foodborne) | Poultry, eggs, reptiles, contaminated foods | Eating contaminated food; cross-contamination in kitchens | Undercooked eggs/meat; poor food handling |
| Malaria | Infected humans via Anopheles mosquitoes | Mosquito bites | Night-time outdoor exposure in endemic regions |
| Cholera | Human fecal contamination of water | Drinking contaminated water or eating contaminated food | Poor sanitation; unsafe water supplies |
| Anthrax | Grazing livestock and contaminated animal products/soil | Handling animal products; inhalation or ingestion of spores | Farming, butchering, processing hides/wool |
