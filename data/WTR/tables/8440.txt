# Pregnancy Safety Evidence Summary for Common Anti-Infectives (Human Animal Data Dominant)

| Drug (Generic) | Common Brand Examples | FDA Pregnancy Category (legacy) | Human Pregnancy Data Summary | Animal Reproductive Findings | Lactation Considerations | Clinical Caveat |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nitrofurantoin | Macrobid | B | Large observational data; avoid near term in G6PD deficiency risk | No major teratogenic signal | Low levels in milk | Avoid at term if concern for hemolysis |
| Doxycycline | Vibramycin | D | Limited; concerns mainly from older tetracyclines | Tooth/bone effects at high exposures | Not preferred | Consider alternatives when pregnant |
| Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole | Bactrim | D (1st/3rd trimester concern) | Folate antagonism concern early; kernicterus concern late | Teratogenicity at high doses in animals | Monitor infant jaundice | Use only if benefits outweigh risks |
| Fluconazole | Diflucan | C (single dose) / D (high dose) | High-dose chronic use linked to birth defects; single-dose data mixed | Teratogenic in animals at high dose | Present in milk | Prefer topical azoles when possible |
| Metronidazole | Flagyl | B | Substantial data; no consistent teratogenic association | No major signal | Compatible | Avoid alcohol due to disulfiram-like reaction |
| Acyclovir | Zovirax | B | Registry data reassuring across trimesters | No major signal | Compatible | Preferred for HSV in pregnancy |
| Ciprofloxacin | Cipro | C | Limited; theoretical cartilage toxicity | Arthropathy in juvenile animals | Use with caution | Reserve for lack of alternatives |
| Vancomycin | Vancocin | B | Limited controlled data; used for serious infections | No major signal | Monitor levels | IV use common in severe disease |
