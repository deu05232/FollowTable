# Common Anti-Infective Medications  Generic vs Brand Names and Patient-Facing Formulations

| Drug class | Generic name | Common brand name(s) | Route / formulation patients see | Typical practical use (examples) | Notes for selection (non-lab) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Penicillin (aminopenicillin) | Amoxicillin | Amoxil | Oral capsule/tablet/suspension | Streptococcal pharyngitis, otitis media, sinusitis | Often first-line when narrow coverage is appropriate |
| Penicillin + beta-lactamase inhibitor | Amoxicillin/clavulanate | Augmentin | Oral tablet/chewable/suspension | Bite wounds, polymicrobial skin/soft tissue infection, sinusitis | Broader spectrum than amoxicillin; more GI side effects |
| Cephalosporin (1st gen) | Cephalexin | Keflex | Oral capsule/suspension | Uncomplicated cellulitis, impetigo | Common outpatient option; does not cover MRSA well |
| Cephalosporin (3rd gen) | Ceftriaxone | Rocephin | IM/IV injection | Gonorrhea, severe pneumonia, pyelonephritis | Convenient once-daily dosing in many settings |
| Tetracycline | Doxycycline | Vibramycin, Doryx | Oral tablet/capsule | Community MRSA skin infections, atypical pneumonia, tick-borne infections | Photosensitivity; avoid in pregnancy/young children in many cases |
| Sulfonamide combo | Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole | Bactrim, Septra | Oral tablet/suspension | MRSA skin infections, some UTIs | Consider allergy history; drug interactions possible |
| Lincosamide | Clindamycin | Cleocin | Oral capsule/solution; IV | Skin/soft tissue infection, dental infections | Higher risk of C. difficile compared with some alternatives |
| Macrolide | Azithromycin | Zithromax | Oral tablet/suspension | Some respiratory infections; chlamydia | Often chosen for short course; QT risk in susceptible patients |
| Fluoroquinolone | Levofloxacin | Levaquin | Oral tablet; IV | Pneumonia, complicated UTI | Reserve when alternatives limited; tendon/CNS risks |
| Nitroimidazole | Metronidazole | Flagyl | Oral tablet; IV; topical gel | Anaerobic infections, bacterial vaginosis | Avoid alcohol during therapy; metallic taste common |
| Antifungal (azole) | Fluconazole | Diflucan | Oral tablet/suspension; IV | Vulvovaginal candidiasis, some systemic yeast infections | Convenient oral option; check interactions |
| Antiviral (anti-HSV/VZV) | Valacyclovir | Valtrex | Oral tablet | Herpes simplex outbreaks, shingles | Prodrug of acyclovir; simpler dosing |
