# Zoonotic and Vector-Borne Diseases  Source Animals Vectors, Transmission Routes, and Exposure Risk Factors

| Category | Disease | Reservoir/source (animal/vector) | Transmission route | Key exposure pathway (what contact enables infection) | Higher-risk activities/occupations | Primary prevention controls |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Zoonotic (bite) | Plague | Rodents; fleas as vector | Flea bite; handling infected animals | Flea exposure from rodents; contact with sick animals | Field workers, hunters, people in rodent-infested housing | Rodent control; flea control; avoid handling dead wildlife |
| Zoonotic (aerosol) | Q fever | Goats, sheep, cattle | Inhalation of contaminated dust/aerosols | Birth products/manure aerosols from barns | Farmers, veterinarians, abattoir workers | Farm hygiene; control aerosols; PPE during parturition |
| Zoonotic (contact) | Ringworm (dermatophytosis) | Cats, dogs, cattle | Direct contact; fomites | Skin contact with infected animals or contaminated grooming tools | Shelter workers, pet owners, dairy workers | Treat infected animals; clean/disinfect fomites; gloves |
| Foodborne zoonosis | Trichinellosis | Pigs, wild boar, bear | Ingestion (undercooked meat) | Eating raw/undercooked game or pork | Hunters, consumers of home-processed meats | Cook meat to safe temps; freeze where effective; meat inspection |
| Foodborne zoonosis | Toxoplasmosis | Cats (oocysts); meat animals | Ingestion (oocysts) or tissue cysts | Cleaning litter; gardening in contaminated soil; undercooked meat | Pregnant people, immunocompromised, gardeners | Hand hygiene; cook meat; litter box precautions |
| Vector-borne | Lyme disease | Small mammals; Ixodes ticks | Tick bite | Outdoor exposure in tick habitats; delayed tick removal | Hikers, forestry workers, campers | Repellent; protective clothing; tick checks; habitat management |
| Vector-borne | West Nile fever | Birds; Culex mosquitoes | Mosquito bite | Evening outdoor exposure near breeding sites | Outdoor workers, residents in endemic seasons | Remove standing water; repellents; community spraying when indicated |
| Water contact zoonosis | Schistosomiasis | Humans as source; freshwater snails as intermediate host | Skin penetration by cercariae in water | Swimming/wading in freshwater in endemic areas | Fishermen, farmers (irrigation), children playing in water | Avoid freshwater exposure; snail control; safe water access |
| Zoonotic (respiratory) | Psittacosis | Birds (parrots, poultry) | Inhalation of dried bird secretions | Cleaning cages; poultry processing; pet bird exposure | Bird owners, poultry workers, pet shop staff | PPE and ventilation; hygiene; quarantine/treat infected birds |
| Zoonotic (spillover) | Nipah virus infection | Fruit bats; pigs as amplifier | Contact with infected animals or contaminated food; person-to-person in some outbreaks | Raw date palm sap; pig farming; caregiving without PPE | Pig farmers, sap collectors, caregivers/health workers during outbreaks | Cover sap collection; farm biosecurity; PPE and isolation during outbreaks |
