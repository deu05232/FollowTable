# Landmark astronomy discoveries (selected)

| Discovery | Field | Year announced | Main scientist(s) / team | Observing method | What was discovered |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cosmic microwave background radiation | Cosmology | 1965 | Arno Penzias; Robert Wilson | Radio antenna observation | Relic radiation supporting the Big Bang model |
| First exoplanet around a Sun-like star (51 Pegasi b) | Exoplanets | 1995 | Michel Mayor; Didier Queloz | Radial velocity | Evidence that planets orbit other stars like the Sun |
| Expansion of the universe (Hubble’s law) | Cosmology | 1929 | Edwin Hubble | Galaxy redshift measurements | Relationship between distance and recession velocity |
| First image of a black hole (M87*) | Astrophysics | 2019 | Event Horizon Telescope Collaboration | VLBI interferometry | Horizon-scale image consistent with a supermassive black hole |
| Pulsars | Astrophysics | 1967 | Jocelyn Bell Burnell; Antony Hewish | Radio astronomy | Regular radio pulses from rapidly rotating neutron stars |
