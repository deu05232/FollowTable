# Lower Back Pain  Symptoms, Causes, and What People Commonly Take

| Primary Symptom Pattern (main entry) | Common Causes | Commonly Taken Medicines (examples) | Non-Drug Options |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sudden pain after lifting | Muscle strain | Ibuprofen; Naproxen | Rest, heat, gentle stretching |
| Pain radiating down one leg | Sciatica | Naproxen; Short course oral steroids | PT, nerve glides |
| Morning stiffness improving with movement | Inflammatory back pain | NSAIDs (e.g., diclofenac) | Rheumatology evaluation |
| Pain with fever and urinary symptoms | Kidney infection | Antibiotics; acetaminophen for fever | Urgent care evaluation |
| Pain after a fall | Fracture | Opioids short-term | Imaging, immobilization |
| Persistent pain with weight loss | Malignancy concern | Analgesics as needed | Urgent medical workup |
| Localized tenderness over a joint | Facet arthropathy | NSAIDs; topical lidocaine | PT, posture training |
