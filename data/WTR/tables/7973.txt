# Incidence and prevalence estimates for selected infectious diseases (mixed measures, clearly labeled)

| Disease | Measure type | Incidence/Prevalence estimate | Population/Timeframe | Notes / Source statistic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Seasonal influenza (symptomatic) | Annual incidence | ~5–10% of adults per year | Global (typical season) | Often-cited WHO estimate range; varies by season and surveillance methods |
| Norovirus gastroenteritis | Annual incidence | ~1 in 5 people per year (~20%) | Global | Community studies commonly report ~20% annual infection burden |
| Tuberculosis (all forms) | Annual incidence rate | ~130 per 100,000 per year | Global (recent years) | Incidence expressed per 100k; depends on country mix |
| HIV infection | Annual incidence rate | ~17 per 100,000 per year | Global (recent years) | Approximate global new infections per population |
| Measles | Annual incidence rate | ~5 per 100,000 per year | Global (recent years) | Highly outbreak-dependent; can be near-zero in high-coverage settings |
| Hepatitis B (chronic infection) | Prevalence | ~3% (≈300 per 10,000) | Global | Prevalence used because chronic carriage is typically reported |
| Hepatitis C (viremic infection) | Prevalence | ~0.7–1.0% | Global | Prevalence varies strongly by region |
| Malaria | Annual incidence rate | ~3,000 per 100,000 per year | Global (recent years) | Concentrated in endemic countries; incidence commonly reported per 100k |
