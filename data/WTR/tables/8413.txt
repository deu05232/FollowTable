# Selected Landmark Scientific Discoveries (Physics & Astronomy) — What Was Discovered and Why It Mattered

| Discovery (phenomenon/object) | Field | Year (first reported) | Key evidence / method | What it was about (short description) | Main implication / impact |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation | Cosmology | 1965 | Microwave horn antenna observations | Relic thermal radiation from the early universe | Strong support for the Big Bang model; enabled precision cosmology |
| Pulsars | Astrophysics | 1967 | Radio timing of periodic signals | Rapidly rotating neutron stars emitting beamed radiation | Confirmed existence of neutron stars; new tests of gravity |
| Gravitational waves (GW150914) | Relativity / Astronomy | 2015 | Laser interferometry (LIGO) | Ripples in spacetime from a binary black-hole merger | Opened gravitational-wave astronomy; validated key GR prediction |
| Higgs boson | Particle physics | 2012 | Proton–proton collisions (ATLAS/CMS) | Quantum excitation linked to electroweak symmetry breaking | Confirmed Standard Model mechanism for particle masses |
| Exoplanet 51 Pegasi b (first around Sun-like star) | Exoplanets | 1995 | Radial velocity spectroscopy | A “hot Jupiter” orbiting a Sun-like star | Transformed planet-formation theories; accelerated exoplanet searches |
| Accelerating expansion of the universe (dark energy evidence) | Cosmology | 1998 | Type Ia supernova distance measurements | Distant supernovae appeared dimmer than expected | Introduced dark energy as dominant cosmic component |
| Ozone hole over Antarctica | Atmospheric science | 1985 | Ground-based spectrophotometry | Severe springtime depletion of stratospheric ozone | Led to global policy response (Montreal Protocol) |
| First direct image of a black hole shadow (M87*) | Astronomy | 2019 | Very-long-baseline interferometry (EHT) | Ring-like emission around a supermassive black hole | Provided direct-scale test of strong gravity near event horizons |
