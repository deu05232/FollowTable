# Functional Effects of Pituitary–Hypothalamic Axis Hormones (Physiology-Focused)

| Hormone | Physiological effect/function | Primary organ/system affected | Net outcome/process regulated |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) | Stimulates thyroid hormone release | Thyroid gland | Supports metabolic rate and thermogenesis via increased T3/T4 |
| Adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH) | Stimulates adrenal cortisol production | Adrenal cortex | Promotes systemic stress adaptation and glucose availability |
| Follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) | Promotes gamete maturation | Ovaries (follicles); testes (Sertoli cells) | Follicular development and estradiol support; spermatogenesis support |
| Luteinizing hormone (LH) | Triggers ovulation and supports steroid hormone production | Ovaries (theca/corpus luteum); testes (Leydig cells) | Ovulation and progesterone production; testosterone production |
| Growth hormone (GH) | Stimulates growth and tissue repair; shifts fuel use | Liver (IGF-1), bone, muscle, adipose tissue | Linear growth; increased protein synthesis; increased lipolysis |
| Prolactin | Drives milk synthesis | Mammary gland | Lactogenesis and maintenance of milk production |
| Antidiuretic hormone (ADH/vasopressin) | Increases renal water reabsorption; reduces urine volume | Kidney collecting ducts | Water conservation; maintains plasma osmolality |
| Oxytocin | Causes uterine contraction and milk ejection reflex | Uterus; mammary gland | Labor contractions; milk let-down |
| Gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) | Stimulates pituitary release of LH and FSH | Anterior pituitary | Coordinates reproductive axis activity and fertility |
| Thyrotropin-releasing hormone (TRH) | Stimulates pituitary TSH (and can support prolactin) release | Anterior pituitary | Activates thyroid axis to regulate metabolism |
| Corticotropin-releasing hormone (CRH) | Stimulates pituitary ACTH release | Anterior pituitary | Activates HPA axis for stress response |
| Somatostatin | Inhibits GH (and TSH) release | Anterior pituitary | Limits growth-axis output; modulates metabolic balance |
| Dopamine (as prolactin-inhibiting factor) | Inhibits prolactin secretion | Anterior pituitary | Suppresses lactation when appropriate |
