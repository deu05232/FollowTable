<table>
  <caption>Common Internet traffic data sources & software.</caption>
  <tr><th>Name</th><th>Data</th></tr>
  <tr><td>CAIDA</td><td>Probably largest and most comprehensive repository of all types of Internet data and research. Hosted by UC San Diego</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Crawdad</td><td>Collection of wireless data traces made at Dartmouth</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Datapository</td><td>Collection of topology data, mostly based on BGP</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Dimes</td><td>A project that seeks to more accurately measure Internet topology by applying traceroute and ping from a collection of different users with downloaded software around the world in a model similar to SETI@Home</td></tr>
  <tr><td>MOME</td><td>Large collection of traffic traces conducted by European based researchers and collaborations</td></tr>
  <tr><td>NLANR</td><td>Older traffic trace project; now mostly housed at CAIDA</td></tr>
  <tr><td>ns-2</td><td>Commonly used network traffic simulator in the network engineering community</td></tr>
  <tr><td>PingER</td><td>Stanford project to monitor Ping response in IPv4 and IPv6 across the Internet</td></tr>
  <tr><td>PREDICT</td><td>A collection of datasets of Internet topology and traffic tailored towards predicting and defending against cyberattacks. Must apply for data access</td></tr>
  <tr><td>RouteViews</td><td>U of Oregon’s database on Internet routing tables and BGP data</td></tr>
  <tr><td>tcpdump</td><td>Main program used to collect traffic for analysis; used in many packet sniffing programs</td></tr>
  <tr><td>UMass Trace Repository</td><td>Collection of publicly available traffic traces conducted at the University of Massachusetts Amherst</td></tr>
  <tr><td>WAND</td><td>Collection of traces done by the University of Waikato, New Zealand</td></tr>
  <tr><td>WIDE MAWI</td><td>Japan’s WIDE Project traffic trace archive; data source of many graphs in this paper</td></tr>
</table>
