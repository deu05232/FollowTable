<table>
  <caption>Definitions of the inputs used in the model.</caption>
  <tr><th>Name</th><th>Definition</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Inputs</td><td></td></tr>
  <tr><td>M_(tot,1),M_(tot,2)</td><td>The total masses (baryonic plus dark) of galaxies 1 and 2 respectively.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>R_(tot,1),R_(tot,2)</td><td>The three-dimensional half-mass radii of the total masses (baryonic plus dark) of galaxies 1 and 2 respectively.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>V_(peri),R_(peri)</td><td>The theoretical pericentric velocity and distance of the first encounter, defined when the galaxies are separated by ~ R_(vir).</td></tr>
  <tr><td>K_(tot,1),K_(tot,2)</td><td>The total kinetic energies of the galaxy/halo systems of galaxy 1 and 2 respectively. In our simulations K_(tot,1)~eq 0.35~GM_(tot,1)^(2)/R_(tot,1) with very little scatter.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>f_(g,1),f_(g,2)</td><td>The gas fractions of galaxies 1 and 2, defined as (gas mass)/(total mass).</td></tr>
  <tr><td>R_(1),R_(2)</td><td>The stellar three-dimensional half-mass radii of galaxies 1 and 2.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>M_(1),M_(2)</td><td>The stellar masses of galaxies 1 and 2.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>M_(dm,1),M_(dm,2)</td><td>The dark matter mass inside 1/2 the stellar three-dimensional half-mass radius of galaxies 1 and 2 respectively.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>M_(stars,1),M_(stars,2)</td><td>The stellar mass inside 1/2 the stellar three-dimensional half-mass radius of galaxies 1 and 2 respectively.</td></tr>
</table>
