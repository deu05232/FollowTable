# Cranial Nerves III, IV, VI  General Roles and Typical Deficits

| Cranial nerve | Main motor targets (general) | Parasympathetic role | Typical lesion signs | Common exam maneuver |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CN III (Oculomotor) | Most extraocular muscles (general) | Pupil constriction, accommodation | Ptosis, eye "down and out", mydriasis | H-test; check pupillary light reflex |
| CN IV (Trochlear) | Superior oblique (general statement) | null | Vertical diplopia, worse on stairs | Look down and in; head tilt test |
| CN VI (Abducens) | Lateral rectus (general statement) | null | Failure of abduction, esotropia | Ask patient to abduct eye |
| CN III (Edinger–Westphal) | — | Preganglionic parasympathetic to ciliary ganglion | Fixed dilated pupil | Swinging flashlight test |
| CN III (Somatic motor nucleus) | — | null | Ophthalmoplegia pattern | H-test |
| CN VI (nucleus/PPRF context) | — | null | Horizontal gaze palsy | Horizontal saccades |
| CN IV (decussation note) | — | null | Contralateral SO weakness | Head tilt maneuver |
| CN III/IV/VI (collective) | Eye movements overall | Pupil (III) | Diplopia patterns | Cover-uncover test |
