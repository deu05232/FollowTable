# Deer Habitat & Yard Risk Factors Affecting Browse Pressure

| Factor | Typical Deer Response | How It Changes Yard Risk | Mitigation Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Distance to woodland edge | Increased visits near cover | High risk within 50–100 m | Add fencing or dense barrier plantings |
| Nearby water source | More frequent movement corridors | Moderate to high | Reduce attractants; manage drainage |
| Winter severity | Concentrates deer into sheltered neighborhoods | High in harsh winters | Use physical exclusion during peak months |
| Presence of fruiting trees | Draws deer for fallen fruit | High when fruit drops | Clean up fruit; consider netting |
| Low human activity at night | More nocturnal browsing | High | Motion lights and deterrents (limited effectiveness) |
| Dog activity | Avoidance of some yards | Lower where dogs patrol | Effect varies; deer may habituate |
| Open lawn vs. dense shrubs | Deer prefer edges and cover | Higher with dense cover nearby | Increase visibility; prune lower branches |
