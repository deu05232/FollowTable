# Pacific War timeline milestones (1941–1945) leading up to Hiroshima

| Date | Event | Why it mattered in the Pacific war |
|---|---|---|
| Dec 8, 1941 | Attack on Pearl Harbor draws the U.S. into war with Japan | Starts the U.S.–Japan war in the Pacific |
| Jun 4–7, 1942 | Battle of Midway | Major turning point that shifts initiative to the U.S. |
| Aug 7, 1942 | Guadalcanal campaign begins | First major Allied offensive in the Pacific |
| Oct 20, 1944 | Battle of Leyte Gulf | Japan’s navy suffers decisive losses |
| Feb 19, 1945 | Battle of Iwo Jima begins | Intensifies the fight for bases near Japan |
| Apr 1, 1945 | Battle of Okinawa begins | Heavy casualties underline the cost of invasion |
| Jul 26, 1945 | Potsdam Declaration issued | Sets surrender terms as the war nears its end |
| Aug 6, 1945 | Atomic bomb dropped on Hiroshima | Climactic event ending the Pacific war phase |
