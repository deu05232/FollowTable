# Common Elements and Their Typical Oxidation States (Neutral-Atom Perspective)

| Element | Symbol | Common Oxidation States | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sodium | Na | +1 | Alkali metal; usually forms +1 compounds |
| Magnesium | Mg | +2 | Alkaline earth metal |
| Aluminum | Al | +3 | Frequently +3 in salts |
| Carbon | C | -4, +2, +4 | Multiple oxidation possibilities |
| Nitrogen | N | -3, +3, +5 | Wide range in covalent compounds |
| Sulfur | S | -2, +4, +6 | Common in oxides and sulfides |
| Chlorine | Cl | -1, +1, +5, +7 | Halogen with several oxidation states |
| Iron | Fe | +2, +3 | Common transition-metal states |
| Copper | Cu | +1, +2 | Often +2 in aqueous chemistry |
| Manganese | Mn | +2, +4, +7 | Notable in permanganate chemistry |
