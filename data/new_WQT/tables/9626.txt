# Country-Specific Yellow Fever Rules for Travelers (Requirement Triggered by Itinerary vs Destination Risk)

| Destination | Yellow fever discussed? | Entry requirement status | Trigger for requirement (explicit) | Recommendation status | Practical planning note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Kenya | Yes | Required in some cases | Arriving from/transiting through a yellow-fever-risk country (certificate requested) | Recommended for many travelers visiting risk regions | Plan vaccine timing to ensure certificate is valid before departure |
| South Africa | Yes | Conditional | Only if coming from a yellow-fever-risk country; otherwise not required | Not routinely recommended unless exposure risk elsewhere | Itinerary changes (e.g., adding a stop in a risk country) can create a new requirement |
| Ghana | Yes | Required | Mandatory for entry (commonly requested at arrival) | Recommended | High likelihood of certificate checks—carry documentation |
| Brazil | Yes | Not generally required | No blanket entry rule for most travelers | Recommended for endemic areas | Trip within-country (Amazon vs coastal cities) changes recommendation |
| Peru | Yes | Not generally required | No blanket entry rule for most travelers | Recommended for jungle/lowland areas | Visiting Cusco/Lima only typically differs from Amazon itineraries |
| Indonesia | Yes | Conditional | Required if arriving from a yellow-fever-risk country | Usually not recommended for Indonesia-only travel | Transit through a risk country can trigger the entry requirement |
| Thailand | Yes | Conditional | Required only if arriving from a yellow-fever-risk country | Typically not recommended | Even airport transit in a risk country may matter depending on rules |
| Uganda | Yes | Required | Mandatory for entry (often requested) | Recommended | Entry planning should assume proof will be checked |
