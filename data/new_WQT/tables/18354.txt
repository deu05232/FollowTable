# Abbreviations Used in a Single Cardiology Concept  Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) Workup

| ACS Workup Step | Abbreviation(s) commonly seen | What it refers to | Why it matters | Typical timing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Initial assessment | ACS, CP, SOB | Acute coronary syndrome; chest pain; shortness of breath | Determines urgency | At presentation |
| ECG interpretation | STEMI, NSTEMI | ST-elevation MI; non-ST-elevation MI | Guides reperfusion vs medical therapy | Within 10 minutes |
| Cardiac biomarkers | hs-Tn, CK-MB | High-sensitivity troponin; creatine kinase-MB | Confirms myocardial injury | 0 and 1–3 hours |
| Antiplatelet therapy | ASA, P2Y12 | Aspirin; P2Y12 inhibitor class | Prevents thrombosis | Early |
| Anticoagulation | UFH, LMWH | Unfractionated heparin; low-molecular-weight heparin | Reduces clot propagation | Early |
| Imaging/angiography | CAG, PCI | Coronary angiography; percutaneous coronary intervention | Identifies/treats culprit lesion | Same day if high risk |
| Risk scoring | TIMI, GRACE | Risk stratification tools | Predicts outcomes | During admission |
| Disposition | ICU, CCU | Intensive care unit; coronary care unit | Level of monitoring | After diagnosis |
