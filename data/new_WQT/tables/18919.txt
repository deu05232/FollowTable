# Companion Plants That Repel Common Garden Pests (Plant-by-Plant Outcomes)

| Plant (common name) | Scientific name | Pests repelled (outcome) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Basil | *Ocimum basilicum* | mosquitoes, houseflies | Often grown near seating areas and entryways |
| Lavender | *Lavandula angustifolia* | mosquitoes, moths, fleas | Dried flowers also help deter moths in closets |
| Rosemary | *Salvia rosmarinus* | mosquitoes, cabbage moths | Fragrant foliage; can be used as a smoky deterrent when burned outdoors |
| Peppermint | *Mentha × piperita* | ants, aphids, spider mites | Spreads aggressively; best in containers |
| Nasturtium | *Tropaeolum majus* | aphids, whiteflies | Commonly used as a “trap/repellent” companion in mixed beds |
| Garlic | *Allium sativum* | aphids, Japanese beetles | Strong odor may reduce beetle and sap-sucker pressure |
| Catnip | *Nepeta cataria* | mosquitoes, cockroaches | Reported to deter multiple insects around patios |
| Sage | *Salvia officinalis* | cabbage moths, carrot fly | Aromatic herb frequently used in vegetable borders |
