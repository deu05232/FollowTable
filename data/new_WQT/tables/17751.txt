# Specific Gravity  Definition, Units, and Example Conversions

| Item | Description | Example Value |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Definition | Ratio of a substance’s density to the density of water at a reference temperature | SG = ρ/ρ_water |
| Units | Dimensionless quantity (no units) | “Unitless” |
| Reference water density | Density of water used for the ratio | ρ_water(4 °C) ≈ 1000 kg/m³ |
| Temperature note | SG depends on reference temperature and measurement temperature | SG@20/20 |
| Convert density to SG | Divide density by water density at same reference | ρ=850 kg/m³ → SG≈0.850 |
| Convert SG to density | Multiply SG by water density | SG=1.10 → ρ≈1100 kg/m³ |
| Typical reporting | Common ways SG is listed on datasheets | “SG @ 15.6 °C” |
| Example liquids (illustrative) | Not a lookup list; only examples | Ethanol SG≈0.79; Glycerin SG≈1.26 |
