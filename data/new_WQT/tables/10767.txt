# Governor General of New France — Term Notes and Delegations (Narrative-Heavy Reference Sheet)

| Term block (approx.) | Narrative summary (includes office holders) | Key events / notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1663–1672 | The first decade of royal administration included Augustin de Saffray de Mésy (1663–1665) followed by Daniel de Rémy de Courcelle (1665–1672), during which the colony’s institutions were consolidated. | Establishment and early operation of the Sovereign Council; growing military pressures |
| 1672–1682 | Louis de Buade de Frontenac governed through the 1670s, with disputes involving officials and priorities for western trade routes. | Rivalries with administrators; attention to Great Lakes diplomacy |
| 1682–1685 | Joseph-Antoine Le Febvre de La Barre is typically cited for a short term marked by frontier tension and controversial decisions. | Campaign planning and diplomacy issues |
| 1685–1689 | Jacques-René de Brisay de Denonville’s period is often summarized together with the shift toward more aggressive frontier policy. | Fort building and conflicts; shifting alliances |
| 1689–1698 | Frontenac returned (second term, 1689–1698) and is frequently mentioned in connection with wartime leadership and alliances. | Defense and raids during broader imperial conflict |
| 1698–1703 | Louis-Hector de Callière’s tenure is commonly described as stabilizing diplomatic relations after prolonged warfare. | Treaty-making and administrative continuity |
