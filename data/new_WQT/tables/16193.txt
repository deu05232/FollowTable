# Solubility Rules by Anion Class (Not an Ion Charge List)

| Anion Class | Usually Soluble With | Common Exceptions | Example Salts |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nitrates (NO3) | all cations | none | NaNO3, Ca(NO3)2 |
| Acetates (C2H3O2) | all cations | Ag+ sometimes | KC2H3O2, AgC2H3O2 |
| Chlorides/Bromides/Iodides (Cl/Br/I) | most cations | Ag, Pb, Hg2 | NaCl, AgCl |
| Sulfates (SO4) | most cations | Ba, Sr, Pb, Ca (low) | Na2SO4, BaSO4 |
| Carbonates (CO3) | alkali metals, NH4 | most others insoluble | Na2CO3, CaCO3 |
| Phosphates (PO4) | alkali metals, NH4 | most others insoluble | K3PO4, AlPO4 |
| Hydroxides (OH) | alkali metals | most others insoluble; Ca/Sr/Ba slightly | NaOH, Mg(OH)2 |
| Sulfides (S) | alkali metals, alkaline earths (some) | many transition metals insoluble | Na2S, FeS |
