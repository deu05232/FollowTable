# Selected Canadian Shipwreck Events (Atlantic Canada & Gulf of St. Lawrence)

| Ship | Date (YYYY-MM-DD) | Vessel type | Canadian location (province/territory) | Waterbody/area | Cause | Outcome/notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| RMS Titanic | 1912-04-15 | Ocean liner | Off Newfoundland and Labrador | North Atlantic (Grand Banks region) | Struck iceberg | Sank with major loss of life; wreck later located deep offshore.
| RMS Empress of Ireland | 1914-05-29 | Ocean liner | Near Rimouski, Quebec | St. Lawrence River | Collision (with SS Storstad) | Sank rapidly; heavy casualties.
| SS Atlantic | 1873-04-01 | Ocean liner | Near Lower Prospect, Nova Scotia | Atlantic Ocean | Navigational error / ran aground | Broke up on rocks; many fatalities.
| SS Caribou | 1942-10-14 | Passenger ferry | Off Port aux Basques, Newfoundland and Labrador | Cabot Strait | Torpedoed (WWII) | Sank after U-boat attack; civilian and military casualties.
| SS Mont-Blanc (Halifax Explosion) | 1917-12-06 | Munitions cargo ship | Halifax, Nova Scotia | Halifax Harbour | Collision and explosion | Ship destroyed; massive blast devastated Halifax.
| HMCS Esquimalt | 1945-04-16 | Naval minesweeper | Off Halifax, Nova Scotia | Atlantic Ocean | Torpedoed (WWII) | Sunk near war’s end; fatalities reported.
| SS Princess Sophia | 1918-10-25 | Passenger ship | Near Skagway (Alaska) but wreck site associated with Canadian route via Yukon | Lynn Canal (route serving Yukon) | Grounding on reef in storm | Catastrophic loss; operated on Canadian-Yukon-connected service routes.
| HMCS Athabaskan (G07) | 1944-04-29 | Destroyer | Off Nova Scotia | Atlantic Ocean | Torpedoed (WWII) | Sank during engagement; survivors and POWs.
| SS Newfoundland | 1914-03-31 | Sealing steamer | Off Newfoundland and Labrador (near ice fields) | North Atlantic | Ice/operational disaster | Associated with sealing tragedy; vessel damaged amid disaster context.
| SS Florizel | 1918-02-24 | Passenger steamer | Near Cappahayden, Newfoundland and Labrador | Atlantic Ocean | Ran aground in storm | Wrecked close to shore; many deaths.
