# Orbital Foramina and Traversing Nerves Relevant to Eye Movement

| Opening/foramen | Region | Structures transmitted | Clinical correlation | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Superior orbital fissure | Orbit apex | CN III (sup/inf divisions), CN IV, CN VI, V1 branches | Ophthalmoplegia, sensory loss | Location of multiple motor nerves |
| Optic canal | Sphenoid | CN II, ophthalmic artery | Vision loss | Not a motor pathway for eye muscles |
| Inferior orbital fissure | Orbit | Infraorbital nerve (V2), zygomatic nerve, vessels | Midface numbness | Not primary for EOM innervation |
| Foramen rotundum | Middle cranial fossa | V2 (maxillary) | Facial sensory loss | Indirect orbital relevance |
| Foramen ovale | Middle cranial fossa | V3, accessory meningeal artery | Mastication weakness | Not EOM-specific |
| Cavernous sinus (not a foramen) | Parasellar | CN III, IV, V1, V2, VI + ICA | Painful ophthalmoplegia | Cranial neuropathies cluster |
| Annulus of Zinn (tendinous ring) | Orbit apex | Passage for CN III, VI, nasociliary nerve | Orbital apex syndrome | Anatomical landmark |
| Superior orbital fissure (common tendinous ring division) | Orbit apex | Structures inside vs outside ring | Selective deficits | Helps localize lesions |
