# States of matter comparison  electrical behavior of gas, plasma, liquid, and solid

| Property | Gas | Plasma | Liquid (ionic) | Solid (metal) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Typical electrical conductivity | Very low | Very high | Moderate to high | High |
| Primary charge carriers | Neutral molecules | Electrons and ions | Ions | Electrons |
| Breakdown / onset behavior | Requires ionization | Already ionized | Depends on concentration | Ohmic |
| Temperature dependence (trend) | Increases after breakdown | Complex; often decreases with collisions | Often increases with temperature | Often increases resistivity with temperature |
| Example context | Air insulation | Fusion/arc | Salt water | Copper wire |
| Dominant model | Dielectric | Plasma kinetics | Electrolyte theory | Drude model |
| Notes | Insulator until breakdown | Can be treated as quasi-neutral | Strongly chemistry-dependent | Crystal defects matter |
