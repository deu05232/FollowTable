# Autonomic Nervous System Chemical Messengers by Synapse Location

| Messenger substance | Primary role in ANS signaling | Where released in the pathway | Division / typical targets |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acetylcholine (ACh) | Neurotransmitter | Preganglionic neuron → autonomic ganglion (nicotinic receptors) | Sympathetic + Parasympathetic (all preganglionic) |
| Acetylcholine (ACh) | Neurotransmitter | Postganglionic neuron → effector organ (muscarinic receptors) | Parasympathetic (most targets) |
| Norepinephrine (NE) | Neurotransmitter | Postganglionic neuron → effector organ (adrenergic receptors) | Sympathetic (most targets) |
| Epinephrine (Epi) | Hormone / circulating messenger | Adrenal medulla → bloodstream | Sympathetic “adrenomedullary” pathway; widespread targets |
| Dopamine (DA) | Neurotransmitter | Postganglionic neuron → effector organ | Sympathetic subset (e.g., renal vascular smooth muscle, D1 receptors) |
| Nitric oxide (NO) | Co-transmitter / paracrine mediator | Postganglionic terminals → effector tissue | Parasympathetic nitrergic signaling (e.g., GI tract, erectile tissue) |
| ATP (purinergic) | Co-transmitter | Autonomic nerve terminals → effector (often co-released with NE or ACh) | Both divisions in some tissues; prominent in sympathetic vasoconstrictor nerves |
| Neuropeptide Y (NPY) | Co-transmitter / neuromodulator | Sympathetic postganglionic terminals → effector | Often co-released with NE; vascular smooth muscle modulation |
| Vasoactive intestinal peptide (VIP) | Co-transmitter / neuromodulator | Parasympathetic postganglionic terminals → effector | Often co-released with ACh; smooth muscle relaxation/secretory modulation |
