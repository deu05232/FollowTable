# Vaccinations for Japan (Travel Advice) — Vaccine-by-Vaccine Guidance for Travelers

| Vaccine | Status for Entry/Travel | Applies to All Travelers? | Who Should Get It (Traveler Categories) | Notes / Proof Needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Routine vaccines (MMR, Tdap, Varicella, Polio, Influenza) | Ensure up to date | Yes | All travelers | Core baseline before international travel |
| COVID-19 | Recommended; entry rules can change | Varies | All eligible travelers | Verify current entry requirements and airline/transit policies |
| Hepatitis A | Consider for some travelers | No | Travelers eating adventurously, visiting rural areas, or on longer trips | Generally lower risk than many destinations but still possible |
| Hepatitis B | Consider / recommended for some | No | Long stays, potential medical care, sexual exposure risk, tattoos/piercings | Useful for frequent travelers |
| Japanese encephalitis | Recommended for some | No | Extended rural stays, outdoor activities in endemic season, visiting agricultural areas | Often advised if staying ≥1 month in risk areas |
| Rabies (pre-exposure) | Consider for some travelers | No | Remote travel where prompt post-exposure prophylaxis may be delayed; occupational animal exposure | Japan has very low risk, so usually only niche need |
| Typhoid | Generally not routinely recommended | No | Unusual high-risk food/water exposures | More commonly advised for other destinations |
| Yellow fever | Not required unless arriving from a yellow-fever-risk country | No | Travelers with recent travel/transit in yellow-fever-risk countries | Proof may be required at entry when arriving from risk areas |
