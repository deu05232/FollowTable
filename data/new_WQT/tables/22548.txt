# Sympathetic Pathways  Segmental Origins and Peripheral Nerve Routes

| Target region | Major sympathetic route | Typical spinal levels | Example named nerves | Primary action summary |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Head/neck | Cervical sympathetic chain | T1–T4 (via superior cervical ganglion) | Carotid plexus | Pupil dilation, sweating |
| Heart | Cervical & upper thoracic cardiac nerves | T1–T5 | Cardiac plexus branches | Increases cardiac output |
| Lungs | Pulmonary plexus | T2–T6 | Pulmonary nerves | Bronchodilation |
| Foregut | Greater splanchnic → celiac ganglion | T5–T9 | Greater splanchnic nerve | Reduces GI motility |
| Midgut | Lesser/least splanchnic → superior mesenteric ganglion | T10–T12 | Lesser splanchnic nerve | Reduces secretion |
| Hindgut/pelvis | Lumbar splanchnic → inferior mesenteric/hypogastric plexus | L1–L2 | Hypogastric nerves | Alters bladder/sexual function |
| Upper limb | Sympathetic chain → gray rami | T2–T8 | Peripheral vascular nerves | Vasomotor control |
| Lower limb | Sympathetic chain → gray rami | T10–L2 | Peripheral vascular nerves | Vasomotor control |
