# Cross-State U.S. Metro Areas Grouped by Primary State (Examples)

| Primary state | Metro area (MSA) | States included | How it is tied to the primary state |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| District of Columbia | Washington–Arlington–Alexandria | DC–VA–MD–WV | Core jurisdiction is DC; commonly grouped with DC for metro identification |
| New York | New York–Newark–Jersey City | NY–NJ–PA | Largest share of population and core city in New York |
| Illinois | St. Louis | MO–IL | Illinois portion anchored by Metro East; often listed under both MO and IL |
| Kentucky | Cincinnati | OH–KY–IN | Kentucky counties form a major subregion; commonly indexed under KY as well as OH |
| Tennessee | Memphis | TN–MS–AR | Core city and largest share in Tennessee |
| Utah | Logan | UT–ID | Core city in Utah; includes adjacent Idaho counties |
| Oregon | Portland–Vancouver–Hillsboro | OR–WA | Core city in Oregon; metro extends into Washington |
| Kansas | Kansas City | MO–KS | Kansas side substantial; commonly indexed under KS and MO |
| Pennsylvania | Philadelphia–Camden–Wilmington | PA–NJ–DE–MD | Core city in Pennsylvania; metro spans multiple states |
| Minnesota | Minneapolis–St. Paul–Bloomington | MN–WI | Core cities in Minnesota; includes western Wisconsin counties |
| North Carolina | Charlotte–Concord–Gastonia | NC–SC | Core city in North Carolina; extends into South Carolina |
| Rhode Island | Providence–Warwick | RI–MA | Core city in Rhode Island; includes nearby Massachusetts areas |
