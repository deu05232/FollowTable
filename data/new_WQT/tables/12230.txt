# Electrical Conductivity Classes of Common Materials (Qualitative Comparison)

| Material category | Example materials | Conductivity level (qualitative) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Good conductors | Copper, Silver, Aluminum | Very high | Used for wiring and busbars |
| Moderate conductors | Iron, Steel, Brass | Medium | Higher losses than copper |
| Semiconductors | Silicon, Germanium, GaAs | Variable | Strongly depends on doping and temperature |
| Electrolytes | Salt water, Acids, Bases | Low to medium | Depends on ion concentration |
| Insulators | Glass, Rubber, PTFE | Very low | High breakdown strength |
| Superconductors | YBCO, NbTi | Effectively infinite (below Tc) | Requires cryogenic temperatures |
| Plasmas | Arc discharge, Ionosphere | High | Depends on ionization fraction |
