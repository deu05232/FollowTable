# Autonomic Reflex Physiology  Stimulus–Response Patterns

| Reflex | Primary stimulus | Sensors | Central integration | Efferent output (general) | Net effect |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Baroreceptor reflex | Increased arterial pressure | Carotid sinus, aortic arch | Medulla | Increased vagal, decreased sympathetic | Lowers HR and BP |
| Chemoreceptor reflex | Low O2 / high CO2 | Carotid & aortic bodies | Medulla | Increased sympathetic to vessels | Raises BP, supports perfusion |
| Bainbridge reflex | Increased venous return | Atrial stretch receptors | Brainstem | Reduced vagal influence | Increases HR |
| Diving reflex | Facial cold/water | Trigeminal afferents | Brainstem | Increased vagal to heart | Bradycardia |
| Micturition reflex | Bladder stretch | Bladder mechanoreceptors | Spinal cord + pons | Parasympathetic activation | Voiding |
| Pupillary light reflex | Increased retinal illumination | Retina → CN II | Midbrain | Parasympathetic to iris | Constriction |
| Thermoregulatory sweating | Increased core temperature | Hypothalamic inputs | Hypothalamus | Sympathetic to sweat glands | Sweating |
