# Public holidays in Japan (selected) — date, holiday, and brief significance

| Date | Holiday | Brief significance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| February 11 | National Foundation Day | Commemorates the founding of Japan and reflects on national history. |
| April 29 | Shōwa Day | Honors the Shōwa era and encourages reflection on that period. |
| May 3 | Constitution Memorial Day | Marks the postwar constitution and promotes reflection on government and rights. |
| November 23 | Labour Thanksgiving Day | Gives thanks for work and production; rooted in traditional harvest celebrations. |
