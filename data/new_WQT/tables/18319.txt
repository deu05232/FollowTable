# Common Irish Surnames and Their Gaelic (Irish) Equivalents (Reference List)

| English Surname | Gaelic Equivalent (Irish) | Variant Gaelic Forms (Older/Alternate) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Murphy | Ó Murchadha | Ó Murchú | Also seen as O’Murphy in older records |
| Kelly | Ó Ceallaigh | Ó Ceallacháin | Spelling varies by region |
| O'Sullivan | Ó Súilleabháin | Ó Súileabháin | Often appears without the apostrophe in English |
| Walsh | Breathnach | An Breathnach | Literally “Welshman”; sometimes preceded by article |
| Smith | Mac Gabhann | Mac an Ghabhann | Occupational surname (“smith”) |
| O'Brien | Ó Briain | Ua Briain | “Ua” is an older form of Ó |
| Byrne | Ó Broin | Ó Beirn | Anglicized spellings vary (Byrne/Beirne) |
| Ryan | Ó Maoilriain | Ó Maoilriaghain | Multiple historical spellings |
| Farrell | Ó Fearghail | Ó Fearail | Common in Longford/Leitrim |
| Duffy | Ó Dubhthaigh | Ó Dubhaidh | Can be confused with Duff in records |
| Higgins | Ó hUiginn | Uí hUiginn | Initial “h” appears after Ó in Irish orthography |
