# Antibiotic Classes and Spectrum Summary Relevant to Genitourinary Infections

| Antibiotic class | Example agents | Typical spectrum highlights | Notable adverse effects | Major interactions |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Fluoroquinolones | Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin | Gram-negative rods, some atypicals | Tendinopathy, QT prolongation | Chelation with cations |
| Tetracyclines | Doxycycline | Chlamydia, atypicals, some Gram+ | Photosensitivity, esophagitis | Reduced absorption with iron/calcium |
| Sulfonamide combo | TMP-SMX | Many Enterobacterales, some MRSA | Rash, hyperkalemia | Warfarin potentiation |
| Macrolides | Azithromycin | Atypicals, some Gram+ | GI upset, QT prolongation | CYP interactions (less with azithro) |
| Beta-lactams (penicillins) | Amoxicillin-clavulanate | Mixed Gram+/Gram-, anaerobes | Diarrhea, allergy | Allopurinol rash risk |
| Cephalosporins | Ceftriaxone, Cephalexin | Varies by generation | Allergy, C. diff | Few major |
| Nitroimidazoles | Metronidazole | Anaerobes, protozoa | Metallic taste, disulfiram-like | Alcohol interaction |
| Aminoglycosides | Gentamicin | Aerobic Gram-negative rods | Nephro/ototoxicity | Increased toxicity with diuretics |
