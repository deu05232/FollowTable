# Concerta ER dosing overview  age-based starting dose and permitted range (side-by-side comparison)

| Patient category (row = group) | Recommended starting dose | Allowed dose range | Usual maximum daily dose | Special dosing limits |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Pediatric patients (6–12 years) | 18 mg once daily | 18–54 mg/day | 54 mg/day | Increase in 18 mg increments; assess response/tolerability |
| Adolescent patients (13–17 years) | 18 mg once daily | 18–72 mg/day | 72 mg/day | Do not exceed 2 mg/kg/day (whichever is lower) |
| Adult patients (18–65 years) | 18 mg once daily (some start 36 mg) | 18–72 mg/day | 72 mg/day | Use lowest effective dose; titrate by 18 mg steps |
| Adults with lower body weight | 18 mg once daily | 18–54 mg/day (individualized) | Often ≤54 mg/day | Consider weight-based ceiling in practice (clinical judgment) |
| Patients new to stimulants (any age within labeled groups) | 18 mg once daily | 18–54 mg/day initially | Varies by age group | Start low, titrate gradually; monitor BP/HR/appetite |
| Patients with prior stimulant intolerance (any age within labeled groups) | 18 mg once daily | 18–36 mg/day (individualized) | Varies by tolerability | Slower titration and lower max may be appropriate |
