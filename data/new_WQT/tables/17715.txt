# Orbit  Superior Orbital Fissure Contents and Grouped Muscle Control (Not Muscle-by-Muscle)

| Orbital region / structure | Structures passing through | Muscles affected if the region is involved | Nerve names provided (grouped) | Why this is clinically used |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Superior orbital fissure (general) | CN III, CN IV, CN V1, CN VI, superior ophthalmic vein | “Most extraocular muscles” (grouped) | Oculomotor, Trochlear, Abducens (grouped) | Orbital apex syndrome overview |
| Common tendinous ring (annulus) | Portions of CN III, CN VI; optic nerve nearby | Recti muscles as a group | Oculomotor + Abducens (not per muscle) | Explains patterns of combined palsies |
| Outside the tendinous ring | Lacrimal nerve, frontal nerve, trochlear nerve (often described) | Superior oblique + sensory loss | Trochlear (mentioned alongside V1 branches) | Distinguishes apex vs fissure lesions |
| Cavernous sinus | CN III, IV, V1, V2, VI + ICA | Multiple EOM limitations together | Oculomotor/Trochlear/Abducens (collective) | Localization of painful ophthalmoplegia |
| Petroclival region | Abducens vulnerable along long course | Abduction deficit (only one muscle referenced) | Abducens (not in a muscle table) | Explains isolated CN VI palsy localization |
| Midbrain (oculomotor fascicles) | CN III fascicles near red nucleus | Mixed CN III pattern (grouped) | Oculomotor (grouped) | Fascicular syndromes |
| Dorsal midbrain | Trochlear decussation region | Vertical diplopia pattern (not per muscle) | Trochlear (grouped) | Localization of CN IV lesions |
| Pons (abducens nucleus/PPRF) | Horizontal gaze center | Conjugate gaze palsy | Abducens (gaze center) | Differentiates nucleus vs nerve |
| Orbital floor | Infraorbital nerve (V2) | Sensory deficit only; EOM generally spared | Trigeminal V2 | Helps separate sensory vs motor findings |
