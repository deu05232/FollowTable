# Common antibiotics and dosing schedules used for suspected bacterial prostatitis (adult outpatient regimens)

| Antibiotic | Typical dose | Frequency | Typical duration | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ciprofloxacin | 500 mg | twice daily | 4–6 weeks | Oral regimen often used when bacterial prostatitis suspected |
| Levofloxacin | 500 mg | once daily | 4–6 weeks | Alternative fluoroquinolone option |
| Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (DS) | 160/800 mg | twice daily | 4–6 weeks | Option when pathogen susceptible |
| Doxycycline | 100 mg | twice daily | 2–4 weeks | Often used when STI organism suspected |
| Azithromycin | 500 mg | once daily | 3–7 days | Commonly used for chlamydial coverage in selected scenarios |
| Ceftriaxone | 500 mg IM | single dose | once | Sometimes paired with doxycycline in STI-related presentations |
