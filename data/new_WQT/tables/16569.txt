# Culture-guided antibiotic dose options for bacterial prostatitis (adult dosing; oral unless noted)

| Antibiotic (generic) | Dose and frequency | Route | Typical treatment length | Example scenario (culture/suspicion) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Norfloxacin | 400 mg twice daily | PO | 4–6 weeks | Chronic bacterial prostatitis with susceptible gram-negative isolate |
| Moxifloxacin | 400 mg once daily | PO | 2–4+ weeks (specialist-guided) | Selected resistant organisms when alternatives limited |
| Fosfomycin tromethamine | 3 g every 48–72 hours | PO | 2–6+ weeks (varies by protocol) | Multidrug-resistant gram-negative prostatitis (off-label protocols exist) |
| Amoxicillin | 500 mg three times daily | PO | 2–4 weeks (culture-guided) | Enterococcus spp. susceptible to aminopenicillins |
| Cephalexin | 500 mg four times daily | PO | 2–4 weeks (culture-guided) | Susceptible urinary pathogens when narrow-spectrum appropriate |
| Cefuroxime axetil | 500 mg twice daily | PO | 2–4 weeks (culture-guided) | Beta-lactam option for susceptible isolates |
| Gentamicin | 5–7 mg/kg once daily | IV | Initial 1–3 days then step-down per response | Severe infection needing IV aminoglycoside coverage |
| Ertapenem | 1 g once daily | IV/IM | Often 1–2+ weeks then step-down if possible | ESBL-producing Enterobacterales with limited oral options |
