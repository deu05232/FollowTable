# Antibiotics for Prostatitis  Dosing Regimens and Major Drug Interactions

| Antibiotic | Typical dose & frequency (adult) | Duration (typical) | Major interaction trigger | Clinical note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ciprofloxacin | 500 mg PO twice daily | 2–4 weeks | Tizanidine (contraindicated) | Monitor tendon symptoms |
| Levofloxacin | 500 mg PO once daily | 2–4 weeks | QT-prolonging antiarrhythmics | Avoid in known QT prolongation |
| Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (DS) | 160/800 mg PO twice daily | 4–6 weeks | Warfarin (↑INR) | Check INR more frequently |
| Doxycycline | 100 mg PO twice daily | 10–14 days | Isotretinoin (↑ICP risk) | Separate from iron supplements |
| Azithromycin | 500 mg PO daily | 3–5 days | Antacids (↓absorption) | Consider QT risk |
| Fosfomycin | 3 g PO every 48–72 h | 2–6 weeks | Metoclopramide (↓levels) | Used for resistant organisms |
| Ceftriaxone | 1 g IM/IV once daily | 1–2 days (step-down) | Calcium IV solutions (precipitation) | Avoid co-infusion with calcium |
| Gentamicin | 5–7 mg/kg IV once daily | individualized | Loop diuretics (↑ototoxicity) | Requires drug-level monitoring |
