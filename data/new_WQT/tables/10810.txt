# Brainstem Nuclei and Pathways Involved in Eye Movements

| Structure | Location | Associated cranial nerve(s) | Role in eye movement control | Lesion clue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Oculomotor nucleus | Midbrain | CN III | Somatic motor output to eye movers | Ipsilateral ophthalmoplegia |
| Edinger–Westphal nucleus | Midbrain | CN III (parasympathetic) | Pupillary constriction/accommodation | Dilated, nonreactive pupil |
| Trochlear nucleus | Midbrain (dorsal) | CN IV | Motor output (decussates) | Contralateral vertical diplopia |
| Abducens nucleus | Pons | CN VI | Lateral gaze coordination | Horizontal gaze palsy |
| PPRF | Pons | VI-related circuitry | Horizontal saccade generator | Ipsilateral saccade deficit |
| MLF | Brainstem tract | III/IV/VI connections | Conjugate gaze coordination | Internuclear ophthalmoplegia |
| Superior colliculus | Midbrain | Tectal pathways | Reflexive saccades | Abnormal saccadic initiation |
| Cerebellar flocculus | Posterior fossa | Vestibulo-ocular circuitry | VOR adaptation | Gaze-evoked nystagmus |
