# Yoga Practice Reference  Sanskrit Asana Names with Alignment Notes

| Sanskrit (IAST) | English Name | Category | Key Alignment / Benefits (long) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Adho Mukha Svanasana | Downward-Facing Dog | Standing / Inversion | Hands shoulder-width, press knuckles down, externally rotate upper arms, lengthen spine; often used as a transitional posture and mild inversion to build shoulder stability and hamstring length. |
| Trikonasana | Triangle Pose | Standing | Lengthen both sides of the waist, stack shoulders, engage quadriceps; traditionally used to develop lateral flexibility and balance while grounding through the feet. |
| Virabhadrasana I | Warrior I | Standing | Square hips forward, lift sternum, maintain knee tracking; can be used to cultivate strength and endurance in legs and hip flexors. |
| Virabhadrasana II | Warrior II | Standing | Front knee over ankle, arms energized, gaze over front hand; builds stamina and awareness of pelvic alignment across many vinyasa sequences. |
| Paschimottanasana | Seated Forward Bend | Seated | Hinge at hips, lengthen spine before folding; frequently taught as a calming posture to quiet the nervous system and release posterior chain tension. |
| Setu Bandha Sarvangasana | Bridge Pose | Backbend | Feet hip-width, press into heels, lift pelvis, broaden collarbones; strengthens glutes and spinal extensors while opening the front body. |
| Salamba Sarvangasana | Supported Shoulderstand | Inversion | Weight on shoulders not neck, support back with hands; traditionally described as restorative and balancing but requires careful setup. |
