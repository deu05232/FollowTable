# Key World War II Timeline Events Leading to the Atomic Bombing of Hiroshima (Chronological Sequence)

| Full Date (DD Month YYYY) | Event | Why it matters for the lead-up |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 07 December 1941 | Japan attacks Pearl Harbor; the United States enters WWII. | Triggers full U.S. mobilization and shifts the Pacific War into total conflict. |
| 04 June 1942 | Battle of Midway begins. | Major turning point in the Pacific, accelerating Japan’s strategic decline. |
| 20 June 1944 | Battle of the Philippine Sea ends. | Destroys much of Japan’s carrier air power, enabling deeper U.S. advances. |
| 24 July 1944 | U.S. forces begin the liberation of Guam (Marianas campaign). | Secures positions that later support strategic air operations against Japan. |
| 10 March 1945 | Tokyo is devastated in a major firebombing raid (Operation Meetinghouse). | Escalates the air war and raises urgency about ending the conflict. |
| 01 April 1945 | Battle of Okinawa begins. | Extremely high casualties intensify pressure for a rapid end to the war. |
| 16 July 1945 | Trinity test: first successful detonation of an atomic device. | Confirms the atomic bomb is viable for wartime use. |
| 26 July 1945 | Potsdam Declaration issued demanding Japan’s surrender. | Sets an explicit ultimatum and frames consequences for non-surrender. |
| 06 August 1945 | Atomic bomb dropped on Hiroshima. | Culmination of the escalation chain described in the timeline. |
