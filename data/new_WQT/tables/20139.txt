# Antibiotic Tissue Penetration and Microbiology Considerations in Prostatitis

| Antibiotic | Prostate penetration (qualitative) | Typical spectrum highlight | Common target organisms | Resistance considerations | Clinical comment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ciprofloxacin | High | Gram-negative coverage | E. coli, Proteus | Rising fluoroquinolone resistance | Useful when susceptibility known |
| Levofloxacin | High | Gram-negative + some atypicals | E. coli | Regional resistance varies | Alternative to ciprofloxacin |
| TMP-SMX | Moderate | Broad (incl. some Gram+/-) | E. coli | High resistance in some areas | Best if culture susceptible |
| Doxycycline | Moderate | Atypicals | Chlamydia spp. | Resistance less relevant for Chlamydia | Consider for STI-associated cases |
| Azithromycin | Variable | Atypicals | Chlamydia spp. | Macrolide resistance in some pathogens | Often used in STI contexts |
| Ceftriaxone | Low–moderate | Gonococcal coverage | N. gonorrhoeae | Monitor local susceptibility | Often paired with other agents |
| Fosfomycin | Variable | MDR Gram-negative | ESBL-producing E. coli | Susceptibility testing helpful | Increasing interest for resistant cases |
| Gentamicin | Poor | Gram-negative | Severe infections | Resistance possible | Typically adjunctive in severe disease |
