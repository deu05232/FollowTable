# Nazi Extermination (Death) Camps  Core Sites and Key Context

| Extermination camp (German designation) | Occupied territory (1942–1944) | Primary killing method(s) | Main operational period (mass killing) | Victim estimate (approx.) | Notes on extermination role |
|---|---|---|---|---:|---|
| Chełmno (Kulmhof) | Wartheland (annexed Poland; today Poland) | Gas vans; shootings | Dec 1941–Mar 1943; Jun 1944–Jan 1945 | 150,000–320,000 | First stationary extermination site using gas vans; focused on immediate killing rather than detention.
| Bełżec | General Government (today Poland) | Fixed gas chambers (engine exhaust) | Mar 1942–Dec 1942 | 430,000–600,000 | Built explicitly for Aktion Reinhard; very few survivors; camp dismantled after operations.
| Sobibór | General Government (today Poland) | Fixed gas chambers (engine exhaust) | May 1942–Oct 1943 | 170,000–250,000 | Aktion Reinhard extermination center; operations ended after prisoner revolt and closure.
| Treblinka II | General Government (today Poland) | Fixed gas chambers (engine exhaust); shootings | Jul 1942–Aug 1943 | 700,000–900,000 | Largest Aktion Reinhard killing site; distinct from Treblinka I labor camp.
| Auschwitz II–Birkenau | Occupied Poland (today Poland) | Zyklon B gas chambers; shootings | 1942–Nov 1944 | ~1,000,000+ (mostly Jews) | Combined camp complex; Birkenau functioned as a major extermination center alongside forced labor.
| Majdanek (Lublin) | General Government (today Poland) | Zyklon B; shootings | 1942–1943 (peaks); through 1944 | 78,000–235,000 | Mixed camp; included large-scale killing (e.g., mass shootings) and gassing as part of extermination policy.
| Maly Trostinets (near Minsk) | Reichskommissariat Ostland (today Belarus) | Shootings; gas vans | 1942–1944 | 60,000–100,000 | Extermination site tied to Minsk area deportations; primarily mass shootings and mobile gassing.
| Jasenovac (Ustaše-run, Axis ally) | Independent State of Croatia (today Croatia) | Shootings; bladed weapons; starvation | 1941–1945 | 80,000–100,000+ | Not SS-run but operated as an exterminatory camp system targeting Serbs, Jews, Roma, and others.
