# Autonomic Neurotransmitters and Typical Target-Organ Responses

| Messenger substance | Main ANS division / release site | Representative effector organs | Typical physiological outcome |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Acetylcholine (ACh) | Parasympathetic postganglionic terminals | Heart (SA/AV node) | Decreased heart rate and slowed AV conduction |
| Acetylcholine (ACh) | Sympathetic preganglionic terminals (ganglia) | Adrenal medulla (chromaffin cells) | Triggers catecholamine release into blood |
| Norepinephrine (NE) | Sympathetic postganglionic terminals | Peripheral arterioles | Vasoconstriction and increased blood pressure |
| Epinephrine (Epi) | Adrenal medulla (circulating hormone) | Bronchioles | Bronchodilation |
| Dopamine | Renal sympathetic pathways (selected vascular beds) | Kidney vasculature | Increased renal blood flow (vasodilation) |
| Nitric oxide (NO) | Parasympathetic nitrergic terminals (selected tissues) | Corpus cavernosum | Smooth muscle relaxation and erection |
| VIP (vasoactive intestinal peptide) | Enteric/parasympathetic co-transmitter | GI tract | Increased intestinal secretion and smooth muscle relaxation |
