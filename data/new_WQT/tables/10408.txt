# Forage Categories in Suburban Areas  What Deer Eat and Where They Find It

| Forage Category | Examples | Typical Habitat Source | Peak Season | Why Deer Select It | Substitution in Yards |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Woody browse | Twigs, shrub tips, evergreen sprays | Hedges, woodland edge | Winter | Available above snow; higher fiber | Foundation shrubs, hedgerows |
| Forbs | Clover, dandelion, plantain | Lawns, disturbed soil | Spring–Summer | High protein and moisture | Weedy beds, turf edges |
| Grasses | Young lawn grass, sedges | Turf, meadows | Spring | Tender growth early in season | Mowed lawns |
| Mast | Acorns, beechnuts | Mature trees | Fall | High energy for winter | Oak/beechnut drop zones |
| Agricultural crops | Corn, soy | Nearby fields | Late Summer–Fall | Concentrated calories | Adjacent rural lots |
| Fruits/berries | Fallen apples, berries | Orchards, hedges | Late Summer | Sugars and water | Crabapple trees |
| Garden vegetables | Beans, lettuce | Vegetable beds | Summer | Highly palatable, succulent | Kitchen gardens |
