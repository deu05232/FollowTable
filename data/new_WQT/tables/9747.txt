# Generic Bond Rating Categories and Typical Default Risk (Not Country-Specific)

| Rating Band | Typical S&P Range | Typical Moody's Range | Typical Fitch Range | General Interpretation | Typical 10Y Spread (bps) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Prime | AAA to AA- | Aaa to Aa3 | AAA to AA- | Highest credit quality | 0–50 |
| High Grade | A+ to A- | A1 to A3 | A+ to A- | Strong capacity to pay | 50–120 |
| Upper Medium | BBB+ to BBB- | Baa1 to Baa3 | BBB+ to BBB- | Adequate capacity; adverse conditions may weaken | 120–250 |
| Non-Investment (Speculative) | BB+ to BB- | Ba1 to Ba3 | BB+ to BB- | Faces major ongoing uncertainties | 250–500 |
| Highly Speculative | B+ to B- | B1 to B3 | B+ to B- | More vulnerable to nonpayment | 500–900 |
| Substantial Risk | CCC+ to CCC- | Caa1 to Caa3 | CCC+ to CCC- | Default is a real possibility | 900–1500 |
| In Default | D | C | RD/D | Payment default | 1500+ |
