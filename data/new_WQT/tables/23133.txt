# Cooking Oils Comparison  Smoke Point and Fat Profile (SFA MUFA PUFA)

| Oil (common form) | Typical smoke point (°C) | Best for heat level (practical) | Saturated fat (SFA, %) | Monounsaturated fat (MUFA, %) | Polyunsaturated fat (PUFA, %) | Notes on heated behavior |
| --- | ---: | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Avocado oil (refined) | 270 | High heat (searing) | 12 | 70 | 13 | High smoke point; neutral flavor; good for short high-heat cooking |
| Avocado oil (unrefined) | 190 | Medium heat | 12 | 70 | 13 | Lower smoke point than refined; better for sautéing than searing |
| Canola oil (refined) | 205 | Medium-high heat | 7 | 63 | 28 | Versatile; moderate stability due to higher MUFA |
| Coconut oil (virgin) | 175 | Low-medium heat | 82 | 6 | 2 | Saturated-heavy; can smoke earlier; coconut aroma |
| Grapeseed oil (refined) | 215 | Medium-high heat | 10 | 16 | 70 | High PUFA; can oxidize faster with prolonged high heat |
| Extra-virgin olive oil | 190 | Medium heat | 14 | 73 | 11 | Flavorful; suitable for sautéing; avoid prolonged high-heat frying |
| Olive oil (refined / “light”) | 240 | High heat | 14 | 73 | 11 | Higher smoke point than EVOO; better for frying |
| Peanut oil (refined) | 230 | High heat (stir-fry/fry) | 17 | 46 | 32 | Common for frying; good high-heat performance |
| Sunflower oil (high-oleic, refined) | 230 | High heat | 10 | 80 | 8 | High MUFA variant; generally more stable than regular sunflower |
| Sunflower oil (linoleic, refined) | 225 | Medium-high heat | 11 | 20 | 66 | Higher PUFA; better for shorter heating than long frying |
| Soybean oil (refined) | 230 | Medium-high heat | 15 | 24 | 58 | Widely used; higher PUFA can reduce long-heat stability |
| Sesame oil (toasted) | 175 | Low heat / finishing | 14 | 43 | 41 | Toasted type smokes sooner; usually used for flavor at low heat |
