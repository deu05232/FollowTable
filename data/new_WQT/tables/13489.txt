# Felony vs. Misdemeanor Definitions and Thresholds (General U.S. Overview)

| Concept | Typical definition | Common threshold | Example wording used in statutes | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Felony (general) | More serious criminal offense | Often > 1 year imprisonment | "punishable by imprisonment in a state prison" | Threshold varies by jurisdiction |
| Misdemeanor (general) | Less serious criminal offense | Often ≤ 1 year in county jail | "punishable by imprisonment not exceeding one year" | Some states have gross misdemeanors |
| Infraction/violation | Non-criminal or lowest-level offense | Usually fine-only | "civil penalty" or "non-criminal" | Not always fingerprintable |
| Attempt | Inchoate offense reducing grade | Often one level lower than completed crime | "attempt is a felony of the next lower class" | Rules vary widely |
| Conspiracy | Agreement to commit crime | Grade can track target offense | "punished as if the offense were committed" | Some states cap punishment |
| Accessories | Assistance before/after crime | May mirror principal or be reduced | "accessory after the fact" | Treatment differs |
| Habitual offender | Recidivist enhancement | Adds years or raises class | "persistent felony offender" | Usually sentencing-focused but not uniform |
| Parole/probation | Community supervision | Eligibility varies | "eligible after serving X%" | Often not a separate offense |
| Expungement | Record relief mechanism | Requires waiting period | "set-aside" / "sealing" | Not punishment itself |
| Collateral consequences | Non-criminal penalties | Licensing/immigration impacts | "ineligible for" | Not tied to a specific term |
