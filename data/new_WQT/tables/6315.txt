# Seigneurial system of New France
> Types of tenure

| Type | Description |
| --- | --- |
| En franc aleu noble | A type of freehold which, if held by an individual, conferred the status of nobility . Such lands were given without any consideration other than rendering fealty and homage . Only two grants were made in New France, both to the Jesuit Order . [15] |
| En franc aleu roturier | Similar to en franc aleu noble , without conferring nobility. Such tenure was exempt from all burdens, and subject to no seigniorial rights or dues of any nature. [16] |
| En franche aumône | Granted to religious, educational or charitable institutions. In addition to rendering fealty and homage, holders were bound to perform specified services in return for the grant. [17] |
| En fief (also called en seigneurie ) | The form under which seigniories were created. It was subject to certain conditions: The rendering of fealty and homage to the King, through his representative. [18] Within 40 days of taking possession of the seigniory, he must deposit an aveu et dénombrement (i.e., a map and detailed description). [19] For those seigniories created from 1711 on, the obligation to subgrant the land, which effectively forced the seigneur to bring settlers into New France. [20] The obligation to pay the quint (i.e., a fee based on the mutation value of the property) to the Crown. [21] The obligation of military service. [22] |
| En arrière-fief | Where a dominant seigneur chose to subdivide his seigniory into smaller units. A holder of a lesser seigniory owed his obligations to the dominant seigneur, instead of directly to the Crown. [23] |
| En censive and en roture | The type of tenure held by a tenant of a seigniory, in return for paying certain dues to the seigneur. Such tenants were referred to as censitaires . [24] The dues were of various natures: Cens et rentes : annual payments due to the seigneur at St. Martin's Day (11 November). Cens were nominal amounts, while rentes were specified at the time of the grant, and could be in either produce or money. [25] Lods et ventes : payable to the seigneur upon the sale of the land, being set at one-twelfth of its value. [26] Droits de banalité : various obligations for censitaires to use the seigniory mill and oven, where such were provided. [27] Corvée : days of free labour that the tenant was obliged to perform for the seigneur. [28] |
