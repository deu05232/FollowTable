# Pacific War Timeline Milestones Leading to the Hiroshima Bombing (1941–1945)

| Date | Event (Pacific Theater) | Why it mattered for the road to Hiroshima |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1941-12-07 | Attack on Pearl Harbor | Triggered full U.S. entry into the Pacific War against Japan. |
| 1942-04-18 | Doolittle Raid on Japan | Signaled U.S. ability to strike the Japanese home islands and boosted U.S. resolve. |
| 1942-05-04 to 1942-05-08 | Battle of the Coral Sea | Checked Japanese expansion; preserved Australia’s line of communication. |
| 1942-06-04 to 1942-06-07 | Battle of Midway | Major turning point that shifted naval initiative toward the United States. |
| 1942-08-07 | Guadalcanal Campaign begins | Start of sustained Allied offensive in the Solomon Islands. |
| 1943-11-20 | Battle of Tarawa begins | Demonstrated costs of amphibious assaults; accelerated island-hopping tactics. |
| 1944-06-15 | Battle of Saipan begins | Put U.S. forces within range of Japan; intensified pressure on Japanese leadership. |
| 1944-10-20 | Leyte landings begin | Opened the Philippines campaign and threatened Japan’s resource supply lines. |
| 1944-10-23 to 1944-10-26 | Battle of Leyte Gulf | Crippled Japanese naval power, enabling further advances toward Japan. |
| 1945-02-19 | Battle of Iwo Jima begins | Provided a critical forward base and showed expected costs of invading near Japan. |
| 1945-04-01 | Battle of Okinawa begins | Extremely high casualties reinforced urgency to end the war without a mainland invasion. |
| 1945-07-26 | Potsdam Declaration issued | Allied ultimatum to Japan to surrender, setting immediate context for escalation. |
| 1945-08-06 | Atomic bombing of Hiroshima | Culminating event in the timeline leading to Japan’s surrender. |
