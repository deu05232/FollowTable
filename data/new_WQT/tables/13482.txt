# Collateral Consequences of Felony Convictions by State (Emphasis on Rights and Licensing, Not Sentencing)

| State | Voting rights after felony conviction | Jury service eligibility | Firearm rights restoration | Professional licensing impacts (examples) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Florida | Restoration depends on completion of sentence and financial obligations | Often restricted until rights restored | Separate restoration process | Real estate, nursing boards consider felony history | Focuses on civil consequences |
| Virginia | Requires individual rights restoration process | Typically barred until restored | Separate petition/relief | Security clearances and some trades affected | Procedures vary |
| Kentucky | Restoration process required | Restricted until restored | Separate relief required | Certain state licenses may deny applicants | Not a sentencing table |
| Iowa | Rights often return after completion; rules can change | Restricted until completion | Restoration petition possible | Childcare and health-care licensing affected | Policy shifts over time |
| Texas | Rights generally return after completion (except firearms restrictions) | May be limited | Firearms restricted for period/conditions | Many licensed occupations review convictions | Not prison/fine ranges |
| California | Rights often depend on custody/parole status | Limits may apply | Complex relief mechanisms | Background checks affect many boards | Mostly collateral impacts |
| New York | Depends on parole/probation status | Often restricted | Restoration through certificates/relief | Licensing agencies apply "good moral character" | Civil vs. criminal penalties |
| Illinois | Varies with sentence status | Often restricted | Relief via FOID/other procedures | Licensing discretionary denials | Not a penalty-range comparison |
| Georgia | Restoration after completion with application | Restricted until completion | Separate process | Licensing boards may deny/condition licenses | Non-sentencing focus |
| Washington | Restoration after completion and court process | Restricted until restored | Restoration petition | Certain jobs require waiting periods | Primarily civil disabilities |
