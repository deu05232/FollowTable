# Antibiotic regimens for acute bacterial prostatitis (adult) — dose and frequency

| Clinical scenario | Antibiotic (generic) | Dose per administration | Frequency | Route | Typical duration |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Outpatient, uncomplicated | Ciprofloxacin | 500 mg | twice daily | PO | 10–14 days |
| Outpatient, uncomplicated | Levofloxacin | 500 mg | once daily | PO | 10–14 days |
| Outpatient, uncomplicated (alternative) | Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (DS) | 160/800 mg | twice daily | PO | 10–14 days |
| Inpatient, severe/systemic illness | Ceftriaxone | 1 g | once daily | IV | until improved then step-down to PO |
| Inpatient, severe/systemic illness | Piperacillin-tazobactam | 4.5 g | every 6 hours | IV | until improved then step-down to PO |
| Inpatient, severe/systemic illness (if Pseudomonas risk) | Cefepime | 2 g | every 8–12 hours | IV | until improved then step-down to PO |
| Step-down after IV (when stable) | Ciprofloxacin | 500 mg | twice daily | PO | to complete 2–4 weeks total |
| Step-down after IV (when stable) | Levofloxacin | 500–750 mg | once daily | PO | to complete 2–4 weeks total |
