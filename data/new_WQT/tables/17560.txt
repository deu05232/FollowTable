# Foods High in Zinc by Food Group (No Per-Serving Mineral Context)

| Food group | Examples | Typical portion (examples) | Comments |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shellfish | Oysters, crab, lobster | 3 oz cooked | Often cited as zinc-rich |
| Red meat | Beef, veal, lamb | 3–4 oz cooked | Higher in minerals than many poultry cuts |
| Poultry | Chicken thigh, turkey dark meat | 3–4 oz cooked | Moderate source |
| Dairy | Cheddar, mozzarella, yogurt | 1 oz cheese / 1 cup yogurt | Varies by product |
| Nuts & seeds | Pumpkin seeds, sesame, cashews | 1 oz | Also provide fats and fiber |
| Legumes | Chickpeas, lentils, beans | 1/2–1 cup cooked | Phytates may affect absorption |
| Whole grains | Oats, wheat germ, fortified grains | 1 cup cooked / 1 serving | Fortification differs |
| Vegetables | Mushrooms, peas | 1 cup | Generally lower density |
| Fruit | Avocado, berries | 1 cup | Typically small amounts |
