# Chronological list of New France Governor General office holders (name and tenure)

| Office holder | Tenure | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Louis-Hector de Callière | 1699–1703 | Succeeded Frontenac-era administration |
| Philippe de Rigaud de Vaudreuil | 1703–1725 | Longest-serving governor |
| Charles de Beauharnois | 1726–1748 | Administration during expansion |
| Jacques-Pierre de Taffanel de La Jonquière | 1749–1752 | Arrived after War of Austrian Succession |
| Pierre de Rigaud de Vaudreuil-Cavagnial | 1755–1760 | Final governor in Seven Years' War |
| (Vacant/transition) | 1760–1763 | French regime ended during British takeover |
