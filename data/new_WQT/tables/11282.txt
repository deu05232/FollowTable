# GCSE Chemistry Revision — Acids, Bases and Salts (Key Facts List)

| Topic | Definition / Rule | Example | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Strong acids | Fully ionise in water | HCl, HNO3, H2SO4 | High conductivity |
| Weak acids | Partially ionise | CH3COOH | Lower conductivity |
| Alkalis | Soluble bases producing OH− | NaOH, KOH | pH > 7 |
| Neutralisation | Acid + base → salt + water | HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O | Exothermic |
| Indicators | Colour change with pH | Litmus, phenolphthalein | Used in titrations |
| Salts | Ionic compounds from acids | CuSO4, NaCl | Many are soluble |
| Titration | Measuring neutralisation | Acid in burette, alkali in flask | Requires indicator |
