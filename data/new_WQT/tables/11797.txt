# Public Holidays by Country with Descriptions (Meaning-focused, Selected Examples)

| Country | Holiday (official name) | Type | What it commemorates / meaning (brief) | Similar holidays elsewhere / distinguishing clue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Botswana | Good Friday | Religious (Christian) | Remembers the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. | Part of Easter period; occurs before Easter Sunday. |
| Botswana | Easter Monday | Religious (Christian) | Celebrates the resurrection of Jesus Christ (public observance on the Monday after Easter). | Often paired with Good Friday; not universally a public holiday in all countries. |
| Spain | Andalucía Day | Regional / Autonomous community holiday | Commemorates the 1980 Andalusian autonomy referendum and regional identity. | Distinguish from Spain’s national holidays; only applies in Andalucía. |
| Spain | Balearic Islands Day (Día de les Illes Balears) | Regional / Autonomous community holiday | Marks the 1983 statute establishing the Balearic Islands’ autonomy. | Regional identity holiday; not observed nationwide in Spain. |
| Switzerland (Geneva) | Jeûne genevois | Cantonal (religious-historic) | Historic Geneva day of fasting/prayer tied to Protestant history and civic remembrance. | Different from “Swiss Federal Fast”; specific to Geneva with its own tradition. |
| Switzerland (Zurich, some areas) | Knabenschiessen | Cantonal / local tradition | Youth marksmanship festival with historic civic-militia roots; now a community festival and fair. | Name can be mistaken for a sporting event; it is an official day off only in certain areas. |
| Italy (Sardinia) | Sardinia Day (Sa Die de sa Sardigna) | Regional | Commemorates the 1794 uprising in Cagliari against Piedmontese rule; symbol of Sardinian identity. | Not Italy-wide; distinct from national Liberation Day (Apr 25). |
| India (regional) | Panchayati Raj Diwas | Civic / governance | Recognizes the local self-government system (Panchayati Raj) and grassroots democracy. | Often confused with national Constitution-related observances; scope varies by state. |
| Venezuela | Simón Bolívar's Birthday | Historical / national figure | Honors the birth of Simón Bolívar, key leader of independence movements in South America. | Distinguish from Independence Day; focuses on the person rather than a single event date. |
| Venezuela | Indigenous Resistance Day | Cultural-historical | Commemorates Indigenous resistance and reframes colonial-era remembrance in Venezuela. | Related to “Columbus Day” timing but different official framing and meaning. |
