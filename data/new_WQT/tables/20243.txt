# Genetic Disorders by Inheritance Pattern (Overview)

| Inheritance group | Example disorders (not gene-specific) | Typical presentation | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Autosomal dominant | Marfan syndrome; Huntington disease; Neurofibromatosis type 1 | Often appears in multiple generations | Variable expressivity is common |
| Autosomal recessive | Phenylketonuria; Tay-Sachs disease; Sickle cell disease | Often seen in siblings | Carrier parents typically unaffected |
| X-linked recessive | Hemophilia A; Duchenne muscular dystrophy; G6PD deficiency | More males affected | Females may be carriers |
| Mitochondrial | Leber hereditary optic neuropathy; MELAS | Maternal transmission | Heteroplasmy may affect severity |
| Chromosomal (numerical) | Down syndrome; Edwards syndrome; Patau syndrome | Congenital anomalies common | Usually sporadic nondisjunction |
| Chromosomal (structural) | Cri du chat; Wolf-Hirschhorn syndrome | Developmental delay common | Deletions/duplications/translocations |
| Multifactorial | Cleft lip/palate; neural tube defects; type 2 diabetes | Complex risk | Genes + environment interplay |
