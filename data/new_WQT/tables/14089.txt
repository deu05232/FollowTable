# Clinical pupil findings and implicated cranial nerves

| Pupil finding pattern | Likely cranial nerve implication | Typical bedside context |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Unilateral dilated pupil with poor light response | Oculomotor nerve (CN III) involvement | Possible compressive lesion |
| Bilateral pinpoint but reactive pupils | Not a focal ocular-motor nerve palsy pattern | Opioid intoxication, pontine issues |
| Bilateral fixed dilation | Not specific to a single ocular-motor nerve | Severe hypoxia, drug effects |
| Anisocoria with variable reactivity | Consider autonomic/oculomotor pathways | Mixed etiologies |
