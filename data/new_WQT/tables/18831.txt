# Byzantine Offices  Functions, Rank, and Typical Holders (Office-Centric Overview)

| Office | Administrative sphere | Typical rank | Main responsibilities | Typical holder profile | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Megas doux | Navy | Senior | Commander of the fleet | Aristocratic military leader | Often close to the emperor |
| Logothetes tou dromou | Foreign affairs / posts | Senior | Diplomacy and imperial courier system | Experienced bureaucrat | Could act as chief minister |
| Praetorian prefect | Civil administration | Senior | Finance, justice, administration | High civil official | Late antique continuity |
| Strategos | Provincial military | Regional | Army command and provincial governance | Soldier-governor | Theme system office |
| Protasekretis | Chancery | Mid/Senior | Head of secretaries and documents | Senior clerk | Court bureaucracy |
| Eparch of Constantinople | Urban administration | Senior | Policing, markets, guilds | Trusted administrator | Key city office |
| Domestikos ton scholon | Army | Senior | Commander of elite regiments | Leading general | East/West variants |
| Parakoimomenos | Court | Senior | Chamberlain role, access control | Palace insider (often eunuch) | Political influence varied |
