# National Highways in Uttar Pradesh (current numbering) — key in-state corridors and major towns

| NH No. | Corridor type | In-state segment (Uttar Pradesh) | Key towns/cities in UP (examples) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| NH 19 | East–West trunk | Enters from Haryana side near Mathura/Agra region and exits to Bihar near Chandauli | Mathura, Agra, Firozabad, Etawah, Kanpur, Prayagraj, Varanasi, Chandauli | Part of the historic Grand Trunk alignment; major freight corridor |
| NH 27 | East–West trunk | Crosses eastern/central UP from Bihar border area toward western UP | Gorakhpur, Basti, Ayodhya, Lucknow, Kanpur | Forms a long inter-state corridor connecting to Bihar and onward westward |
| NH 30 | East–West spur | Runs across south-central UP toward Madhya Pradesh | Prayagraj, Rewa-link corridor towns (via Mirzapur region) | Connects Ganga-belt to MP; important for Vindhya traffic |
| NH 34 | North–South / diagonal | Links central UP toward Uttarakhand via Ganga towns | Lucknow, Sitapur, Hardoi (corridor region) | Used for movement toward Himalayan states via UP plains |
| NH 24 | East–West (urban-focused) | Runs within western UP NCR approach toward Lucknow direction (legacy corridor) | Ghaziabad, Hapur, Moradabad (corridor region) | Often referenced in older documents; segments upgraded/renumbered in places |
| NH 31 | North–South / river-parallel | Follows the Ganga plain in eastern UP toward Bihar | Ghazipur, Ballia | Important river-belt connectivity; overlaps with other corridors in sections |
| NH 9 | NCR connector | Enters UP from Delhi/NCR and proceeds toward central UP | Ghaziabad, Hapur, Moradabad | Key access route from Delhi into UP (high traffic) |
