# Cranial Nerve III IV VI  Pupil and Coma-Oriented Exam Patterns

| Finding (eyes) | Pupil size/reactivity | Extraocular movement pattern | Most likely nerve implicated | Clinical context emphasized |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Unilateral fixed dilated pupil | Dilated, nonreactive | Eye may be “down and out” | Oculomotor (CN III) | Uncal herniation concern |
| Bilateral pinpoint pupils | Pinpoint, reactive | Often preserved gross EOM | Not specific; pontine involvement or opioids | Coma/toxicology |
| Bilateral mid-position fixed pupils | Mid-position, nonreactive | Variable EOM limitation | Midbrain involvement (not muscle-specific) | Severe anoxia/brain injury |
| Inability to abduct one eye | Usually normal | Loss of abduction | Abducens (CN VI) | Raised ICP/pons lesion |
| Vertical diplopia worse looking down | Usually normal | Impaired depression in adduction | Trochlear (CN IV) | Head tilt compensation |
| Light-near dissociation | Abnormal | EOM may be intact | CN III parasympathetic pathways | Neurosyphilis/tectal lesions |
| Relative afferent pupillary defect | Abnormal swing | EOM intact | Optic nerve (CN II) | Optic neuropathy |
| Bilateral ophthalmoplegia with ptosis | Variable | Multiple directions limited | CN III predominance | Aneurysm/diabetes |
