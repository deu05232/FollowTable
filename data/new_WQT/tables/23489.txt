# New York Felony Sentencing Classes (Penal Law)  Maximum Prison Terms and Fines

| Felony classification | Subtype / notes | Maximum determinate or indeterminate prison term | Maximum fine (general rule) |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| Class A-I felony | Most serious violent felonies (e.g., certain homicide-related, major drug trafficking) | Life imprisonment (indeterminate; maximum = life) | Up to $50,000 (or double the defendant’s gain) |
| Class A-II felony | Serious controlled substance felonies (historic classification retained in many references) | Life imprisonment (indeterminate; maximum = life) | Up to $50,000 (or double the defendant’s gain) |
| Class B felony | Standard B felony (non-violent) | Up to 25 years | Up to $30,000 (or double the defendant’s gain) |
| Class B violent felony | Violent felony category (VFO) | Up to 25 years (determinate sentencing) | Up to $30,000 (or double the defendant’s gain) |
| Class C felony | Standard C felony (non-violent) | Up to 15 years | Up to $15,000 (or double the defendant’s gain) |
| Class C violent felony | Violent felony category (VFO) | Up to 15 years (determinate sentencing) | Up to $15,000 (or double the defendant’s gain) |
| Class D felony | Standard D felony (non-violent) | Up to 7 years | Up to $5,000 (or double the defendant’s gain) |
| Class D violent felony | Violent felony category (VFO) | Up to 7 years (determinate sentencing) | Up to $5,000 (or double the defendant’s gain) |
| Class E felony | Standard E felony (non-violent) | Up to 4 years | Up to $5,000 (or double the defendant’s gain) |
