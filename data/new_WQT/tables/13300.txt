# Oxidation States and Functional Group Trends for Oxygen-Containing Organics

| Functional group (type) | Example molecule | Key bond motif (text) | Typical carbon oxidation state trend | Common transformation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Alcohol | Ethanol | C-O single | lower than carbonyls | Oxidation to aldehyde/ketone |
| Aldehyde | Ethanal | terminal C=O | more oxidized than alcohol | Oxidation to carboxylic acid |
| Ketone | Acetone | internal C=O | similar to aldehydes | Reduction to alcohol |
| Carboxylic acid | Acetic acid | C(=O)-O-H | highly oxidized | Reduction to alcohol (strong) |
| Ester | Ethyl acetate | C(=O)-O-C | highly oxidized | Hydrolysis to acid + alcohol |
| Ether | Diethyl ether | C-O-C | relatively reduced | Cleavage under strong acids |
| Epoxide | Ethylene oxide | three-membered C-O ring | strained, reactive | Ring opening by nucleophiles |
| Anhydride | Acetic anhydride | two acyls on O | very electrophilic | Acylation reactions |
| Acyl chloride | Acetyl chloride | C(=O)-Cl | very electrophilic | Nucleophilic acyl substitution |
| Carbonate | Dimethyl carbonate | O-C(=O)-O | oxidized | Transesterification |
| Peroxide | Di-tert-butyl peroxide | O-O | radical source | Initiates radical reactions |
