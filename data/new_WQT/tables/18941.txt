# Genetic Disorders Classified by Inheritance Pattern (Clinical Grouping)

| Inheritance group | Disorder | Typical onset | Key clinical hallmark | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Autosomal dominant | Huntington disease | Adult | Progressive chorea, dementia | Family history often present |
| Autosomal dominant | Marfan syndrome | Childhood–adult | Tall stature, aortic root dilation | Connective tissue phenotype |
| Autosomal dominant | Neurofibromatosis type 1 | Childhood | Café-au-lait spots, neurofibromas | Variable expressivity |
| Autosomal recessive | Phenylketonuria | Neonatal | Intellectual disability if untreated | Detected in newborn screening programs |
| Autosomal recessive | Tay–Sachs disease | Infancy | Neurodegeneration, cherry-red spot | Higher frequency in some populations |
| X-linked recessive | Hemophilia A | Childhood | Prolonged bleeding | Affects males predominantly |
| Chromosomal (numerical) | Down syndrome | Congenital | Hypotonia, characteristic facies | Trisomy-based syndrome |
| Mitochondrial | Leber hereditary optic neuropathy | Young adult | Acute/subacute vision loss | Maternal inheritance pattern |
