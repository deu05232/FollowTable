# Battery Chemistry Used in Wireless Earbuds and Typical Runtime Ranges

| Battery chemistry | Typical cell type | Common use in earbuds | Typical runtime range (hours) | Key trade-off |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Lithium-ion (Li-ion) | Pouch | Many true wireless models | 4–8 | Higher energy density |
| Lithium-polymer (Li-Po) | Pouch | Sport neckband earbuds | 6–12 | Flexible form factor |
| Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) | Cylindrical | Rare in earbuds | 3–6 | Longer cycle life |
| Nickel-metal hydride (NiMH) | Button/cylindrical | Uncommon today | 2–5 | Lower energy density |
| Alkaline (AAA/AA) | Cylindrical | Some legacy RF headsets | 10–30 | Replaceable |
| Zinc-carbon | Cylindrical | Very rare | 5–15 | Low cost |
| Lithium titanate (LTO) | Specialty | Not typical | 2–4 | Fast charging |
