# Autonomic Divisions and Their Main Chemical Messengers (Ganglionic vs Neuroeffector vs Adrenal)

| Pathway segment | Sympathetic division: main messenger(s) | Parasympathetic division: main messenger(s) | Notes (kept transmitter-focused) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preganglionic → ganglion | ACh | ACh | Fast ganglionic transmission via nicotinic receptors |
| Postganglionic → effector (typical pattern) | NE | ACh | “Typical” neuroeffector transmitter for each division |
| Postganglionic → effector (common sympathetic exception) | ACh | — | Sympathetic cholinergic neuroeffector signaling (not organ-effect oriented) |
| Adrenal medulla → bloodstream | Epinephrine (major), NE (minor) | — | Endocrine arm of sympathetic output; circulating catecholamines |
| Postganglionic co-transmitters (often alongside NE) | ATP, NPY | — | Co-release used for additional signaling; still transmitter-centric |
| Postganglionic co-transmitters (often alongside ACh) | — | VIP, NO | Co-transmitters/modulators frequently reported with parasympathetic signaling |
| Specialized sympathetic dopamine signaling | Dopamine | — | Dopaminergic sympathetic fibers in select vascular beds; transmitter identity emphasis |
