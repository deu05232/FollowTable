# World War II Pacific War Timeline  Milestones Leading to the Hiroshima Atomic Bombing (1941–1945)

| Date | Milestone event | Why it mattered for the path to Hiroshima |
|---|---|---|
| 7 Dec 1941 | Attack on Pearl Harbor | Brought the United States into World War II and opened full-scale U.S.–Japan conflict.
| 8 Dec 1941 | United States declares war on Japan | Formalized the Pacific War and mobilized U.S. industry and military planning.
| 4–7 Jun 1942 | Battle of Midway | Shifted momentum in the Pacific toward the U.S., enabling a sustained advance.
| 7 Aug 1942 | Guadalcanal campaign begins | Marked the start of major Allied offensive operations against Japan.
| 19–20 Jun 1944 | Battle of the Philippine Sea | Weakened Japan’s naval air power, tightening Allied operational control.
| 20 Oct 1944 | Leyte Gulf / liberation of the Philippines begins | Cut key Japanese supply routes and accelerated Japan’s strategic isolation.
| 9–10 Mar 1945 | Tokyo firebombing (Operation Meetinghouse) | Demonstrated escalation of strategic bombing and pressure on Japanese leadership.
| 1 Apr 1945 | Battle of Okinawa begins | Highlighted the expected cost of invasion and intensified search for alternatives.
| 16 Jul 1945 | Trinity test in New Mexico | Confirmed the atomic bomb’s feasibility, enabling a new option for ending the war.
| 26 Jul 1945 | Potsdam Declaration issued | Set terms for Japan’s surrender and framed consequences of refusal.
| 6 Aug 1945 | Atomic bombing of Hiroshima | Culminating event in the escalation and decision sequence described above. |
