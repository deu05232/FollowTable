# Commercial Fuel Blends and Distillation (Boiling) Ranges

| Product / Mixture | Typical Composition (example) | Boiling Range (°C) | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Gasoline (regular) | C4–C12 hydrocarbons (blend) | 30–210 | Multi-component fuel; range depends on refinery |
| Kerosene | C10–C16 hydrocarbons (blend) | 150–275 | Jet fuel fraction; not a single compound |
| Diesel fuel | C12–C22 hydrocarbons (blend) | 180–360 | Broad distillation curve |
| LPG (liquefied petroleum gas) | Propane/Butane mixture | -42 to -0.5 | Composition varies seasonally |
| Petroleum ether | C5–C7 alkanes & cycloalkanes | 30–70 | Lab solvent fraction; mixture |
| Naphtha (light) | C5–C10 hydrocarbons (blend) | 40–200 | Feedstock; not well-defined |
| Heating oil | C14–C20 hydrocarbons (blend) | 200–360 | Commercial mixture |
| Aviation gasoline (Avgas) | C4–C12 hydrocarbons + additives | 30–170 | Additives change volatility |
| Bunker fuel | heavy hydrocarbons (blend) | >350 | Residual mixture; not a pure substance |
