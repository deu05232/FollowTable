# Deer Browse Pressure Indicators and Field Signs for Residential Landscapes

| Indicator | What You Observe | Likely Cause | Time of Year Most Common | How to Confirm | Immediate Action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ragged torn leaves | Shredded edges on broadleaf plants | Deer browsing | Spring–Summer | Look for hoof prints nearby | Temporary fencing |
| Clean angled cuts | Stems cut at 45° | Rabbit browsing | Winter–Spring | Check height of cuts (low) | Hardware cloth guards |
| Missing buds/flowers | Buds removed before opening | Deer or rodents | Spring | Check for tracks and droppings | Repellents, netting |
| Stripped twig tips | New shoots missing on shrubs | Deer browsing | Fall–Winter | Browse line visible | Wrap/burlap barriers |
| Bark rubbed off | Vertical scrapes on trunks | Buck rubbing | Fall | Fresh sapwood exposed | Tree guards |
| Pellet clusters | Small oval droppings | Deer presence | Year-round | Count pellet group density | Change planting plan |
| Nighttime sightings | Deer seen at dusk/dawn | Local herd using yard | Year-round | Trail camera evidence | Exclusion fence planning |
