# Urologic Conditions and Suggested Antibiotic Options (Reference List)

| Condition | First-line antibiotic options (examples) | Alternative options | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acute bacterial prostatitis | Fluoroquinolones; TMP-SMX | 3rd-gen cephalosporin + aminoglycoside | Tailor to culture results |
| Chronic bacterial prostatitis | Fluoroquinolones | Fosfomycin; doxycycline | Longer courses often required |
| Uncomplicated cystitis | Nitrofurantoin; TMP-SMX | Fosfomycin | Avoid fluoroquinolones when possible |
| Acute pyelonephritis | Fluoroquinolones | Ceftriaxone then oral agent | Consider local resistance |
| Epididymitis (STI-associated) | Ceftriaxone + doxycycline | Azithromycin-based regimens | Depends on risk profile |
| Urethritis | Doxycycline | Azithromycin | Evaluate partners |
| Bacterial vaginosis | Metronidazole | Clindamycin | Not an antibiotic for prostatitis |
| Diverticulitis | Amoxicillin-clavulanate | Ciprofloxacin + metronidazole | GI-focused indication |
