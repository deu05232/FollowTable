# Non-Egyptian Deities Appearing in Egyptian (Ptolemaic-Roman) Contexts

| Deity | Cultural origin | How it appears in Egypt | Primary evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Zeus | Greek | Identified with Amun as Zeus-Ammon | Coins; oracle traditions |
| Aphrodite | Greek | Linked with Hathor in some syncretic cults | Ptolemaic dedications |
| Hermes | Greek | Identified with Thoth as Hermes Trismegistus | Hermetic texts (later tradition) |
| Serapis | Greco-Egyptian | State cult combining aspects of Osiris-Apis | Alexandria cult centers |
| Isis (Hellenistic) | Greco-Egyptian reception | Worshiped across the Mediterranean | Inscriptions; temples |
| Dionysus | Greek | Sometimes paired with Osiris in interpretatio graeca | Classical authors |
| Jupiter | Roman | Merged conceptually with Zeus-Ammon | Roman-era coins |
| Fortuna | Roman | Appears in Egyptian-Roman iconography | Reliefs; small bronzes |
