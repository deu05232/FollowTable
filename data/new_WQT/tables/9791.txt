# How to Label a Single Blood Pressure Reading (Category by Higher-Risk Number, mm Hg Ranges)

| Step | What to check | Systolic range (mm Hg) | Diastolic range (mm Hg) | Label/category outcome |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Crisis threshold | ≥180 | ≥120 | Hypertensive crisis (urgent) if either meets/exceeds |
| 2 | Stage 2 threshold | 140–179 | 90–119 | Stage 2 hypertension if either falls in this range |
| 3 | Stage 1 threshold | 130–139 | 80–89 | Stage 1 hypertension if either falls in this range |
| 4 | Elevated check | 120–129 | 60–79 | Elevated if systolic is 120–129 AND diastolic remains 60–79 |
| 5 | Normal check | 90–119 | 60–79 | Normal only if BOTH systolic and diastolic are within these ranges |
| 6 | Low (hypotension) screen | <90 | <60 | Low BP if either is below the low cutoff |
| 7 | Mixed-number rule | Any | Any | If systolic and diastolic land in different categories, use the higher-risk category |
