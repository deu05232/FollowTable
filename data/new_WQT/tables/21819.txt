# Smoke Point and Detailed Fatty Acid Profile of Common Cooking Oils

| Oil | Smoke point (°C) | Major fatty acids (individual) | Minor fatty acids (individual) | Notes |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- | --- |
| Extra virgin olive oil | 190 | Oleic 55–83% | Palmitic 7.5–20%, Linoleic 3.5–21%, Stearic 0.5–5% | Varies by harvest/grade |
| Refined olive oil | 240 | Oleic ~70% | Linoleic ~10%, Palmitic ~12%, Stearic ~3% | Higher smoke point when refined |
| Avocado oil (refined) | 270 | Oleic ~60–75% | Palmitic ~10–15%, Linoleic ~10–15% | Often marketed for high-heat use |
| Canola oil | 205 | Oleic ~60–65% | Linoleic ~18–22%, Alpha-linolenic ~8–12%, Palmitic ~4% | Moderate smoke point |
| Soybean oil | 230 | Linoleic ~50–55% | Oleic ~20–25%, Palmitic ~10–12%, Alpha-linolenic ~6–8% | Common frying oil |
| Sunflower oil (linoleic) | 225 | Linoleic ~60–70% | Oleic ~15–25%, Palmitic ~5–7% | Composition depends on cultivar |
| Coconut oil (virgin) | 175 | Lauric ~45–52% | Myristic ~16–21%, Palmitic ~8–10%, Oleic ~5–8% | High saturated fat, lower smoke point |
| Sesame oil (semi-refined) | 210 | Linoleic ~40–45% | Oleic ~35–40%, Palmitic ~8–10%, Stearic ~4–6% | Nutty aroma when less refined |
