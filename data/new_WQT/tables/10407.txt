# Deer Feeding Habits by Plant Part  Typical Browsing Targets in Ornamentals

| Plant Part | What Deer Commonly Do | Plant Examples Often Affected | Highest Risk Season | Typical Damage Pattern | Mitigation Approach |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| New shoots | Nip off tender growth | Roses, viburnum, hydrangea | Spring | Stubs and missing tips | Bud cages, repellents |
| Flower buds | Remove buds before bloom | Tulips, lilies, hosta scapes | Spring | Buds vanish overnight | Netting during bud stage |
| Leaves | Strip foliage | Hostas, impatiens, daylilies | Summer | Ragged edges, partial defoliation | Fencing, rotate plantings |
| Bark | Rub/peel bark from trunks | Young maples, fruit trees | Fall | Vertical scrapes | Trunk guards |
| Evergreen foliage | Browse during scarcity | Arborvitae, yew, hemlock | Winter | Browning, missing sprays | Burlap, exclusion fence |
| Fruits | Eat fallen/low fruit | Apples, pears, persimmons | Late Summer–Fall | Fruit disappears, tracks | Pick up drops, fencing |
| Seedheads | Clip seed stalks | Sunflower, coneflower | Fall | Clean cuts on stalks | Move plants, protect heads |
