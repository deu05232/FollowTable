# Blood Pressure Categories Using Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP) and Pulse Pressure (PP)

| Category | MAP (mm Hg) | Pulse pressure (mm Hg) | Example SBP/DBP pairs (mm Hg) | How it is labeled |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Low perfusion | < 65 | — | 80/50, 90/40 | Based on MAP only |
| Typical perfusion | 65–90 | 30–50 | 110/70, 120/75 | Based on MAP/PP bands |
| High perfusion | 91–105 | 40–60 | 140/90, 150/85 | MAP-driven label |
| Very high | > 105 | > 60 | 180/100, 190/95 | MAP and PP both high |
| Isolated wide PP | 70–95 | > 60 | 160/90, 170/85 | PP-based concern |
| Isolated narrow PP | 60–85 | < 25 | 90/70, 100/80 | PP-based concern |
| Clinical note | — | — | MAP = (SBP + 2×DBP)/3 | Requires calculation |
| Cuff interpretation | — | — | Not direct SBP/DBP cutoffs | Indirect classification |
