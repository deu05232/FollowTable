# Wars and Conflicts Involving Japan (1945–Present) — Participation List

| Conflict | Years | Japan's role (belligerent/participant) | Allies / Partner forces | Opponents / Adversaries | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| Korean War | 1950–1953 | UN rear-area support (logistics, bases) | United Nations Command (led by United States) | North Korea, China | Japanese territory used for staging and supply; no combat units deployed |
| Vietnam War | 1964–1975 | Rear-area support (bases, logistics) | United States and allied forces | North Vietnam, Viet Cong | Material support and servicing of US forces in Japan and Okinawa |
| Gulf War | 1990–1991 | Non-combat participant (financial + minesweeping after ceasefire) | US-led coalition | Iraq | Maritime Self-Defense Force minesweeping mission in the Persian Gulf (post-hostilities) |
| Iraq War (Coalition operations in Iraq) | 2003–2009 | Non-combat military deployment (reconstruction/support) | United States, United Kingdom, others | Iraqi insurgent groups (non-state), hostile actors | Japan Ground Self-Defense Force deployed to Samawah for humanitarian/reconstruction support |
| War in Afghanistan (ISAF / NATO mission) | 2001–2014 | Support participant (refueling/transport support) | NATO/ISAF partners | Taliban, al-Qaeda | Maritime refueling support in the Indian Ocean; airlift support at times |
| Anti-piracy operations off Somalia (Gulf of Aden) | 2009–present | Armed security operations (maritime) | Multinational anti-piracy coalitions | Somali pirate groups (non-state) | Escort missions; Japanese base in Djibouti supports deployments |
| International campaign against ISIL | 2014–present | Support participant (logistics, training assistance) | US-led coalition partners | ISIL/ISIS | Primarily non-combat support; aid and training to regional partners |
| UN peace operations (multiple missions) | 1992–present | Peacekeeping participant | United Nations member contingents | Varies by mission | Includes missions such as Cambodia (UNTAC), South Sudan (UNMISS), etc. |
