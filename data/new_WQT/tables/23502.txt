# H.264 AVC Profiles and Levels Commonly Seen in DLNA Streaming

| Profile | Level | Max Luma Samples (approx.) | Typical Use Case | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 3.0 | SD/ED | Older mobile/low-power decoders | No B-frames |
| Baseline | 3.1 | 720p | Entry HD streaming | Often used for broad compatibility |
| Main | 3.1 | 720p | Broadcast/consumer devices | B-frames allowed |
| High | 4.0 | 1080p | Blu-ray / higher bitrate | CABAC supported |
| High | 4.1 | 1080p | Mainstream 1080p | Common in consumer encodes |
| High | 5.0 | 4K (limited) | Early UHD experiments | Device support varies |
| High | 5.1 | 4K | UHD streaming | Often too heavy for older DLNA clients |
| Constrained Baseline | 3.1 | 720p | Compatibility-focused | Limits toolset for decoders |
