# Seasonal Deer Browse Preferences by Plant Category (Not Plant-by-Plant Choices)

| Season | Primary Browse Types | Typical Examples Mentioned | What This Means for Plant Selection |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Late Winter | Woody twigs, bark | shrubs, young trees | Deer focus on woody plants when herbaceous cover is scarce |
| Spring | Tender new growth | perennials, emerging shoots | Fresh growth can be heavily browsed |
| Early Summer | Forbs and garden flowers | flowering perennials, annual beds | Flowering areas may be targeted |
| Late Summer | Mixed diet | shrubs + forbs | Browsing pressure can vary with rainfall |
| Fall | Mast and woody browse | acorns, shrubs | Deer shift with available natural foods |
| Drought Periods | Whatever is green | irrigated ornamentals | Landscape plants may become more attractive |
| Heavy Snow Years | Conifers/evergreens | arborvitae, yew | Evergreens can be severely impacted |
| Rut Season | Opportunistic browsing | mixed | Movement increases; damage can spread |
| Urban/Suburban Areas | Ornamental preference | lawns, foundation plants | Deer may lose fear and browse broadly |
