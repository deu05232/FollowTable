<table>
  <caption>Pyrolysis Technology for Waste Carbon Fiber Reinforced Composites</caption>
  <tr><td>Item</td><td>Content</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Input</td><td>Waste automotive carbon fiber reinforced plastics: carbon fiber reinforced thermosetting plastics and carbon fiber reinforced thermoplastics</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Process Description</td><td>Carbon fiber reinforced composite fragments → pyrolysis unit → introduce nitrogen with an oxygen content of 3%–20% to purge air → heat to 400℃–650℃ → pyrolysis reaction occurs in the furnace chamber → combustible liquid, combustible gas, and carbon fibers</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Output</td><td>Combustible liquid and combustible gas (fed back into the pyrolysis unit for heating), carbon fibers (can be used to produce plastics for automobiles, consumer electronics and electrical appliances, medical devices, etc.)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Technology Readiness Level</td><td>■ Mass production stage  □ Pilot production stage</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Recycling Method Category</td><td>□ Physical recycling       ■ Chemical recycling</td></tr>
