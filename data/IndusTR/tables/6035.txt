  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Smoky flavor intensity (if applicable)</td><td>Chew and judge smoky note on a low/medium/high scale; spit out; cleanse palate before the next sample</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Sourness note (fermented products)</td><td>Chew and assess whether sourness is absent, mild, or excessive; record judgement for taste only</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Sweetness note</td><td>Chew and judge sweetness balance relative to saltiness; keep serving temperature consistent across samples</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Metallic or blood-like note</td><td>Chew and pay attention to metallic sensations; if present, record it as an off-flavor</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Mouth-coating or greasy aftertaste</td><td>After spitting out, assess whether an oily coating remains; record persistence and unpleasantness</td></tr>
