<table>
  <caption>Sensory Indicators and Practical Evaluation Methods for Cooked Meat Products (Checklist Format)</caption>
  <tr><th>Sensory attribute</th><th>What to notice (indicator description)</th><th>How to evaluate (practical method)</th><th>Suggested scoring note</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Appearance / Color</td><td>Surface color typical of the product; internal lean and fat color on a fresh cut; color uniformity; presence of discoloration (gray/green/brown)</td><td>Under natural daylight (or 6500 K equivalent), place the sample on a neutral background. Inspect surface first, then make one clean cut and inspect lean/fat. Record uniformity and any abnormal spots.</td><td>0=abnormal, 1=slightly abnormal, 2=normal</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Gloss / Surface moisture</td><td>Appropriate gloss; no excessive wetness, purge, or sticky film; no dull, dried-out surface</td><td>Visually assess at 30–40 cm distance. Tilt the sample to view reflected light. If purge exists, blot with tissue once and reassess surface film.</td><td>Describe: glossy / acceptable / dull</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Odor (before heating)</td><td>Characteristic cured/meaty aroma; absence of sour, rancid, moldy, chemical, or sulfur notes</td><td>Open container and wait 10 s. Hold sample 2–3 cm below nostrils and take 2–3 short sniffs. Note intensity and any off-odor descriptors.</td><td>Off-odor: none / slight / distinct</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Odor (after heating)</td><td>Clean cooked-meat aroma; absence of burnt, stale oil, or oxidized fat notes</td><td>Heat to serving temperature (e.g., 60–65°C core). Immediately after heating, sniff at 5–10 cm distance, then again after 1 min cooling. Record changes.</td><td>Intensity 1–5</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Flavor / Taste</td><td>Balance of saltiness, sweetness, spice, smoke; absence of bitter, metallic, soapy, rancid aftertaste</td><td>Take a bite-sized portion (≈5 g). Chew 10–15 times, breathe out through the nose (retronasal). Spit or swallow consistently across samples. Record primary taste and aftertaste persistence (seconds).</td><td>Aftertaste: short / medium / long</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Texture (bite & chew)</td><td>Tenderness, cohesiveness, springiness; absence of rubbery, crumbly, or pasty mouthfeel</td><td>Use a standard bite: incisors cut through a 1 cm thick slice. Then chew at a steady pace for 20 s. Note resistance at first bite and breakdown during chewing.</td><td>Tenderness 1–5</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Juiciness</td><td>Perceived moisture release during chewing; dryness or excessive greasiness</td><td>During the first 5–10 chews, judge fluid release and lubrication. Compare against a reference sample if available. Record dryness/greasiness separately.</td><td>Juicy 1–5</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Defects / Foreign matter</td><td>Visible bone fragments, casing pieces, cartilage, hard particles; surface mold (when not expected) or unusual inclusions</td><td>Visually scan the cut surface. Gently press and rub a small area with gloved fingers to detect hard particles. If a particle is detected, isolate and describe.</td><td>Pass/Fail + notes</td></tr>
</table>
