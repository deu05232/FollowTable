  <tr><td>Name</td><td>Control Word</td><td>Function</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Acknowledgment Control Word ACK</td><td>Comma,ACK,SEQ_NUM,CRCK28.7,D2.5，D0.0-D31.7，D0.0-D31.7</td><td>Frame acknowledgment. Indicates that a data frame, broadcast frame, or FCT has been received with no errors and in the correct order. The sequence number SEQ_NUM is the sequence number in the data frame, broadcast frame, or FCT that has been acknowledged. An 8-bit CRC is used to verify the integrity of the ACK</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Negative Acknowledgment Control Word NACK</td><td>Comma,NACK,SEQ_NUM,CRCK28.7,D27.5,D0.0-D31.7,D0.0-D31.7</td><td>Frame negative acknowledgment. Indicates that a data frame, broadcast frame, or FCT was not received correctly. The sequence number SEQ_NUM is the sequence number in the last data frame, broadcast frame, or FCT that was acknowledged. An 8-bit CRC is used to verify the integrity of the NACK</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Full Control Word FULL SAC</td><td>Comma,FULL,SEQ_NUM,CRCK28.7,D15.3,D0.0-D31.7,D0.0-D31.7</td><td>Error-recovery buffer full indication. Indicates that the error-recovery buffer is full. To mitigate this, the other end of the link should send acknowledgments for previously received frames/FCTs; when this end receives the acknowledgment, space in the error-recovery buffer is freed. The sequence number SEQ_NUM is the sequence number in the last data frame, broadcast frame, or FCT that was acknowledged. An 8-bit CRC is used to verify the integrity of FULL. If the error-recovery buffer is large enough to handle roughly twice the number of characters the channel can carry, FULL is typically not sent. FULL is only sent when the error-recovery buffer is small and a very long transmission cable is used, or when the link disconnects while transmitting data</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Retry Control Word RETRY</td><td>Comma，RETRY，Reserved，Re-servedK28.7,D7.4,D0.0,D0.0</td><td>Retry control word, providing an error-recovery indication function. Used to indicate to the far end of the link that this end has received a NACK and will send the contents of the error-recovery buffer</td></tr>
