  <tr><td>1×8</td><td>1–2 start with left foot, step-together small hops (L R L), face point 2; 3—turn left 90° and lift the right knee, face point 8; 4—land the right foot crossed in front of the left; 5—turn 270° counterclockwise, face point 2; 6—right knee bent, side leg lift, while turning left 90°; 7–8 face point 8, right foot forward walk</td><td>1–2 both arms pull down in front of the body from up to down to left arm side raise, right arm bent in front of the chest, flower-hand palms facing up and inward respectively; 3—left arm wraps to the right waist, right arm raised diagonally to the side, flower-hand palm outward; 4—both arms lower to the sides; 5—arms cross in front of the chest, fists; 6—from crossed at chest, arms circle via the sides to left arm raised diagonally to the side, palms together; right arm touches right foot; 7–8 arms lower via the sides to bent behind the back; right arm raised diagonally to the left, flower-hand palm up</td><td>Step-together small hops facing point 2: step the left foot forward-right while driving upward to hop; the right foot quickly follows and closes with the left leg in the air. On landing, the ball of the right foot contacts first, then the left foot; shift body weight forward in time; hops are light and springy. During the knee lift, face point 8, lift the bent knee upward parallel to the floor with pointed toes. On landing, internally rotate the right leg with toes turned inward and turn 180° counterclockwise; then weight on the right leg, left leg abducts and turns 180° counterclockwise; engage the core, lift the waist and hips, and raise the center of gravity. For the bent-knee side leg lift, fold the thigh and lower leg parallel to the floor, knee flexed about 90°, toes pointed. Throughout, basic steps are accurate and springy; rhythm is clear; facing is accurate; arm movements are clear and strong; body extension is controlled</td></tr>
  <tr><td>2×8</td><td>1—right leg side step to open stance half squat; 2—jump, left leg side leg lift; 3—left foot crosses front-right; 4—right foot side touch; 5–6 step variation (R L R) into a right-foot-forward tuck step; 7–8 left side sliding step, right side touch</td><td>1–2 both arms circle upward via the sides to diagonal side raises; 3–4 left hand retracts behind the body, right arm bends and circles around the head forward to front raise, flower-hand palm up; 5–6 right arm moves to the right via side raise to bent behind the body; left arm moves from behind via up and down to front-diagonal-down, palms together, palms inward; 7–8 arms cross in front and swing from inside to outside to left arm raised diagonally to the side and right arm lowered diagonally to the side, palms together, palms down</td><td>Side leg lift jump: step right to the right and drive upward from knee flexion to jump; left leg straight side lift at a lower angle (30°–40°) with pointed toes. In the “tuck step”, step the left foot forward with the right knee bent and pressed behind the left knee. Side touch: step the left leg forward, extend the right leg to the side and touch. During the variation steps, step the right foot back first, the left foot quickly closes to the right; heels off the floor; then the right foot forcefully steps forward into the “tuck step”. In the side sliding step, the right leg stays straight and drags toward the left. Rhythm is clear; facing and positions are accurate; arm movements are clear, strong, extended, and controlled</td></tr>
  <tr><td>3×8</td><td>1—feet-together jump; 2—left-leg rear kick run; 3—left-foot forward lunge jump; 4—feet-together jump; 5—straddle jump; 6—right-leg rear kick run; 7–8 right side hip-shift jump once, head turns to point 7</td><td>2—left hand bent in front of the chest, right hand side raise, palms together, palms down; 3—arms bend in front of the body and wrap outward one circle, ending with right arm front-diagonal-down, flower-hand palm up, left arm supports the right elbow; 4—arms cross in front of the chest, fists; 5—arms down by the sides, palms together; 6—arms lift via side raises to right arm bent in front, left arm bent behind the head, open palms forward; 7–8 left arm side raise, right arm up, flower</td><td>To the maximum extent, bend backward toward the glutes (toes pointed), and keep the hip and knee joints on one vertical line. The jumping-jack takeoff must be forceful; when landing in straddle, the projected point of the knee joint must not go beyond the toes. When jumping back to feet together, fold the right lower leg backward; actively squeeze the inner-thigh muscles to adduct and tighten; keep the right hip and knee joints on one vertical line. In the hip-shift jump, feet together then half-squat and jump up; on landing, the left leg is straight with the heel on the ground, the right knee bends while the hip joint forcefully pushes to the right; weight slightly to the right. When the right knee bends, keep the knee above the foot. Running and jumping actions should be relaxed and springy with a clear rhythm. Facing and body-part positions are accurate; arm movements are clear, strong, extended, and controlled</td></tr>
  <tr><td>4×8</td><td>1–2 cross step to the left; 3—right-leg rear kick; 4—left-leg side kick jump; 5–6 starting with the left foot, step and turn 360° counterclockwise; 7–8 jumping jacks</td><td>1–2 left arm supports at the right elbow joint, right arm front raise, flower-hand palm up; 3—left arm bent in front of the chest, right arm at the side; 4—right arm diagonal side down, left arm diagonal side up; 5–6 arms at the sides; 7–8 right arm bent behind the head circles to front raise, flower-hand palm up, left arm at the side</td><td>In the cross step, the first step points the toes in the travel direction with the heel landing first; the second step crosses the other foot behind. Then the left leg jumps in place; on landing, the right leg bends backward into a rear-bent leg. On the fourth step, the right foot lands, and the left leg performs a side kick between 30°–40° downward. In the turn, step the left foot toward point 5 (counterclockwise) and land, then cross with the right foot to turn. Jumping-jack takeoff must be forceful; throughout, rhythm is clear, accurate, and springy. Facing and body-part positions are accurate; arm movements are clear, strong, extended, and controlled</td></tr>
