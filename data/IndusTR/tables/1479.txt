<table>
  <caption>Safe Utilization Rate of Polluted Cultivated Land Indicator</caption>
  <tr><td>Indicator Name (Unit)</td><td>Safe Utilization Rate of Polluted Cultivated Land (%)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Indicator Code</td><td>C30</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Basic Connotation</td><td>The proportion of the area of polluted cultivated land achieving safe utilization, calculated in accordance with the Accounting Method for the Safe Utilization Rate of Polluted Cultivated Land (Trial), to the total area of polluted cultivated land within the administrative region</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Importance for Evaluating the Progress of Beautiful China Construction</td><td>Soil is the material foundation for sustainable economic and social development, related to the health of the people, and related to Beautiful China construction. Protecting the soil environment is an important part of promoting ecological civilization construction and safeguarding national ecological security. At present, the overall situation of China’s soil environment is worrying, and pollution in some areas is relatively severe, which has become one of the prominent shortcomings in building a moderately prosperous society in all respects. Improving the safe utilization rate of polluted cultivated land is a core goal of the Soil Pollution Prevention and Control Action Plan (referred to as the “Soil Ten Measures”), a key focus for effectively strengthening soil pollution prevention and control and gradually improving soil environmental quality, and a key to purifying the environmental conditions of agricultural product production areas, which is of great significance for the evaluation of Beautiful China construction</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Indicator Calculation Formula</td><td>C3o=WS/WBX100% where C30 is the safe utilization rate of polluted cultivated land within the administrative region; WS is the area of polluted cultivated land achieving safe utilization within the administrative region; WB is the total area of polluted cultivated land within the administrative region</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Source</td><td>Agriculture and rural departments, ecological and environmental departments</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Time</td><td>Status data are for the 10 years prior to the evaluation year, and target data are for the 10 years after the evaluation year, on an annual basis</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Spatial Scale</td><td>National, provincial (autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government), prefecture-level administrative units</td></tr>
</table>
