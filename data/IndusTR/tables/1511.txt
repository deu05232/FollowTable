<table>
  <caption>Sensory Evaluation Indicators and Methods for Cured Meat Products</caption>
  <tr><td>Item</td><td>Content</td><td>Evaluation Method</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Color</td><td>Muscle color and fat color of the sample surface and cut surface</td><td>Observe with normal vision under natural light or conditions equivalent to natural light</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Odor</td><td>Characteristic odor, off-odor, and rancid odor before and after cooking</td><td>Place the product below the nostrils and inhale repeatedly with appropriate force for inspection</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Characteristic taste after cooking</td><td>Place a small amount of product in the mouth, chew carefully for 3–5 s and then spit out; while chewing, taste using sensory organs such as the teeth and tongue</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Condition</td><td>Tissue condition, foreign matter, gloss, mucus, mold spots</td><td>Check by touching the sample by hand, and observe with normal vision under natural light or conditions equivalent to natural light</td></tr>
