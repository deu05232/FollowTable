    <tr><td rowspan="2">Appearance / Color</td><td>Lean color, fat color, color uniformity</td><td>1) Place sample on a neutral white tray. 2) Observe at ~30–40 cm distance. 3) Use standardized light (daylight-equivalent) and view both surface and a freshly cut face.</td><td>Avoid colored backgrounds; do not assess through packaging film.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Surface moisture and purge</td><td>1) Tilt tray slowly to see sheen/purge. 2) Lightly blot one corner with odorless white paper to distinguish wet vs. tacky.</td><td>Do not press hard; pressure changes appearance.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Odor (raw)</td><td>Typical fresh meat odor vs. sour, ammoniacal, putrid, rancid notes</td><td>1) Open container and wait 5–10 s for headspace to stabilize. 2) Bring sample 2–3 cm below nostrils. 3) Take 2–3 short sniffs, pause, then one deeper sniff if needed.</td><td>Do not sniff repeatedly without breaks (olfactory fatigue). Avoid perfumes/hand creams.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Texture by touch</td><td>Firmness, elasticity, stickiness/slime</td><td>1) Wear odorless disposable gloves. 2) Press gently with the fingertip for ~1 s and release to observe rebound. 3) Slide fingertip lightly across the surface to detect tackiness or slime.</td><td>Excessive handling warms the sample and changes tackiness.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Drip / exudate feel</td><td>Watery vs. viscous exudate</td><td>1) Touch exudate with fingertip or clean spatula. 2) Rub lightly between fingers to judge viscosity (watery vs. ropy).</td><td>Use fresh gloves between samples to prevent cross-contamination and carryover.</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Basic cooked taste screen (optional)</td><td>Characteristic flavor; off-flavors (sour, bitter, rancid)</td><td>1) Cook a small standardized piece (no seasoning). 2) Cool to warm (not hot). 3) Place a small bite in the mouth, chew 3–5 s, then expectorate; note retronasal aroma.</td><td>Do not swallow during screening; keep water and plain crackers for palate cleansing.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Aftertaste and mouthfeel</td><td>1) After expectoration, breathe out through the nose to detect lingering notes. 2) Rinse mouth with room-temperature water; wait ~30 s before next sample.</td><td>Hot samples mask defects and increase irritation.</td></tr>
