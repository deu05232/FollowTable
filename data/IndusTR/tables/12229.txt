    <tr><td>Lateral Hops</td><td>2×8</td><td>Side-close jumps alternating L/R (travel small)</td><td>Lower before lift: preload by bending ankles/knees/hips together, then push off as one.Land then gather: land on the lead foot first with a soft knee, then quickly bring the other foot in without slamming.Center of mass control: keep torso tall so the landing force goes through the legs, not forward into the back.Elastic rebound timing: rebound immediately after cushioning (no pause, but also no stiff “bounce”).</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Repeated Hops</td><td>3×8</td><td>Single-leg repeated hops in place (2x8 each leg)</td><td>Tripod foot: maintain pressure under big toe, little toe, and heel to stabilize landings.Knee control on repeats: keep the knee slightly flexed and pointing forward; do not let it twist on landing.Absorb vertically: land under the hip (not reaching forward) to reduce braking forces.Consistent height: match hop height each repetition to keep rhythm and reduce impact spikes.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Hamstring-Curl Jump</td><td>4×8</td><td>Step + hamstring-curl jump alternating (curl in the air)</td><td>Curl without hiking hip: lift heel toward glutes while keeping pelvis level; don’t tip the torso.Land with “soft ankle”: allow ankle dorsiflexion as you land, then control heel drop.Stability on the support leg: land with knee slightly bent and aligned; avoid wobble before the next takeoff.Breath and brace: exhale on landing, keep abdomen engaged to prevent heavy collapse.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Open-to-Close Jump</td><td>5×8</td><td>Jump to open stance then jump back to feet together (repeat)</td><td>Two-foot symmetry: both feet should contact at the same time; uneven contact increases knee/hip stress.“Land wide, knees wide”: in open stance, knees push gently outward to track over toes (no valgus).Cushion then lift: absorb first, then rebound—avoid snapping straight into the next jump.Hip hinge option: small hinge to absorb shock while keeping chest lifted.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Traveling Run-Jumps</td><td>6×8</td><td>Alternating run-jump steps traveling diagonally (4–6 contacts)</td><td>Roll-through landing: toe-ball contact → controlled heel drop to manage forward travel.Short ground contact: quick but soft; think “touch and go” rather than “stomp and push”.Arm/torso help absorption: keep trunk stable so legs can act like springs; avoid twisting on impact.</td></tr>
