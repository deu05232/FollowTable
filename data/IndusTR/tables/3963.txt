<table>
  <caption>Definitions of Core Logistics Business Terms</caption>
  <tr><td>Information Entity</td><td>English</td><td>Definition</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Export Consignor</td><td>Export Consignor</td><td>A person (company) who sends goods overseas by signing a contract with carriers such as shipping companies and freight forwarders</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Import Consignor</td><td>Import Consignor</td><td>A person (company) who delivers overseas goods domestically by signing a contract with carriers such as shipping companies and freight forwarders</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Carrier</td><td>Transporter</td><td>A company responsible for transporting people, products, or cargo</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Cargo Shed</td><td>Sheds</td><td>Unlike conventional warehouses that store goods for relatively long periods, cargo sheds are places for temporary storage of goods, including terminals, inland container depots (ICD), container yards, and bonded warehouses</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Freight Forwarder</td><td>Forwarder</td><td>A person (company) who handles cargo transport matters for the consignor. A freight forwarder can play different roles in cargo transport matters, handling transport affairs between the carrier and the consignor, or acting as the consignor’s agent to sign a transport contract with the carrier</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Loading/Unloading Company</td><td>Loading/Unloading Company</td><td>A company that loads and unloads cargo on behalf of importers, exporters, and shipping companies</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Inspection Agent/Tally Company</td><td>Inspection Agent</td><td>A company that counts cargo and inspects its condition when the cargo is loaded/unloaded from a means of transport</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Cargo Tracking</td><td>Cargo Tracking</td><td>The process of tracking the real-time location of cargo or products in transit</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Inspection & Quarantine Agency</td><td>Inspection & Quarantine Agency</td><td>An agency or organization responsible for inspecting transported cargo to prevent infectious diseases or harmful organisms from entering the country via means of transport</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Logistics Node</td><td>Logistics Point</td><td>A specific space established as transport infrastructure, including terminals, inland container depots (ICD), container yards, and bonded warehouses</td></tr>
