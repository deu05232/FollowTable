<table>
  <caption>Proportion of Administrative Villages Where Rural Domestic Wastewater Is Treated or Effectively Controlled Indicator</caption>
  <tr><td>Indicator Name (Unit)</td><td>Proportion of administrative villages where rural domestic wastewater is treated or effectively controlled (%)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Indicator Code</td><td>C24</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Basic Definition</td><td>The proportion of administrative villages where rural domestic wastewater is treated or effectively controlled out of the total number of administrative villages</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Significance for Evaluating the Progress of Beautiful China Construction</td><td>Tackling agricultural and rural pollution is an important task in implementing the rural revitalization strategy, bearing on the full completion of building a moderately prosperous society in all respects and on rural ecological civilization construction. To accelerate the resolution of prominent environmental problems in agriculture and rural areas and to win the critical battle of agricultural and rural pollution control, the Ministry of Ecology and Environment and the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs jointly formulated in 2018 the “Action Plan for the Battle Against Agricultural and Rural Pollution”, accelerating rural domestic wastewater treatment. The proportion of administrative villages where rural domestic wastewater is treated or effectively controlled is a core indicator characterizing the status of rural domestic wastewater treatment, and is of great significance for the evaluation of Beautiful China construction</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Formula</td><td>C24 = AC/ZC X100 % where C24 is the proportion of administrative villages within the administrative region where rural domestic wastewater is treated or effectively controlled; AC is the number of such administrative villages within the administrative region; ZC is the total number of administrative villages within the administrative region</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Source</td><td>Ecology and environment authorities</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Period</td><td>Current-status data are the 10 years before the evaluation year; target data are the 10 years after the evaluation year; annual</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Spatial Scale</td><td>Nationwide; provinces (autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the central government); prefecture-level administrative units</td></tr>
</table>
