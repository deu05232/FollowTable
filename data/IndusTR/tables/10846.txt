  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Caprolactam receiving and storage</td><td>Caprolactam is received in bulk, filtered and stored; inputs include caprolactam, inerting nitrogen, electricity for pumps, and cooling water; outputs include conditioned caprolactam feed to polymerization, off-spec residues returned to rework, and small waste streams such as contaminated filters and tank-bottom sludges.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Ring-opening polymerization (nylon 6 melt)</td><td>Caprolactam is polymerized in reactors; inputs include caprolactam, water as initiator, catalysts/activators (as applicable), heat/steam for reactor temperature control, and inert gas; outputs include nylon 6 melt, unreacted monomer in the melt, and vent gases that may contain traces of caprolactam and water vapor routed to condensation/scrubbing.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Devolatilization and monomer recovery</td><td>The polymer melt is stripped to reduce residual caprolactam; inputs include nylon 6 melt, vacuum/steam ejector services or vacuum pumps, cooling water for condensers, and wash water for scrubbers; outputs include low-monomer polymer melt to spinning, recovered caprolactam/water condensate returned to purification, and wastewater containing caprolactam sent to treatment.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Melt filtration and additive dosing</td><td>The melt is filtered and stabilized prior to spinning; inputs include polymer melt, filter packs/screen media, stabilizers (heat/oxidation stabilizers), TiO2 delustrant or masterbatch (optional), and electricity for dosing pumps; outputs include filtered melt to spinning, spent filter packs containing polymer residue, and off-spec polymer diverted to rework.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Melt spinning (spinnerets)</td><td>Filtered polymer melt is extruded through spinnerets to form filaments; inputs include polymer melt, spin finish oil, compressed air for quenching/handling, and cooling air; outputs include as-spun filaments (undrawn yarn), polymer drips/edge trim, and oily wipe wastes from spin-finish application and equipment cleaning.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Drawing and heat-setting</td><td>As-spun yarn is drawn and heat-set to develop orientation; inputs include undrawn yarn, electricity/thermal oil or steam for heated rolls/ovens, spin finish replenishment, and compressed air; outputs include drawn yarn, broken filaments and waste yarn, and volatilized finish components captured on ventilation filters or sent to abatement.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Winding, packaging, and bale handling</td><td>Yarn is wound, inspected and packaged; inputs include drawn yarn, paper tubes, plastic wrap, cartons/pallets, and electricity; outputs include packaged nylon 6 fiber/yarn, rejects/off-spec packages returned to rework, and packaging wastes such as trim film and damaged cones.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Utilities and auxiliary systems (shared)</td><td>Plant utilities support the main line; inputs include boiler feedwater and treatment chemicals, cooling tower makeup water, compressed-air system oil, and maintenance materials; outputs include steam/condensate loops, cooling tower blowdown, oily wastewater from compressors, and general solid wastes (used oils, spent adsorbents, maintenance rags) routed to disposal or recycling.</td></tr>
  </tbody>
