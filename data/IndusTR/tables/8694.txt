    <tr><td>Falling Leaf (forward/backward diagonal slip)</td><td>Fore-aft balance, edging finesse, travel line</td><td>Maintain a controlled diagonal slide, then reverse direction (forward to backward) without turning the skis into a full turn; keep skis parallel and speed consistent; adjust fore-aft pressure to move tips/tails subtly while torso stays stable; avoid catching edges during direction changes.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Pivot Slips (continuous pivot, minimal travel)</td><td>Rotary separation, edge release, quiet upper body</td><td>Pivot skis 180° repeatedly while remaining in a narrow corridor with minimal downhill acceleration; release edges to pivot, then re-engage edges to stabilize (no hopping); keep upper body facing downhill with pelvis/legs doing the rotation; maintain even pivot speed both directions.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Javelin Turns (inside ski crossed over)</td><td>Outside-ski dominance, hip/leg steering, balance</td><td>Lift and cross the inside ski tip over the outside ski during the shaping phase without touching down; maintain a stable outside-ski platform (outside ski carries primary pressure); keep skis tracking smoothly with no abrupt braking; torso remains balanced over the outside ski with controlled angulation.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>One-Ski Glide (straight, each ski)</td><td>Balance, pressure control, stance stability</td><td>Glide on one ski with the free ski held off the snow for a sustained distance; remain centered (no tail-heavy stance) and keep the gliding ski running flat-to-slight edge without wobble; pelvis stays level with minimal upper-body sway; repeat on both left and right with comparable stability.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Linked Pole-Plant Parallel Turns</td><td>Pole timing/placement, rhythm, turn initiation</td><td>Plant pole consistently just before initiating each new turn (as the old turn finishes and edges release); plant occurs forward and slightly to the side of the new inside ski tip (not behind the boot); pole touch supports rhythm without forcing upper-body rotation; maintain parallel skis and smooth edge change through transitions.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Steep Terrain Controlled Descent (short turns)</td><td>Line choice, speed control, edge engagement, balance</td><td>Descend in a controlled fall-line corridor using turn shape to manage speed; keep feet under hips with active flexion/extension to maintain snow contact; commit pressure to the outside ski in each turn and release cleanly; avoid stemming, traverse “parking,” or excessive skidding that breaks rhythm.</td></tr>
