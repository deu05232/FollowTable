<table>
  <caption>Energy Consumption per Unit of GDP Indicator</caption>
  <tr><td>Indicator Name (Unit)</td><td>Energy consumption per unit of GDP (tons of standard coal per 10,000 yuan)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Indicator Code</td><td>C44</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Basic Definition</td><td>The energy consumed by a country (region) to produce one unit of gross domestic product (GDP)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Significance for Evaluating the Progress of Beautiful China Construction</td><td>Energy consumption per unit of GDP is one of the core indicators for measuring green economic growth and the progress of the energy revolution, and is a primary indicator reflecting the level of energy consumption and the status of energy saving and consumption reduction. It can directly reflect the degree of economic dependence on energy, and can also indirectly reflect industrial structure, the level of equipment and technological outfitting, the composition of energy consumption, and utilization efficiency, among other aspects. Reducing energy consumption per unit of GDP is one of the core goals in China’s ecological civilization assessments and Beautiful China construction, and is of great significance for the evaluation of Beautiful China</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Formula</td><td>C44 = AN/BN where C44 is energy consumption per unit of GDP; AN is energy consumption; BN is GDP calculated at comparable prices of the base year. For comparability, constant prices should be used in the calculation</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Source</td><td>Statistical authorities; development and reform authorities</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Period</td><td>Current-status data are the 10 years before the evaluation year; target data are the 10 years after the evaluation year; annual</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Spatial Scale</td><td>Nationwide; provinces (autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the central government); prefecture-level administrative units</td></tr>
</table>
