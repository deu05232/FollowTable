  <caption>Behavioral Competency Requirements for Science & Technology Evaluation Personnel (Capability-Focused)</caption>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Competency Item</th><th>Behavioral Expectation (What it means in practice)</th><th>Common Evidence in Work Outputs (Non-experience-based)</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>B1: Integrity & Ethical Conduct</td><td>Acts consistently with professional ethics; avoids misrepresentation, selective reporting, or data manipulation even under pressure; documents decisions transparently.</td><td>Clear audit trail; consistent application of rules; explicit disclosure statements.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>B2: Confidentiality & Information Stewardship</td><td>Protects sensitive information; shares data only on a need-to-know basis; applies secure handling practices throughout collection, storage, analysis, and reporting.</td><td>Redaction where required; controlled access logs; secure transfer procedures followed.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>B3: Methodological Rigor</td><td>Selects and applies appropriate evaluation methods; checks assumptions; triangulates sources; addresses limitations explicitly rather than hiding uncertainty.</td><td>Method justification notes; sensitivity checks; limitation sections tied to impact on findings.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>B4: Analytical Judgment</td><td>Interprets evidence prudently; distinguishes correlation vs. causation; avoids overgeneralization; forms balanced conclusions proportional to evidence strength.</td><td>Conclusions mapped to evidence; uncertainty statements; alternative interpretations considered.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>B5: Stakeholder Neutrality</td><td>Engages with different stakeholders without favoritism; prevents undue influence; keeps evaluation criteria stable and not tailored to any party’s preferences.</td><td>Standardized interview guides; consistent scoring rationales; documented handling of lobbying attempts.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>B6: Bias Awareness & Mitigation</td><td>Identifies cognitive and organizational biases (confirmation bias, anchoring, prestige bias); uses structured techniques to reduce them.</td><td>Predefined rubrics; blinded review where feasible; independent cross-checks recorded.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>B7: Communication Clarity</td><td>Explains complex findings in plain language; separates facts, assumptions, and recommendations; avoids ambiguous wording that can be misused.</td><td>Executive summaries with “facts vs. judgment”; consistent terminology; traceable figures/tables.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>B8: Adaptability Under Uncertainty</td><td>Adjusts data collection plans when constraints arise while protecting validity; prioritizes critical evidence; maintains composure during shifting requirements.</td><td>Revised protocols with rationale; risk logs; maintained comparability of core indicators.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>B9: Conflict Resolution & Decision Discipline</td><td>Handles disagreements professionally; facilitates resolution using evidence and rules; makes timely decisions and records the basis for them.</td><td>Decision memos; meeting notes with agreed criteria; documented escalation path when needed.</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>
