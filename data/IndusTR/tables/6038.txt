<table>
  <caption>Taste indicator and tasting method for cooked meat products (training-oriented protocol)</caption>
  <tr><th>Item</th><th>Content to Judge</th><th>Evaluation Method</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Characteristic cooked-meat flavor typical of the product</td><td>Serve warm after cooking; place a small bite in the mouth, chew slowly for about 3–5 s, then spit out; judge whether the characteristic flavor is clearly present</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Off-flavor (e.g., abnormal bitterness, chemical notes)</td><td>After chewing, focus on any atypical notes; record presence/absence and perceived intensity; rinse mouth with water between samples</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Rancid/oxidized flavor</td><td>Chew and assess any stale or oxidized notes; if detected, note when it appears (immediate vs aftertaste)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Saltiness balance</td><td>Chew and judge whether saltiness is too weak, acceptable, or too strong; use the same sample size for each product</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Aftertaste persistence</td><td>Spit out after chewing; wait briefly and assess lingering flavor; record if aftertaste is clean or unpleasant</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Taste</td><td>Overall taste acceptability</td><td>Based on the above taste attributes, provide an overall acceptability judgement for taste only (do not consider appearance or odor)</td></tr>
