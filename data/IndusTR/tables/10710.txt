  <caption>Proper vs improper rib and junction design focused on shrinkage cavities</caption>
  <tr><th>Item</th><th>Proper design</th><th>Improper design</th><th>Shrinkage-related note</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Rib thickness ratio</td><td>Rib ≤ 0.8× base wall</td><td>Rib ≈ base wall or thicker</td><td>Thick ribs create hot spots and shrinkage cavities at the rib root</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Rib spacing</td><td>Spread ribs to avoid a thick cluster</td><td>Ribs placed too close, forming a thick band</td><td>Clustered ribs hold heat and increase shrinkage porosity</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Intersection radius</td><td>Large fillet with gradual blending</td><td>Small radius and abrupt blend</td><td>Abrupt intersection thickens the node and drives shrinkage void formation</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Junction with boss</td><td>Boss is cored; connection is webbed</td><td>Solid boss with thick base pad</td><td>Solid boss base commonly forms internal shrinkage cavities</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Section change</td><td>Long, smooth taper</td><td>Stepped change</td><td>Stepped change creates a last-to-freeze zone that shrinks and pulls metal</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Hot-spot relief</td><td>Add lightening pocket / relief</td><td>Leave solid mass</td><td>Relief reduces feeding distance and shrinkage cavity probability</td></tr>
