<table>
  <caption>Core Competencies for Science & Technology Evaluators (Independence, Objectivity, Judgment, and Professional Conduct)</caption>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Competency Item</th><th>Definition / Standard</th><th>Do / Don’t Guidance</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>C1: Independence Safeguarding</td><td>Maintains autonomy of analysis and conclusions; recognizes threats to independence (financial, relational, institutional) and applies mitigation or recusal.</td><td>Do: disclose ties early and document mitigation. Don’t: accept direction on conclusions.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>C2: Objectivity & Evidence Primacy</td><td>Bases findings on verifiable evidence and reproducible reasoning; avoids pre-committing to outcomes or selecting evidence to fit a narrative.</td><td>Do: use evidence matrices. Don’t: “cherry-pick” indicators.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>C3: Fair Treatment & Procedural Fairness</td><td>Applies consistent criteria and methods across evaluated entities; ensures comparable opportunity to provide information; prevents discriminatory or arbitrary treatment.</td><td>Do: apply the same rubric and thresholds. Don’t: change criteria midstream for one party.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>C4: Professional Skepticism</td><td>Questions claims proportionally to risk and impact; verifies critical information; detects inconsistencies without assuming bad faith.</td><td>Do: verify high-stakes inputs. Don’t: accept unverifiable claims as facts.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>C5: Systems Thinking</td><td>Understands the evaluated S&T activity as part of a broader system (inputs, processes, outputs, outcomes, externalities); anticipates unintended effects.</td><td>Do: map causal pathways. Don’t: assess outputs without considering context.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>C6: Risk-Based Prioritization</td><td>Allocates attention and scrutiny to the most decision-relevant uncertainties and risks; avoids spending effort on low-impact details.</td><td>Do: focus on materiality. Don’t: treat all issues as equally critical.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>C7: Accountability & Traceability</td><td>Keeps decisions, assumptions, and data sources traceable; enables third parties to understand how conclusions were reached.</td><td>Do: maintain version control and source logs. Don’t: make undocumented changes.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>C8: Learning Orientation</td><td>Reflects on feedback and new evidence; updates judgments when warranted; improves methods without compromising consistency.</td><td>Do: run post-evaluation reviews. Don’t: defend prior conclusions when evidence shifts.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>C9: Stress Tolerance & Pressure Resistance</td><td>Maintains quality and impartiality under time pressure, disagreement, or external criticism; avoids reactive decisions.</td><td>Do: use decision checklists. Don’t: rush conclusions to satisfy stakeholders.</td></tr>
  </tbody>
</table>
