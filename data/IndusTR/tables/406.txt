<table>
  <caption>Cumulative Decline Rate of Fine Particulate Matter (PM2.5) Concentration in Prefecture-Level and Above Cities Indicator</caption>
  <tr><td>Indicator Name (Unit)</td><td>Cumulative decline rate of fine particulate matter (PM2.s) concentration in prefecture-level and above cities (%)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Indicator Code</td><td>C15</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Basic Definition</td><td>The proportion of the difference between the annual average concentration of fine particulate matter (PM2.s) in prefecture-level and above cities and the annual average concentration of fine particulate matter (PM2.5) in prefecture-level and above cities in the base year to the annual average concentration of fine particulate matter (PM2.5) in prefecture-level and above cities in the base year</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Significance for Evaluating the Progress of Beautiful China Construction</td><td>The ecological environment is an important component of Beautiful China construction. The cumulative decline in fine particulate matter (PM2.5) concentration directly reflects the effects achieved by various ecological and environmental protection policies and measures. It is a core indicator for assessing the effectiveness of air pollution prevention and control, and is of great significance for the evaluation of Beautiful China construction</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Formula</td><td>C15 = (C140 - C14t）/C140 where C15 is the cumulative decline rate of annual average fine particulate matter (PM2.5) concentration within the administrative region; C140 is the annual average concentration in the base year within the administrative region; C14i is the annual average concentration in the calculation year within the administrative region</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Source</td><td>Ministry of Ecology and Environment (field monitoring data)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Period</td><td>Current-status data are the 10 years before the evaluation year; target data are the 10 years after the evaluation year; annual</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Spatial Scale</td><td>Nationwide; all prefecture-level administrative units; provinces (autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the central government)</td></tr>
</table>
