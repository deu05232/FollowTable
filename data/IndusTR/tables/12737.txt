    <tr><td>Speed control</td><td>Controls speed primarily through turn shape and absorption; no prolonged braking slides that stop flow.</td></tr>

    <tr><td>Absorption/extension (legs)</td><td>Visible flexion to absorb the front of bumps and extension into the trough; torso does not bounce up and down.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Pressure management</td><td>Pressure peaks are managed in the trough; avoids overloading the tips on bump faces causing deflection.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Turn initiation</td><td>Initiates turns early on the bump/crest area with both skis; avoids late pivoting after entering the trough.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Upper body discipline</td><td>Upper body remains quiet and oriented generally down the fall line; hands stay forward without excessive arm swing.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Pole plant</td><td>Consistent pole touches timed to support each turn; pole is placed slightly forward and downhill, not behind the boots.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Recovery / stability</td><td>Maintains balance without falls; any mistakes are corrected within 1–2 bumps while keeping forward movement.</td></tr>
