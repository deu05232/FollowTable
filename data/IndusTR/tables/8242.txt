<table>
  <caption>Sensory indicators and evaluation methods for cooked meat products (indicator-focused)</caption>
  <tr><th>Indicator</th><th>What to assess</th><th>How to evaluate</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Appearance</td><td>Surface color uniformity, visible fat distribution, presence of discoloration</td><td>Inspect under natural daylight; rotate the sample to check uniformity on all sides</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Aroma</td><td>Typical cooked-meat aroma, presence of sour or rancid notes before and after heating</td><td>Warm the sample slightly; smell at close distance with repeated short inhalations</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Texture (bite)</td><td>Firmness, springiness, perceived juiciness during chewing</td><td>Take a small bite, chew 3–5 seconds, then spit; note resistance and moisture release</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Aftertaste</td><td>Lingering meaty flavor vs bitter or metallic notes</td><td>Wait 10–15 seconds after chewing; record dominant aftertaste sensations</td></tr>
</table>
