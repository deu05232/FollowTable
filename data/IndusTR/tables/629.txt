  <tr><td>Indicator Name (Unit)</td><td>Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) concentration in prefecture-level and above cities (micrograms per cubic meter)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Indicator Code</td><td>C14</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Basic Definition</td><td>Annual average concentration of particulate matter in ambient air with an aerodynamic equivalent diameter ≤2.5 μm (the arithmetic mean of daily average concentrations within a calendar year)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Significance for Evaluating the Progress of Beautiful China Construction</td><td>The 19th National Congress of the CPC proposed making the battle against pollution one of the three critical battles to secure a decisive victory in building a moderately prosperous society in all respects. It called for prioritizing the resolution of prominent ecological and environmental problems as a key livelihood area, continuously implementing air pollution prevention and control actions, and resolutely winning the battle for blue skies; taking a marked improvement in air quality as a rigid requirement; basically eliminating heavy pollution weather; significantly improving atmospheric environmental quality; addressing the public’s “heart-and-lung trouble”; and enhancing people’s sense of happiness from blue skies. Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) concentration is a core monitoring indicator of the “Action Plan for Air Pollution Prevention and Control” (commonly referred to as the “Air Ten Measures”), and is of great significance for the evaluation of Beautiful China construction</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Formula</td><td>C14 = ∑PM2.5 /n where C14 is the annual average fine particulate matter (PM2.5) concentration within the administrative region; PM2.5 is the daily average fine particulate matter concentration within the administrative region; n is the number of days with valid monitoring of PM2.5 concentration in a calendar year. The daily average concentration for an administrative region can be calculated first by computing the daily average concentration at each air monitoring site, then taking the arithmetic mean across sites to obtain the regional daily average concentration, and then computing the mean PM2.5 daily concentration over the statistical period for the administrative region; summing the annual daily mean concentrations and dividing by the number of valid monitoring days yields the annual average concentration data</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Source</td><td>Ecology and environment authorities (field monitoring data)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Period</td><td>Current-status data are the 10 years before the evaluation year; target data are the 10 years after the evaluation year; annual</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Spatial Scale</td><td>Nationwide; provinces (autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the central government); prefecture-level administrative units</td></tr>
