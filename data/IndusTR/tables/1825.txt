<table>
  <caption>Version Negotiation Procedure and Requirements</caption>
  <tr><td>Serial No.</td><td>Item</td><td>Requirements</td></tr>
  <tr><td>1</td><td>Name</td><td>Version negotiation</td></tr>
  <tr><td>2</td><td>Objective</td><td>The charger and the vehicle negotiate to determine the communication protocol version.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>3</td><td>Description</td><td>After confirming that the physical connection is completed (per GB/T18487.5) and the communication link is established, both parties negotiate the communication protocol version, send to the other party the highest protocol version number supported by their side (version comparison is numerical comparison), and read the other party's determination result. Version negotiation shall meet the following requirements. If the other party sends negotiation successful and the negotiated version is consistent, or the other party sends negotiation failed, the negotiation ends. If the other party sends continue negotiation, determine whether the received version is supported; if supported, send negotiation successful and modify the protocol version to the negotiated version. If the version is not supported and is lower than the version sent by the other party, keep the current version and "continue negotiation". If the version is not supported and is higher than the other party's version but there exists a version lower than the version sent by the other party, adjust the protocol version according to the "expected version number" to point to the highest version below the other party's expected version, send the protocol version and "continue negotiation". If the received version is already below the minimum version, send "negotiation failed". After version negotiation succeeds, the vehicle continues sending "negotiation successful" until the charger sends the next-stage message. The charger enters the function negotiation stage after receiving the vehicle's "negotiation successful" and the version is not lower than V2.0.0. If any of the following conditions is met, both parties enter the communication procedure in Appendix M (no need to send the stage confirmation message): negotiation failed; more than 10 s has elapsed since this side first sent the version negotiation message; the vehicle receives a CHM/CRM message; version negotiation succeeds and the successful negotiated version is lower than V2.0.0. After entering the communication procedure in Appendix M, the charger shall comply with GB/T18487.5, and the vehicle shall wait for the charger to close the auxiliary power supply and send CHM/CRM messages.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>4</td><td>Preconditions</td><td>Start simultaneously after confirming that the physical connection is completed and the communication link is established. After auxiliary power supply or message wake-up of the vehicle, the charger shall re-initiate version negotiation (applicable only to automatic reconnection or charging restart).</td></tr>
  <tr><td>5</td><td>Protocol Version SAC</td><td>The charger and the vehicle should support multiple versions of the communication protocol. When negotiation succeeds, both parties support the same protocol version; otherwise, negotiation fails. The communication protocol version number consists of CAN type, major version, minor version, and provisional version, and shall meet the following requirements: the current CAN type is CAN2.0B, while applications for CANFD and CANXL are reserved; the major version is updated when the communication protocol has structural changes (e.g., changes in functional modules); the minor version is updated when the communication protocol has major functional changes; the provisional version is only for temporary purposes such as internal enterprise demonstrations and tests, and the provisional version number is 0 in officially released versions.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>6</td><td>End Conditions</td><td>Conditions for successful negotiation include: version negotiation succeeds and the version is lower than V2.0.0 (e.g., V1.1.0); the charger provides auxiliary power supply in due course as needed, and both parties exchange information according to Appendix M; version negotiation succeeds and the version is not lower than V2.0.0; both parties send "negotiation successful", and both parties exchange information according to the mutually agreed protocol version. Conditions for negotiation failure include: version negotiation fails; the charger or vehicle sends "negotiation failed", and both parties communicate according to Appendix M; version negotiation times out; the charger or vehicle sends "negotiation failed", and both parties communicate according to Appendix M. Exit conditions include: during version negotiation, the charger may open switch S1, both parties stop communication, and exit the charging process; during version negotiation, the vehicle may open switch S2, both parties stop communication, and exit the charging process.</td></tr>
</table>
