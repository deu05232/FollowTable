    <tr><td>Dissolving pulp preparation and slurry make-up</td><td>Dissolving pulp is opened, metered and slurried; inputs include dissolving pulp sheets, process water, and small amounts of wet-strength/breaking aids (as used); outputs include wetted pulp slurry to dissolution, dust captured in bag filters, and rejected pulp contaminants collected as solid waste.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>NMMO dissolution (dope preparation)</td><td>Cellulose is dissolved in N-methylmorpholine N-oxide (NMMO) under controlled moisture; inputs include pulp slurry, NMMO solution, stabilizers (to prevent solvent degradation), heat for dissolution, and vacuum for deaeration; outputs include homogeneous spinning dope, off-spec gel lumps removed by screening, and solvent-rich condensate returned to solvent conditioning.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Dope filtration and deaeration</td><td>The dope is filtered and degassed to protect spinnerets; inputs include spinning dope, filter candles/screen packs, and vacuum services; outputs include filtered dope to spinning, spent filters containing cellulose/NMMO residues, and vent streams directed to condensation to recover NMMO and water.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Dry-jet wet spinning and air gap forming</td><td>Dope is extruded through spinnerets across an air gap into coagulation; inputs include filtered dope, spinneret packs, controlled air flow, and spin-finish/antistatic agents (if applied); outputs include freshly formed filaments, polymer/gel deposits on packs requiring cleaning, and occasional off-grade threadline waste collected as solid waste.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Coagulation and primary washing</td><td>Filaments enter an aqueous bath to regenerate cellulose and extract solvent; inputs include coagulation bath water and make-up chemicals for bath control; outputs include regenerated cellulose tow, NMMO-containing wash liquor sent to solvent recovery, and wastewater purge containing dissolved organics routed to treatment.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Solvent recovery and reconcentration (NMMO loop)</td><td>Wash liquors are concentrated to recycle solvent; inputs include NMMO-containing liquor, steam/heat for evaporation, cooling water for condensation, and polishing chemicals/adsorbents (as used); outputs include reconcentrated NMMO solution returned to dope prep, recovered water reused or discharged after treatment, and spent adsorbents or still bottoms requiring disposal.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Bleaching / finishing and secondary washing (optional)</td><td>Fiber is optionally bleached and finished; inputs include hydrogen peroxide or other bleaching agents, chelants, finishing oils/softeners, and rinse water; outputs include finished tow/yarn, spent bleaching liquors and rinse waters to treatment, and empty chemical containers handled as hazardous or recyclable waste depending on residues.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Drying, crimping/cutting, and packaging</td><td>Fiber is dried and converted into staple or filament packages; inputs include wet fiber, hot air/steam for dryers, crimping aids (if used), bale wrap, and cartons/pallets; outputs include lyocell fiber product, fiber fly/dust captured in lint filters, off-cuts and off-grade fiber to rework, and packaging wastes such as trim film and broken straps.</td></tr>
