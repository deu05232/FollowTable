<table>
  <caption>Definition of inspection effectiveness grading</caption>
  <tr><td>Inspection effectiveness level</td><td>Description</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Highly effective (A)</td><td>In almost all cases, the inspection method can correctly identify the actual damage condition (confidence (80%～100%])</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Moderately highly effective (B)</td><td>In most cases, the inspection method can correctly identify the actual damage condition (confidence (60%～80%])</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Moderately effective (C)</td><td>In half of the cases, the inspection method can correctly identify the actual damage condition (confidence (40%～60%])</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Low effectiveness (D)</td><td>The inspection method can only provide limited information to correctly identify the actual damage condition (confidence (20%～40%])</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Ineffective (E)</td><td>The inspection method cannot or can hardly provide information to correctly identify the actual damage condition, and is ineffective for detecting specific damage mechanisms (confidence (0～20%])</td></tr>
</table>
