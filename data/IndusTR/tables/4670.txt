<table>
  <caption>Meaning and Application of Indicator Lamp and Pushbutton Colors</caption>
  <tr><td>Color</td><td>Meaning</td><td>Description</td><td>Application examples</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Red</td><td>Danger or alarm</td><td>Danger or immediate action required</td><td>Important equipment stops operating; temperature or pressure of water, oil, etc. reaches a critical value; important circuit loses power</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Yellow</td><td>Caution</td><td>Change in status or about to change</td><td>Temperature or pressure value is abnormal, but has not reached a critical value</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Green</td><td>Safe (normal operation or normal work)</td><td>Safe status indication</td><td>Machine operating normally; liquid circulating normally; pressure, temperature, current, etc. within specified limits</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Blue</td><td>Guidance/information (a specific meaning may be assigned as needed)</td><td>A specific meaning not covered by the above three colors red, yellow, and green may be assigned</td><td>Motor ready to start; no-load generator ready to close; heating circuit of stopped motor energized</td></tr>
  <tr><td>White</td><td>No specific meaning</td><td>Any meaning; may be used when the three colors red, yellow, and green are considered not applicable</td><td>Insulation-to-ground indication; synchronizing indicator lamp; telephone call; automatically controlled equipment</td></tr>
</table>
