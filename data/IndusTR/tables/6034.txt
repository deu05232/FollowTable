    <tr><th>Step</th><th>Purpose (Taste Only)</th><th>Practical Procedure</th><th>Assessor Judgment / Recording</th></tr>

    <tr><td>1. Serving temperature check</td><td>Reduce temperature-driven flavor bias</td><td>Serve the cooked sample warm (approx. 40–50 °C at the bite point). If hotter, wait 1–2 min before tasting; if cooler, rewarm briefly without browning.</td><td>Record sample temperature band (hot / warm / cool) and proceed only if within target.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>2. Portioning (bite size)</td><td>Standardize flavor dose</td><td>Cut to a uniform cube/slice delivering ~5–8 g edible mass per bite (remove inedible casing/skin when not intended to be eaten).</td><td>Tick portion size category (5–8 g target met: yes/no). Note any deviation.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>3. Palate preparation</td><td>Minimize carryover taste</td><td>Rinse mouth with room-temperature water; wait 30 s. If strong seasoning is expected, additionally eat 1 bite of plain cracker/bread and wait another 30 s.</td><td>Confirm palate reset completed (water only / water + cracker).</td></tr>
    <tr><td>4. First bite placement</td><td>Consistent tongue exposure</td><td>Place the entire portion on the middle of the tongue; close mouth immediately and avoid inhaling through the mouth during the first seconds.</td><td>Assess initial flavor impact (0–10 intensity) within the first 3 s.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>5. Chewing time standardization</td><td>Release flavor compounds comparably</td><td>Chew at a steady pace for 10–15 s (about 12–20 chews) without swallowing; move bolus across both sides of the tongue once during chewing.</td><td>Rate key basic tastes: saltiness, sweetness, sourness, bitterness, umami (each 0–10). Note any metallic/chemical notes.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>6. Swallow vs. expectorate rule</td><td>Control aftertaste measurement</td><td>For routine screening: expectorate after 10–15 s. For full flavor profile: swallow after 10–15 s (use the same rule for all assessors in the session).</td><td>Indicate method used (expectorate/swallow). Do not mix methods within a panel run.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>7. Aftertaste timing</td><td>Standardize lingering flavor evaluation</td><td>Start a timer at swallow/expectorate. Evaluate aftertaste at 10 s and 30 s (no food/drink during this window).</td><td>Score aftertaste intensity (0–10) and aftertaste character (pleasant / neutral / unpleasant) at both timepoints.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>8. Off-flavor check</td><td>Detect unwanted tastes</td><td>Perform a focused check for off-flavors (rancid, burnt, soapy, bitter spike, sulfurous) after 30 s, then rinse with water.</td><td>Record off-flavor presence (none / slight / moderate / strong) and descriptor(s).</td></tr>
    <tr><td>9. Inter-sample interval</td><td>Avoid taste fatigue</td><td>Wait 60–90 s between samples; rinse with water. If sample is strongly spiced, extend to 120 s.</td><td>Confirm interval met. Note fatigue if perceived (yes/no).</td></tr>
    <tr><td>10. Final flavor quality rating</td><td>Overall taste-only decision</td><td>After completing intensity and aftertaste scoring, give one overall flavor quality score based solely on taste (not aroma/texture).</td><td>Overall taste acceptability (1–9 hedonic or 0–10 quality). Provide 1–2 short flavor descriptors.</td></tr>
