  <tr><td>Enhanced thermal test</td><td>Expose the specimen to a thermal environment for 60 min; the heat flux density provided by this thermal environment shall be at least equivalent to the heat flux density received by a specimen with a surface absorption coefficient of 0.8 (or its actual value in the specified flame) when, in a completely still environment, it is exposed to a hydrocarbon fuel–air flame with a minimum average flame emissivity of 0.9 and an average temperature of at least 800℃</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Impact test</td><td>The specimen impacts the test target in an orientation causing the most severe damage to it and at a speed not less than 90 m/s</td></tr>
