    <tr><td rowspan="8">Single Skill</td><td rowspan="8">Front Snap Kick (Mae Geri)</td><td>Starting stance & guard</td><td>Stand balanced in a natural fighting stance; hands up protecting the head; eyes forward; weight distributed so the kicking leg can lift without a hop.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Knee lift (chamber)</td><td>Lift the knee straight up in front of the body to at least waist height; thigh tracks forward; hips stay level without leaning back.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Supporting foot & balance</td><td>Supporting foot stays flat or pivots slightly for alignment; heel stable; no stepping or hopping during the kick.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Hip alignment</td><td>Hips square to the target; pelvis neutral; avoid opening the hip outward or twisting the torso to “throw” the kick.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Lower-leg extension</td><td>Extend the lower leg sharply from the knee to strike; extension path is straight toward the target line; no swinging arc.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Point of force (striking surface)</td><td>Impact is delivered with the ball of the foot (toes pulled back); force is driven through the target line rather than “slapping” with the instep.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Retraction (re-chamber)</td><td>Immediately retract the kicking foot back to the chamber after the strike; knee returns to the same raised line before setting down.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Recovery & coordination</td><td>Return to the original stance under control; upper body remains upright; breathing matches the kick (exhale on strike); guard is maintained throughout.</td></tr>
