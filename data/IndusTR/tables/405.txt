<table>
  <caption>Cumulative Decline Rate of Construction Land Area Used per Unit of GDP Indicator</caption>
  <tr><td>Indicator Name (Unit)</td><td>Cumulative decline rate of construction land area used per unit of GDP (%)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Indicator Code</td><td>C49</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Basic Definition</td><td>The extent to which the construction land area used per unit of GDP in the evaluation year in a country (region) has cumulatively decreased compared with the base year</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Significance for Evaluating the Progress of Beautiful China Construction</td><td>The cumulative decline in construction land area used per unit of GDP is a major indicator reflecting the effectiveness of various land-saving policy measures in the process of economic development. When the result is positive, it indicates that the construction land area used per unit of GDP in the evaluation year has decreased compared with the base year; when the result is negative, it indicates that it has increased compared with the base year. Reducing the construction land area used per unit of GDP is one of the core goals in China’s ecological civilization assessments and Beautiful China construction, and is of great significance for the evaluation of Beautiful China</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Formula</td><td>C49 = (C480- C481)/ C480 X100% where C49 is the cumulative decline rate of construction land area used per unit of GDP; C480 is the construction land area used per unit of GDP in the base year; C481 is the construction land area used per unit of GDP in the evaluation year. For comparability, GDP should be calculated using constant prices of the base year</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Source</td><td>Natural resources authorities; statistical authorities</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Period</td><td>Current-status data are the 10 years before the evaluation year; target data are the 10 years after the evaluation year; annual</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Spatial Scale</td><td>Nationwide; provinces (autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the central government); prefecture-level administrative units</td></tr>
</table>
