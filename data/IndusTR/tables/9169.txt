    <tr><td rowspan="7">Short-Radius Parallel Turns (consistent corridor)</td><td>Ski-to-snow relationship</td><td>Skis remain parallel through the full turn; no wedge entry.</td><td>Parallel maintained in all turns; any divergence is momentary and does not create a braking wedge.</td><td>Repeated wedge entries or sustained A-frame/wedge that changes speed/control strategy.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Turn shape & rhythm</td><td>Similar turn size and timing from turn to turn.</td><td>Rhythm is steady; turn size stays within a consistent corridor without abrupt speed checks.</td><td>Irregular cadence; frequent stalling or sudden braking to regain control.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Edge change timing</td><td>Release old edges and engage new edges smoothly between turns.</td><td>Edge change happens during transition without a pause; skis flatten then re-engage cleanly.</td><td>Hesitation between turns; stems or pivots used to start most turns.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Pressure control</td><td>Progressive build of pressure to the outside ski through the shaping phase.</td><td>Outside ski clearly carries the load by mid-turn; pressure increases smoothly (no harsh slam).</td><td>Inside ski dominant; sudden pressure spikes causing skids or loss of line.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Upper/lower body separation</td><td>Lower body turns under a stable upper body.</td><td>Torso remains generally facing down the fall line with controlled counter; minimal rotation to force the turn.</td><td>Whole-body rotation to initiate turns; shoulders swing with skis.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Balance & fore/aft management</td><td>COM stays over the feet; ankles flex to keep shins engaged.</td><td>Skier stays centered; no tail washing from backseat or tip-diving from excessive forward lean.</td><td>Consistent backseat posture; repeated recoveries with arm flailing or hopping.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Pole usage (if used)</td><td>Pole touch supports timing without disrupting balance.</td><td>Pole touch occurs near initiation with the hand forward; does not cause upper-body drop or twist.</td><td>Pole plant late/behind body; planting causes shoulder drop or rotation.</td></tr>
