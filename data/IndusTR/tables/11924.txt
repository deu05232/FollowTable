    <tr><td>Color</td><td>Uniform pink/red; no gray rings or greenish tint</td><td>Natural cooked color; no excessive browning/spotting</td><td>Observe under natural light. Cut a 1 cm slice and inspect both surface and cross-section; note uniformity and edge discoloration.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Odor</td><td colspan="2">Characteristic cooked-meat aroma; absence of sour, rancid, smoky taint (if not intended)</td><td>Hold sample 2–3 cm below nostrils and take 2 short sniffs. After warming the cut piece in the hand for 10–15 s, sniff once more to detect subtle off-odors.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Juiciness</td><td>Moist bite; not dry or watery</td><td>Moist fiber; not chalky or excessively wet</td><td>Press a cut surface with a paper towel for 2 s. Note moisture transfer and whether free water pools on the plate after 1 min.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Texture / Bite</td><td>Fine, elastic gel; no graininess or crumbling</td><td>Distinct fibers; not rubbery or mushy</td><td>Take a standardized bite (one piece ~10 mm cube). Chew 5 times and assess resistance; continue to 15 chews and judge cohesiveness and breakdown.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Seasoning Balance</td><td colspan="2">Saltiness/spice level appropriate; no excessive sweetness or bitterness</td><td>Evaluate during the taste step (below). Record salt perception at ~5 s and spice after ~10 s; compare to product specification if available.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Taste (Standardized Tasting Step)</td><td colspan="2">Characteristic flavor; absence of sour, bitter, rancid, metallic, or chemical notes</td><td>Prepare a bite-size portion (2–4 g; about a 10–15 mm cube) at 20–25°C. Place in the mouth, chew slowly for 10 s (about 12–15 chews), moving the bolus across the tongue and toward the molars. Perform one gentle nasal exhale while chewing (retronasal). Spit out into a cup; do not swallow during inspection. Rinse with room-temperature water, wait 60 s, then proceed to the next sample.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Aftertaste / Off-flavor Delay</td><td colspan="2">Any late-emerging rancidity, smoke harshness, or bitterness</td><td>After spitting and rinsing, wait 30 s. Note any lingering flavor and rate intensity (0–3). If off-flavor appears late, record as “delayed”.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Mouthfeel Defects</td><td>Greasy, waxy, gritty particles</td><td>Dry, stringy, or excessive gelatin release</td><td>During chewing (first 5–10 s), focus on lubrication, particle perception, and film formation. Confirm by rubbing tongue against palate once, then spit out.</td></tr>
