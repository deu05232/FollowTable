  <caption>Sensory Inspection Checklist for Fresh (Raw) Meat Cuts (Beef/Pork/Lamb)</caption>
  <thead>
    <tr><th>Indicator</th><th>What to Check</th><th>Practical Evaluation Method (Step-by-Step)</th></tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr><td>Surface Color</td><td>Typical species color; no abnormal green/gray patches; fat color normal</td><td>Inspect under natural daylight or equivalent (5000–6500 K). View both surface and a fresh cut (make a clean slice ~1 cm deep). Compare to reference photos if available.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Color Uniformity</td><td>Evenness of color across the cut; no localized darkening</td><td>Rotate the cut 360° and observe from 30–50 cm. Note any streaks, blood pooling, or uneven pigment.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Odor (Before Handling)</td><td>Normal fresh meat odor; absence of sour, ammonia, or putrid notes</td><td>Hold the sample 2–3 cm below the nostrils and take 2–3 short sniffs. Rest 10 s and repeat once to avoid olfactory fatigue.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Odor (After Cutting)</td><td>Hidden off-odors released from the interior</td><td>Make a fresh cut; immediately smell the cut surface using the same sniffing pattern (2–3 short sniffs). Record any sour/rancid notes.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Surface Moisture/Exudate</td><td>Stickiness, excessive purge, or slimy film</td><td>Touch lightly with a gloved finger for 1–2 s. Check for tacky or slippery feel; blot with white paper towel to assess purge color and amount.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Texture / Firmness</td><td>Springiness and firmness appropriate for the cut; no mushiness</td><td>Press the surface with a fingertip to ~5 mm depth for 2 s, then release. Observe recovery time and whether an indentation remains.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Fat Appearance</td><td>Fat should not be oxidized (yellowing beyond normal), dry, or crumbly</td><td>Inspect fat under the same light; pinch a small fat edge (if present) to check brittleness. Note any waxy/oxidized look.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Taste (Cooked Sample from Raw Meat)</td><td>Characteristic cooked-meat taste; absence of sour/metallic/rancid notes</td><td>Cook a small portion (10–15 g) to an internal temperature of 72°C (plain, no seasoning). Cool for 2 min. Place ~2–3 g in the mouth, chew 10–15 times (~10 s), spread across tongue and molars. Evaluate basic taste and off-flavors, then spit out. Rinse mouth with water and wait 30–60 s before the next sample.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Aftertaste</td><td>Persistence of pleasant meat notes; no lingering bitterness or rancidity</td><td>After spitting, breathe out through the nose (retronasal) once; wait 20–30 s and note any delayed off-flavor. Record intensity (none/slight/strong).</td></tr>
  </tbody>
