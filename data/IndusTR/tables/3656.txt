  <tr><td>1×8</td><td>1—4 feet together, bend knees and pulse 2 times; 5—8 step in place left and right 8 times</td><td>1—4 hands on hips; 5—8 arms bent, swing at the sides of the body</td><td>Engage the core and keep the torso tall; steps should be springy; arm swings should be strong</td></tr>
  <tr><td>2×8</td><td>1—4 alternate three steps forward (left-right-left) and lift the right knee; 5—8 three steps backward with the right foot and lift the left knee</td><td>1- arms move through bent elbows, extend forward to a forward horizontal raise, fists clenched with knuckles facing each other; 2— lower down to the sides of the body, fists clenched; 3— through side raise to shoulder level with elbows bent, fists clenched with knuckles facing inward; 4— clap hands in front of the chest; 5—8 same as 1—4</td><td>Engage the core and keep the torso tall. When stepping forward, land with the heel first and quickly transition to the full sole; when stepping backward, do the opposite. When lifting the knee, bend the knee and lift upward parallel to the floor with pointed toes. Steps should be springy with cushioned landings. Clap with cupped palms</td></tr>
  <tr><td>3×8</td><td>1—8 start with the left foot, V-step 2 times</td><td>1—2 left and right arms, in sequence, lift elbows through bent arms to a diagonal side-up raise; 3—4 clap in front of the chest 2 times; 5—8 same as 1—4</td><td>V-step: after the feet step alternately to the diagonal front, keep the feet on the same straight line and bend the knees, then return to the original position in sequence. Steps should be springy with cushioned landings; arm pathways and positions are clear, movements are strong</td></tr>
  <tr><td>4×8</td><td>1—2 side close step to the left, torso turns slightly left; 3—4 side close step to the right, torso turns slightly right; 5—6 bend knees and pulse 2 times, torso turns left; 7—8 bend knees and pulse 2 times, torso turns right</td><td>1—2 left arm bends at the elbow and slides across in front of the face, open hand, eyes follow the hand; 3—4 same as 1—2, opposite direction; 5—8 same as 1—4</td><td>Pulsing: with elastic knee flexion, keep the knee joint above the toes as you pulse, and the center of gravity lowers accordingly. When extending the knees, drive the knee joint backward and upward to straighten both knees; coordinated use of the leg muscles produces a smooth pulsing motion. The movement rhythm matches the music; the torso turns with the arm movements, eyes follow the hand</td></tr>
