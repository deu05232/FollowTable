<table>
  <caption>CO₂ Emissions per Unit of GDP Indicator</caption>
  <tr><td>Indicator Name (Unit)</td><td>CO₂ emissions per unit of GDP (tons per 10,000 yuan)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Indicator Code</td><td>C1</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Basic Definition</td><td>CO₂ emissions generated in the production process to create one unit of gross domestic product (GDP) in a country or region during a certain period</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Significance for Evaluating the Progress of Beautiful China Construction</td><td>Both the “Opinions of the CPC Central Committee and the State Council on Thoroughly Fighting the Battle Against Pollution” and the “Outline of the 14th Five-Year Plan for National Economic and Social Development of the People’s Republic of China and the Long-Range Objectives Through 2035” clearly state: “By 2025, CO₂ emissions per unit of GDP will drop by 18% compared with 2020.” CO₂ emissions per unit of GDP are mainly used to reflect the relationship between economic growth and CO₂ emissions in a country (region). It is a core evaluation indicator that signifies the level of green and low-carbon development in a region and is of great significance for the evaluation of Beautiful China construction</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Formula</td><td>C=TP/GDP where C is CO₂ emissions per unit of GDP; TP is the total carbon emissions within the administrative region; GDP is gross domestic product calculated at comparable prices of the base year</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Source</td><td>Development and reform authorities</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Data Period</td><td>Current-status data are the 10 years before the evaluation year; target data are the 10 years after the evaluation year; annual</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Spatial Scale</td><td>Nationwide; provinces (autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the central government); prefecture-level administrative units</td></tr>
</table>
