    <tr><td>1</td><td>Roll labeling</td><td>Each roll shall have a label with product name, thickness, lot number, and date.</td><td>Label present and legible on both roll ends.</td><td>Missing label; unreadable ink; mismatched lot number.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>2</td><td>Packaging integrity</td><td>Protective wrap shall fully cover the roll.</td><td>No tears or holes in wrap upon delivery.</td><td>Wrap punctured; core exposed; water ingress signs.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>3</td><td>Core condition</td><td>Core shall support roll without collapse.</td><td>Core not crushed; roll is round.</td><td>Oval roll; cracked core; flattened areas.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>4</td><td>Handling damage</td><td>Rolls shall be lifted with suitable equipment and slings.</td><td>No fork punctures or gouges observed.</td><td>Forklift tine marks; edge tears; abrasion bands.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>5</td><td>Storage conditions</td><td>Store off the ground and protect from standing water.</td><td>No mud contamination; no pooling water around rolls.</td><td>Rolls stored in puddles; soil embedded in surface.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>6</td><td>UV exposure (pre-installation)</td><td>Cover rolls when stored outdoors for extended periods.</td><td>Covering present; no chalking observed.</td><td>Faded surface; chalk residue; brittle feel.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>7</td><td>Shipment documentation</td><td>Provide packing list and certificate of conformance.</td><td>Documents match delivered quantity and thickness.</td><td>Missing CoC; quantity mismatch; wrong thickness listed.</td></tr>
