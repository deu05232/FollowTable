  <tr><td>Basic Definition</td><td>The proportion of expenditure on research and development (R&D) and experimental development in gross domestic product (GDP) of a country (region)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Significance for Evaluating the Progress of Beautiful China Construction</td><td>Science and technology are the primary productive force and the driving force of economic growth. The share of expenditure on R&D and experimental development in GDP is an internationally common indicator for measuring the intensity of science and technology investment and the level of scientific and technological development in a country or region. Expenditure on R&D and experimental development refers to the actual spending by society as a whole in the field of science and technology on basic research, applied research, and experimental development in order to increase the total stock of knowledge. The larger the share of R&D and experimental development expenditure in GDP, the higher the intensity of R&D investment, indicating a higher level of science and technology investment. Increasing the share of expenditure on R&D and experimental development in GDP is one of the core goals for promoting innovation-driven development and supporting Beautiful China construction, and is of great significance for the evaluation of Beautiful China</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Formula</td><td>C54 = AM/BM X100% where C54 is the share of expenditure on R&D and experimental development in GDP; AM is expenditure on R&D and experimental development; BM is GDP</td></tr>
