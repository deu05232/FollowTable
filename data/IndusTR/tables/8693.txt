    <tr><td>Wedge Christie (wedge-to-parallel finish)</td><td>Progression from wedge to matching skis, edge engagement, pressure shift</td><td>Initiate each turn in a shallow wedge, then match skis to parallel by the second half of the turn; outside ski becomes the primary pressure ski through the control phase; inside ski matches without stepping or lifting; skis finish parallel across the hill with controlled speed.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Garlands (one-direction, repeated partial turns)</td><td>Release/engage edges, upper-body discipline, travel line control</td><td>Start in a traverse, steer the skis slightly into the fall line and back to the same traverse without fully crossing the fall line; keep shoulders facing generally down the hill (no big upper-body rotation); maintain even tempo and consistent return to the original travel line each repetition.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Hockey Stop (both directions)</td><td>Edging, rotary control, balance over feet, stopping distance</td><td>From a gentle glide, pivot skis to across the fall line and set edges to stop within a short, controlled distance; stop without sitting back (hips over feet); both skis engage edges together (no one-ski skid); repeat cleanly to both left and right.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Basic Parallel Turns (no pole plant focus)</td><td>Ski parallelism, turn shape, edge change timing, pressure management</td><td>Skis remain parallel throughout; link smooth S-shaped turns with a clear edge release and re-engagement each transition; outside ski carries more pressure through the shaping/control phase; maintain speed control via turn shape rather than braking wedge or excessive skidding.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Short-Radius Parallel Turns (rhythm and line)</td><td>Turn frequency, upper/lower body separation, stability, travel line</td><td>Maintain a consistent quick rhythm with a narrow corridor down the fall line; legs steer actively while torso remains quiet and facing downhill; edge change happens smoothly each turn with no hopping; pressure builds progressively on the outside ski and releases cleanly into the next turn.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Long-Radius Carved Turns (clean tracks)</td><td>Edge angles, carving vs skidding, balance, turn completion</td><td>Produce clean pencil-line tracks with minimal side-slip; progressively increase edge angle through the turn while staying balanced (no tail wash); complete each turn across the hill before releasing edges; maintain parallel skis and consistent spacing without divergence.</td></tr>
