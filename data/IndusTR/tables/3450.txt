  <tr><td>2×8</td><td>1—2 Left rear-side step-together jump; 3—4 Right rear-side step-together jump; 5—6 Feet together, knees bent in a half squat; 7—Straight split-leg jump; 8—Land with knee-bend cushioning</td><td>1—2 Left arm circles outward at the elbow to bend above the shoulder, fist with palm inward; 3—4 Right arm circles outward at the elbow to bend above the shoulder, fist with palm inward; 5—Arms raise diagonally upward to the sides; 6—7 Arms cross inward and circle through side lateral raise to side-up raise, palms together with palms facing out; 8—Arms lower to sides</td><td>Step-together jump: when stepping, bend knees to lower the center of gravity, toes land first; the other leg quickly steps up to join and jump upward forcefully; the center of gravity moves quickly accordingly. In the air, actively squeeze the inner thighs to bring legs together; rhythm is clear and landings are cushioned. For straight jump takeoff, legs together, bend knees into a half squat and push off the ground powerfully; body stays upright in the air, legs straight with pointed toes; on landing, bend knees for cushioning. Steps are accurate, clear, and elastic; arm pathways are clear and smooth, strong with large range</td></tr>
  <tr><td>3×8</td><td>1—4 Charles step starting with left foot; 5—6 Step left foot to the side into a straddle stance while bouncing knees 2 times; 7—8 Bring left leg back to feet together while bouncing knees 2 times</td><td>1—2 Arms swing at sides into left straight-arm upward punch, right fist at waist; 3—4 Arms swing at sides into left forward-downward punch, right fist at waist; 5—6 Arms side lateral raise; pump arms upward 2 times, fists with palms facing down 7—8 Arms bent horizontally in front of chest, fists with palms facing down</td><td>Counts 1—4 are “Charles step”, i.e.: step forward with the left foot, then lift the right knee forward, place the right foot down, and touch the left foot back. During the movement, pay attention to transitional landings; the upper body slightly leans back and forward with the steps. During bounces, the body coordinates and exerts force together</td></tr>

During the snap kick, the thigh initiates force first, then the lower leg snaps/extends; the knee of the working leg must extend forward-downward with control; keep the kick height as much as possible within 30°～40°. The movement should be elastic; upper body remains upright throughout. During half squat, the projection line of the knee joint is above the toes; center of gravity is between both legs</td></tr>
