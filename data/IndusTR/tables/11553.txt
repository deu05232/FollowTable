  <caption>Secondary Indicator Definition — CO₂ Emissions per Unit of GDP (C1) and Related Notes for Beautiful China Evaluation</caption>
  <tr><th>Field</th><th>Description</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Indicator Name (Unit)</td><td>CO₂ emissions per unit of GDP (tons per 10,000 yuan)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Indicator Code</td><td>C1</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Category</td><td>Green and low-carbon development</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Basic Definition</td><td>CO₂ emissions generated to create one unit of gross domestic product (GDP) in a country/region during a given period.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Interpretation</td><td>A lower value indicates better decoupling of economic growth from carbon emissions and stronger low-carbon competitiveness.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Why it matters for evaluation</td><td>It captures whether growth is achieved with fewer emissions, aligning with carbon peaking and neutrality goals and reflecting the quality of economic development.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Typical policy linkage</td><td>Energy-saving and carbon-reduction measures, industrial upgrading, and cleaner power supply that reduce carbon intensity.</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Notes on use</td><td>Best used for cross-region comparison because it normalizes emissions by economic output.</td></tr>
