    <tr><td>Float Serve (Overhand)</td><td>Firm contact, minimal spin, stable posture, targeted placement</td><td>Contacts the center of the ball to produce little/no spin; serve clears the net and shows a noticeable “float” effect while remaining controlled and playable for the server’s team (in-bounds, intentional target area).</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Serve Receive</td><td>Forearm Pass on Serve Receive</td><td>Ready position, platform angle, footwork to the ball, controlled rebound</td><td>Moves to get behind the ball and forms a stable platform; directs the first pass to the setter zone near the net (roughly between positions 2–3) at a height and speed that allows a set without a scramble.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Overhead Receive (when appropriate)</td><td>Hand shape, no double contact, controlled redirection</td><td>Uses correct hand shape and absorbs the ball cleanly (no obvious double contact/carry); redirects the serve to a target area suitable for setting rather than popping straight up or shanking wide.</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Setting</td><td>Overhead Set (Stationary)</td><td>Triangle hand shape, quick release, no lift/carry, consistent height</td><td>Sets with clean contact (no carry) and consistent arc; delivers a hittable ball to an attacker with appropriate height and timing rather than an uncontrolled push.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Set After Movement</td><td>Footwork to ball, square shoulders, balance, decision-making</td><td>Adjusts to an off-target pass, stops under control, and produces a usable set; demonstrates intention (chooses a target) and keeps the set playable even when moving.</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Attacking</td><td>Approach and Spike (Left-side / Position 4 style)</td><td>3–4 step approach, arm swing mechanics, timing, contact at peak</td><td>Uses a coordinated approach and jumps under control; contacts the ball high with full arm swing and directs it into the opponent court with intent (not a standing poke or uncontrolled contact).</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Tip/Roll Shot (Off-speed attack)</td><td>Decision to use off-speed, soft hand, placement to open space</td><td>Chooses a soft attack when a full swing is not available; places the ball to open space with control, keeping it in-bounds and making the play purposeful rather than accidental.</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Defense</td><td>Dig (Forearm Defense vs Attack)</td><td>Base posture, reaction movement, platform control, rebound angle</td><td>Maintains a low, ready posture and reacts to an attacked ball; keeps the dig playable by controlling the rebound so a teammate can make the next contact (not a wild deflection).</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Block Fundamentals (Single block)</td><td>Hand position over net, timing, penetration, landing control</td><td>Times the jump with the hitter, reaches with hands over the net plane, and lands safely under control; block contact (if made) redirects the ball down or to a playable area for teammates.</td></tr>
