    <tr><td rowspan="2">Feet-together vertical jump</td><td>Takeoff</td><td>Load through a small knee/ankle bend; push the floor away evenly through both feet; keep ribs stacked over pelvis to avoid arching.</td><td>Jumping with straight knees; pushing more through one foot; leaning back to “get height.”</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Landing</td><td>Land quietly toe-to-heel; absorb with ankles → knees → hips; maintain slight knee bend and keep knees tracking over toes.</td><td>Heel slamming; knees collapsing inward; stiff, noisy landing.</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Side-close jump</td><td>Air / Rebound</td><td>Keep springiness by minimizing hang time; bring the closing foot in quickly under the center of mass to prepare a controlled re-jump.</td><td>Closing foot late; legs drifting wide; losing rhythm due to over-jumping.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Landing</td><td>Touch down on the ball of the foot first, then let the heel lower; soften the landing with a clear knee bend before the next push-off.</td><td>Landing flat-footed; “locking” knees; bouncing with the torso instead of absorbing in the legs.</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Run-jump step (alternating hops)</td><td>Support leg</td><td>Use a small, elastic hop; keep the pelvis level; push off through the forefoot without overextending the knee.</td><td>Overstriding; pelvis dropping; pushing off with a rigid ankle.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Landing switch</td><td>Switch legs under the body; absorb impact with a quick, soft knee bend; keep the trunk stable and stacked.</td><td>Landing with the foot far ahead; torso wobble; knee snapping into extension.</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Hamstring-curl jump landing</td><td>Descent control</td><td>Prepare to land by aligning the landing foot under the hip; keep the curl leg controlled (no flailing) to stabilize landing.</td><td>Landing with the foot forward; uncontrolled lower-leg swing.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Ground contact</td><td>Toe-to-heel roll-through; absorb with ankle/knee flexion; keep shoulders quiet to show “cushioned” landings.</td><td>Heavy landing sound; shoulders bouncing; heels dropping abruptly.</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Open-stance jump (out-in or open landing)</td><td>Placement</td><td>Land with feet placed symmetrically; knees and toes point the same direction; keep center of mass between both feet.</td><td>Feet uneven; toes turned out while knees cave in; landing too wide to control.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Shock absorption</td><td>Use a deeper but controlled knee bend (not a collapse); keep hips back slightly to share load; maintain stable spine.</td><td>Dropping hips straight down; chest collapsing forward; knees driving past toes excessively.</td></tr>
