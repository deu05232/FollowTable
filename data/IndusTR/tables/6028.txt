    <tr><td>Lighting for visual checks</td><td>1) Use daylight-equivalent lighting. 2) View sample against a neutral background; rotate the piece to check all sides, then view a clean cut surface.</td><td>Color uniformity, browning, visible fat distribution.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Surface appearance</td><td>Gloss, dryness, visible defects (burnt spots, casing issues, foreign matter)</td><td>1) Inspect from top and side angles. 2) If present, peel back casing/skin carefully and re-check the exposed surface.</td><td>Presence/absence and severity of defects.</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Odor evaluation</td><td>Orthonsal aroma (before tasting)</td><td>1) Cover the cup for 5 s, then open and sniff immediately. 2) Take 2 short sniffs, pause 5 s, then re-check if necessary.</td><td>Intensity (low/medium/high) and descriptors (smoky, spicy, rancid, sulfurous, etc.).</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Off-odor confirmation</td><td>1) Warm the sample briefly in the cup by holding it in the hand for ~10–15 s (no heating devices). 2) Sniff again to see if off-notes intensify.</td><td>Whether off-odor increases with warming (yes/no) and descriptor.</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Taste and flavor</td><td>Characteristic flavor and balance</td><td>1) Take a bite-size portion. 2) Chew slowly 5–8 s to release juices. 3) Breathe out through the nose during chewing to judge retronasal aroma; then expectorate or swallow per panel rules.</td><td>Flavor intensity, main notes, balance, any atypical flavors.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Aftertaste / persistence</td><td>1) After swallowing/expectorating, wait 10–15 s without drinking. 2) Note lingering sensations; then rinse with room-temperature water and eat a plain cracker; wait ~60 s before next sample.</td><td>Duration of aftertaste, specific off-notes (bitter, metallic, rancid).</td></tr>
    <tr><td rowspan="2">Texture / mouthfeel</td><td>Firmness and springiness</td><td>1) Compress sample between molars once, then release to feel rebound. 2) Continue chewing to judge springiness and cohesiveness.</td><td>Firm/soft; springy/not springy; cohesive/crumbly.</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Juiciness and fattiness perception</td><td>1) Chew 3–5 times and pause briefly to sense saliva mixing and juice release. 2) Note lubrication and fat coating on the tongue/palate.</td><td>Juiciness level and fat mouthcoating (none/low/medium/high).</td></tr>
